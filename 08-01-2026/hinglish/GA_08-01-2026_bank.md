## TOPIC 1: RBI Report – Bank NPAs Multi‑Year Lows, Agriculture Sector me Naya Stress

### Kya hua?

- RBI ki latest **Financial Stability Report / sectoral analysis** (coverage dated **8 January 2026** ke aas‑paas) ke mutabik:  
  - **Scheduled Commercial Banks (SCBs)** ka **Gross NPA ratio ~2.1–2.2%** tak gir gaya – **multi‑year low**.  
  - **Net NPAs ~0.5%** ke aas‑paas – balance sheet repair (AQR, IBC, recap) ka cumulative result.  
- Report batati hai ki NPA reduction mainly:  
  - **Recoveries &amp; upgrades** se aaya (sirf write‑off nahi),  
  - iska matlab borrower cash‑flows genuinely improve huye.  
- Lekin composition me important change:  
  - **Agriculture sector** ab **sabse bada share** hold kar raha hai NPAs ka – uska credit share se zyada.  
  - Retail me bhi **unsecured personal loans / credit cards** ki fast growth ke saath kuch pockets of stress dikh rahe hain.  

*(Official background: RBI Financial Stability Report – December 2025; highlights summarised from 8 Jan 2026 banking coverage.)*

### Bank exam ke liye kyun important?

- Facts:  
  - “SCB GNPA ratio ~2.1–2.2%, NNPA ~0.5% (multi‑year low).”  
  - “Agriculture NPAs ka share ab sabse bada.”  
- Concept:  
  - NPA cycle corporate se **agri + unsecured retail** side move ho raha hai – PSL, KCC, small borrowers par focus.  
- Mains / interview:  
  - “Discuss how India’s credit clean‑up has shifted systemic risk from large corporates to agriculture and unsecured retail credit.”  

### Memory Trick – “NPA 2–0.5, par Khet me Dikkat”

> Yaad rakho: **“2–0.5 – banks safe, par kisan side me risk alive.”**  
> Yaani GNPA ~2%, NNPA ~0.5%, lekin agriculture NPAs highest share.

## TOPIC 2: IRDAI Investment Norms – Insurance Act Amendments se Naya Framework

### Kya hua?

- **Insurance Act ke amendments** (2025) ke baad, **Sections 27A/27B/27C/27D** ko merge karke ek unified **Section 27** bana diya gaya.  
- Ab:  
  - **Sovereign investments (G‑sec etc.)** Act me hi defined rahenge,  
  - baaki saari investment details **IRDAI regulations** ke through set hongi (zyaada flexibility).  
- Key changes (Whalesbook / biz coverage – **2 Jan 2026**):  
  - Policyholders ke funds ko support karne wale assets par **encumbrance ban** ab **repo / reverse‑repo / securities lending** par apply nahi karega → insurers ko liquidity management me relief.  
  - **Private limited companies** me investment par old blanket ban hata diya gaya – broader investment universe (safeguards ke saath).  
- Insurance sector ka AUM ~**₹74.4 lakh crore** (March 2025) – jisme bada hissa sovereign debt me invested hai.  

### Bank exam ke liye kyun important?

- Insurers bade **long‑term institutional investors** hain – G‑Secs, corporate bonds, infrastructure, housing finance companies me invest karte hain.  
- IRDAI ko zyaada flexibility →  
  - corporate bond / infra market deepen ho sakta hai,  
  - policyholders ke returns better, products bank deposits/Mutual Funds se compete kar paayenge.  
- Mains point:  
  - “Explain how Insurance Act investment amendments can support India’s bond markets and long‑term financing.”  

### Memory Trick – “Section 27 = 2–7 = ‘To Heaven’ for Flexibility”

> Socho **Purane 27A–B–C–D sab ek lift me ja kar 27 ban gaye** –  
> ab IRDAI lift se regulation floor pe aa‑ja kar rules jaldi change kar sakta hai.

## TOPIC 3: Budget 2026 – Insurance Reforms &amp; Premium Financing se Credit Boom

### Kya hua?

- **Union Budget 2026** se pehle insurance industry me expectation hai ki:  
  - **Insurance coverage + retirement security** expand karne wale reforms aayenge.  
  - **Premium financing** (loan lekar large policy premium pay karna) ko support karne ke liye credit framework smooth kiya jayega.  
- Industry commentary (7 Jan 2026, Budget 2026 preview articles):  
  - **Simplified KYC, better data sharing, clearer digital‑lending norms** → premium financing products saste &amp; fast ho sakte hain.  
  - Agar **tax deduction / incentives** premiums par bada diye gaye to log higher‑ticket policies lenge → zyada structured credit ki demand.  

### Bank exam ke liye kyun important?

- Banks / NBFCs ke liye:  
  - **Premium finance loans** = new retail product (secured by policy’s surrender value / collateral).  
  - Cross‑sell: insurance + loan + credit card / wealth – **fee + interest income**.  
- Mains / interview:  
  - “In what ways can Budget‑led insurance reforms create a credit boom? Discuss premium financing and digital lending framework.”  

### Memory Trick – “Insurance + EMI = Credit Combo”

> Yaad rakho: **Budget 2026** ka theme insurance ke liye ho sakta hai –  
> **“Policy bhi badi, EMI bhi sahi”** – dono milkar credit boom create kar sakte hain.

## TOPIC 4: India Fintech 2025 – $2.5 Billion Funding Amid Fewer Deals

### Kya hua?

- 2025 me India ka overall startup funding **$11 billion** tak slip ho gaya (YoY ~8% decline),  
  lekin **fintech sector relatively strong** raha:  
  - Lagbhag **$2.5 billion** funding ~**120 deals** me (7 Jan 2026 coverage).  
- Trends:  
  - Deals ki **count kam**, lekin **ticket size bada** (~$20.8 million median) – conviction‑based, large cheques.  
  - High focus areas:  
    - **Credit‑led consumption** (personal loans, gold loans, BNPL, credit cards),  
    - **Payment infra** (ToneTag, Juspay, Cashfree),  
    - **Wealthtech / insurtech** (Smallcase, InsuranceDekho).  

### Bank exam ke liye kyun important?

- Fintech funding pattern batata hai ki:  
  - Lenders ko **data, underwriting, compliance** me tech partnerships ki zaroorat rahegi.  
  - Card + personal loan growth → **unsecured retail risk** ka area jise RBI FSR ne highlight kiya.  
- Mains:  
  - “Discuss recent trends in fintech funding and their implications for banks’ retail credit strategy.”  

### Memory Trick – “Kam deals, bada deal”

> Line: **“Fintech me deals ghat gaye, par har deal bada ho gaya – $2.5B 120 deals me.”**  
> Matlab quantity se jyada **quality + size** par focus.

## TOPIC 5: PLI Scheme – Steel &amp; Mobile Manufacturing Boost, Credit Scope for Banks

### Kya hua?

- Govt ke **Production Linked Incentive (PLI) Scheme** (April 2020 se) ka impact latest data (8 Jan 2026 economy coverage) me highlight hua:  
  - **Crude steel output** 2020‑21 ke ~103.5 MT se badh kar **144.3 MT (2023‑24)**.  
  - Finished steel domestic consumption **136 MT+** tak pahunch gayi.  
  - **Electronics output** 2020‑21 ke ~₹5.5 lakh crore se **₹9.5 lakh crore** ke aas‑paas (2023‑24),  
    jisme **mobile phone manufacturing value ~₹4.22 lakh crore** (total electronics ka ~44.3%).  
  - Medical devices exports bhi $2.5 bn se badh kar ~$3.8 bn (2020‑21 → 2023‑24).  

### Bank exam ke liye kyun important?

- High‑capex sectors (steel, mobile manufacturing, medical devices) =  
  - **Term loans, project finance, working capital, trade finance** ki badi demand.  
- PLI success example:  
  - Mains me “Role of PLI in supporting India’s manufacturing and credit growth” type answers me direct data use ho sakta hai.  

### Memory Trick – “PLI = Produce, Loan, Invest”

> PLI ko yaad rakho: **P – Produce (factory), L – Loan (bank), I – Invest (capex &amp; exports).**  
> Scheme ka structure manufacturing ko push karta, banks ko credit opportunity deta.
