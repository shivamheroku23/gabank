## TOPIC 1: RBI December 2025 Monetary Policy – 25 bps repo cut

### Kya hua?

- RBI’s Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) meeting (3–5 December 2025) decided to **cut the repo rate by 25 bps to 5.25%** and keep the stance **neutral**.
- The **Standing Deposit Facility (SDF)** was placed at **5.00%**, while the **MSF and bank rate** were set at **5.50%**.
- RBI also announced **Open Market Operation (OMO) purchases of around ₹1 lakh crore** of G-Secs in tranches to manage durable liquidity.
- Additionally, RBI planned a **USD 5 billion dollar–rupee buy/sell swap** to support FX liquidity and the rupee.
- CPI inflation had fallen sharply, even below the 2% lower tolerance band, while GDP growth forecast was retained near **7.3%** for the year.
- (Official source: RBI Monetary Policy Statement – December 2025)

### Bank exam ke liye kyun important?

- Repo, SDF, MSF/bank rate and stance are **direct factual questions** in prelims and mains.
- Shows RBI is comfortable easing rates due to **very low inflation** while growth remains strong.
- OMOs plus FX swaps highlight how RBI manages **liquidity, yields and rupee stability** together.
- Impacts **loan rates, deposit rates, bond yields and banking margins**, core for banking awareness.

### Memory Trick – “5–5–5 ka combo”

> Repo 5.25, SDF 5.00, MSF/Bank Rate 5.50 – teenon ko yaad rakho as **“5–5–5 ka repo combo”** with neutral stance.

## TOPIC 2: Insurance Laws (Amendment) Bill, 2025 – deeper reform

### Kya hua?

- The Government introduced the **Insurance Laws (Amendment) Bill, 2025** in Parliament to further reform the insurance sector.
- Provisions include **more flexible capital norms**, scope for **composite licences**, and easing entry for specialised insurers.
- Aim is to attract more **FDI, domestic capital and new players** into life, general, health, micro and niche insurance.
- The bill fits into the broader financial-sector reform agenda and complements earlier amendments in 2021–22.
- Regulators and the Finance Ministry emphasised customer protection, solvency, and better penetration in rural and small towns.

### Bank exam ke liye kyun important?

- Banking and insurance exams often ask about **new financial sector bills and key features**.
- Composite licences and relaxed capital norms link to **financial deepening and inclusion**.
- Impacts bancassurance, cross-selling and product design for **banks tied up with insurance companies**.

### Memory Trick – “INSURE = INvite SURe investors”

> Think **“Insurance Amendment = INvite SURe investors”** – the bill’s aim is to invite more investors and players into insurance.

## TOPIC 3: India–Oman Free Trade Agreement (FTA)

### Kya hua?

- The Government approved an **India–Oman Free Trade Agreement (FTA)** focusing on trade in **energy, petrochemicals, textiles, food products and services**.
- The FTA seeks to reduce or eliminate **customs duties on a large number of tariff lines** between the two countries.
- Oman is a key partner for **crude oil, LNG, fertilisers and remittances from Indian workers** in the Gulf.
- The deal is expected to boost bilateral trade, investment and supply-chain linkages in the Indian Ocean region.
- It complements India’s wider FTA strategy with partners like UAE, Australia and the UK.

### Bank exam ke liye kyun important?

- FTAs affect **export–import flows, BoP, forex earnings** and hence banking and trade finance.
- Oman is important for **energy security and NRI remittances**, both key for external sector questions.
- Questions can focus on **country pairing, sectors covered, and strategic location (Gulf/Indian Ocean)**.

### Memory Trick – “OMAN = OIL + MARINE”

> Remember **India–OMAN FTA** as **“OIL + MARINE”** – energy (oil, gas) plus marine trade route partner in the Gulf.

## TOPIC 4: National Frequency Allocation Plan (NFAP) 2025

### Kya hua?

- The Department of Telecommunications (DoT) released the **National Frequency Allocation Plan (NFAP) 2025**.
- NFAP is the **national roadmap for allocating radio spectrum** to different services – telecom, broadcasting, defence, satellite, navigation etc.
- The 2025 version rationalises bands to support **5G/6G, satellite broadband, critical communications and defence needs**.
- It aims to reduce interference, optimise spectrum use and align with international ITU regulations.
- Effective from the end of December 2025, NFAP 2025 will guide future auctions and administrative spectrum assignments.
- (Official reference: DoT / National Centre for Communication Security)

### Bank exam ke liye kyun important?

- Spectrum policy directly impacts **telecom companies’ capex, ARPU and debt**, which banks finance.
- NFAP 2025 is a likely question in **digital economy, payments and telecom–banking convergence**.
- Also connects to **UPI, digital payments, fintech and data-centre growth**, all dependent on reliable networks.

### Memory Trick – “NFAP = Network Frequency Allocation Plan”

> Just expand NFAP in your head as **“Network Frequency Allocation Plan 2025”** – spectrum roadmap for all networks.

## TOPIC 5: Jal Jeevan Mission – Sujal Gaon ID linkage

### Kya hua?

- Under **Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM)**, the Government linked central funds to mandatory onboarding of **“Sujal Gaon ID”** for each village.
- Sujal Gaon ID works as a **unique identifier for villages** to track tap-water supply coverage, quality and functionality.
- States must ensure that villages are properly onboarded on the JJM system to receive full fund releases.
- This improves **monitoring, accountability and data-based decision making** in rural water supply.
- It is part of efforts to complete **Har Ghar Jal** targets with quality and sustainability.

### Bank exam ke liye kyun important?

- JJM is a flagship scheme – often asked in **priority sector, rural development, financial inclusion** context.
- Better water infrastructure improves **health, productivity and creditworthiness** of rural households.
- Banks may get opportunities in **project finance, CSR and livelihood-linked lending** around water and sanitation.

### Memory Trick – “SUJAL = Smart Unique JAL ID”

> Think **“SUJAL = Smart Unique JAL ID”** – a unique ID to keep every village’s drinking water data clean and clear.
