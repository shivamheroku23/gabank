## TOPIC 3: K-4 Missile Test from INS Arighaat (Sea-based Nuclear)

- India ne **K-4 medium-range ballistic missile** ka successful test fire kiya.  
- Launch platform: **INS Arighaat** – nuclear-powered submarine (SSBN).  
- Range: Around **3,500 km** class.  
- Developed by: **DRDO.**  
- Solid-fuel missile with advanced navigation.  
- Purpose:
  - **Sea-based nuclear deterrence** strengthen.  
  - India ki **nuclear triad** (Land, Air, Sea) ko strong banaana.  
  - **Second-strike capability** improve.

**Exam point:**

- K-4 kis type ki missile hai? → **Submarine-launched ballistic missile (SLBM).**  
- Range ~3,500 km.  
- Kis se launch hua? → **INS Arighaat.**  
- Kis ne develop kiya? → **DRDO.**

**Memory Trick:**

- “K-4 = 3.5k km door se hit” – 4 naam, 3.5 range.  
- Ya line: **“K-4 – Submarine se chalta, 3.5k km tak phir bhi dikhta.”**

## TOPIC 14: Defence Contracts & DRDO Pinaka LRGR

### Defence Contracts (MoD)

- MoD ne **₹4,666 crore** ke capital contracts sign kiye:
  - ~**4.25 lakh CQB carbines** (₹2,770 cr) – Bharat Forge Ltd & PLR Systems Pvt Ltd, Army & Navy ke liye.  
  - **48 Heavy Weight Torpedoes** (~₹1,896 cr) – WASS Submarine Systems S.R.L., Italy – **Kalvari‑class submarines (Project‑75)** ke liye.  
  - Torpedo delivery: **Apr 2028** se start, **early 2030** tak complete.
- **Project‑75:**
  - 6 Kalvari‑class diesel‑electric attack submarines (Scorpene design).

### Pinaka LRGR 120

- **Pinaka Long Range Guided Rocket (LRGR 120)** ka maiden flight test Chandipur ITR me successful.  
- Range: **120 km.**  
- Designed by:
  - **Armament Research & Development Establishment (ARDE)**.  
  - **High Energy Materials Research Laboratory (HEMRL)**.  
  - Support: **DRDL & RCI** (all DRDO labs).  
- Pinaka system:
  - Named after **Lord Shiva’s bow (Pinaka).**  
  - Kargil war me high-altitude positions neutralise karne me famous.

**SSC exam point:**

- CQB carbine, heavy torpedoes, Project‑75 basics.  
- Pinaka LRGR: **range 120 km, DRDO product.**

**Memory Trick:**

- 4,666 crore: “4 guns, 6 torpedoes, 6–6 numbers” (rhythm).
