## TOPIC 1: Decade of Startup India – National Startup Day 2026

### What happened?

- **16 January** is observed as **National Startup Day**, marking the launch of **Startup India on 16 January 2016**.  
- On 16 January 2026, the Prime Minister is scheduled to attend a programme at **Bharat Mandapam, New Delhi** to celebrate **10 years of Startup India**.  
- He will **interact with startup founders, incubators and investors**, and address the ecosystem on the next phase of reforms.  
- Over the decade, **more than 2,00,000 startups** have been recognised across India under the Startup India framework (DPIIT‑recognised).  
- The initiative aims to make India a **“nation of job creators, not job seekers”**, by improving **capital access, incubation, mentorship and regulatory support**.

(Official source: PIB – https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2214764, Photo: https://static.pib.gov.in/WriteReadData/specificdocs/photo/2021/aug/ph202183101.png)

### Why is this important for bank exams?

- Startup India is central to **credit demand, venture capital flows, fintech growth and SME banking**, all key topics for banking awareness.  
- Questions can test the **launch year (2016)**, **date (16 January)**, or **approximate number of recognised startups (2 lakh+)**.  
- In mains/interviews, this is a strong example of how **policy, digital infrastructure and finance** combine to drive entrepreneurship.

### Memory Trick – “16–16 for startups”

> Remember: **Startup India launched on 16‑01‑2016; National Startup Day is 16‑01 every year.** Think of “16–16 = Startup India”.

## TOPIC 2: National Campaign on Entrepreneurship – Boost to Women‑Led Enterprises

### What happened?

- The **Ministry of Rural Development** launched a **National Campaign on Entrepreneurship** to strengthen **women‑led enterprises**.  
- Under **Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana – National Rural Livelihoods Mission (DAY–NRLM)**, the campaign targets:  
  - Training **50,000 Community Resource Persons (CRPs)** in enterprise promotion.  
  - Providing **Entrepreneurship Development Programme (EDP)** training to about **50 lakh women Self Help Group (SHG) members**.  
- The focus is on **non‑farm rural enterprises** – shops, services, food processing, small manufacturing, etc.  
- The campaign builds on models like the **Start‑up Village Entrepreneurship Programme (SVEP)**, which has already helped SHG women run small businesses.  
- The government’s broader goal is to create **3 crore “Lakhpati Didis”** – SHG women earning at least ₹1 lakh per year.

(Official context: Rural Development Ministry / DAY–NRLM campaign on entrepreneurship; Photo: https://d23flp6yshmrmn.cloudfront.net/static/images/ebimg/entrepreneurship_shg_women_2026.jpg)

### Why is this important for bank exams?

- SHG and women‑enterprise expansion increases demand for:  
  - **Micro‑credit, working‑capital loans, overdrafts and digital payments** in rural areas.  
- MCQs can ask: **Which mission? (DAY–NRLM)**, **CRP target (50,000)** or **EDP target (50 lakh women)**.  
- In mains, this is a good case study on **financial inclusion, women entrepreneurship and rural non‑farm growth**.

### Memory Trick – “50k CRP, 50 lakh didi”

> Code it as: **“50k trainers, 50 lakh learners”** – 50,000 CRPs train 50 lakh SHG women entrepreneurs.

## TOPIC 4: Global Risks Report 2026 – WEF Flags Geoeconomic Confrontation

### What happened?

- The **World Economic Forum (WEF)** released the **Global Risks Report 2026** ahead of its annual meeting.  
- The report ranks **“geoeconomic confrontation”** as the **top global risk** in the near term (up to 2028).  
- Geoeconomic confrontation means using **trade, technology, sanctions, investment controls and supply‑chain restrictions** as tools of geopolitical rivalry.  
- Other high‑ranked short‑term risks include **state‑based armed conflict, misinformation/disinformation, societal polarisation and extreme weather events**.  
- For India, key highlighted risks are **cyber insecurity, inequality and weak public services**, which can affect growth and stability.

(Official context: WEF – Global Risks Report 2026; Photo: https://www3.weforum.org/docs/WEF_Global_Risks_Report_2026_Cover.jpg)

### Why is this important for bank exams?

- Exam questions often ask **which organisation releases the Global Risks Report → WEF**.  
- For banking/economy, geoeconomic confrontation directly affects **capital flows, exchange rates, trade finance, sanctions compliance and market volatility**.  
- In mains, you can use this as a reference when discussing **global headwinds for India’s banking and external sector**.

### Memory Trick – “Geo‑economics at No. 1”

> Line: **“In 2026, WEF says – geoeconomic confrontation tops the risk list.”** Connect **WEF + Global Risks + geoeconomics**.

## TOPIC 5: Namami Gange – Aquatic Life Monitoring Centre &amp; Dolphin Rescue Ambulance

### What happened?

- **Union Jal Shakti Minister C.R. Paatil** inaugurated major **aquatic biodiversity conservation initiatives** under the **Namami Gange Mission** at the **Wildlife Institute of India (WII), Dehradun**.  
- A dedicated **“Aqua Life Conservation Monitoring Centre for Ganga and Other Rivers”** has been set up to monitor freshwater biodiversity.  
- The centre hosts **ecotoxicology, aquatic ecology, spatial ecology and microplastics labs** to identify hotspots and guide policy.  
- A specialised **Dolphin Rescue Ambulance** was launched to provide **rapid, scientific rescue support to Ganga dolphins**, India’s **national aquatic animal**.  
- The event also saw launch of the **Indian Skimmer Conservation Project (BNHS)**, a **gharial conservation action plan** and a publication titled **“Millets for Life”** linking biodiversity with nutrition.

(Official source: PIB – https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2214531, Photo: https://static.pib.gov.in/WriteReadData/userfiles/image/image003SALC.jpg)

### Why is this important for bank exams?

- Environment‑linked reforms under **Namami Gange** matter for **green finance, ESG lending, and bonds tied to river‑rejuvenation projects**.  
- Questions can ask: **Which mission? (Namami Gange), which institute? (WII Dehradun), what species? (Ganga river dolphin).**  
- In mains, this fits into **sustainable development, climate risk and green‑banking discussions**.

### Memory Trick – “Dolphin ambulance at Dehradun”

> Visualise **a rescue van with a dolphin logo parked at WII Dehradun**, labelled “Namami Gange Aquatic Life Centre”.
