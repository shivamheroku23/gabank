## TOPIC 1: RBI – Microfinance Sector Needs Close Monitoring

### What happened?

- RBI’s **Trend and Progress of Banking** analysis flagged that microfinance disbursements slowed in some **southern states (esp. Karnataka, Tamil Nadu)** due to regulatory caps and industry guardrails.  
- Self‑regulatory organisations (SROs) like **MFIN** and **Sa‑Dhan** have tightened norms to ensure **responsible lending and borrower protection**.  
- As of March 2025, almost all categories of lenders other than some NBFCs saw **contraction or slower growth in microfinance credit**.  
- RBI advised lenders to **closely monitor stress build‑up** in their microfinance portfolios.

### Why is it important for Bank exams?

- Microfinance is key for **financial inclusion, SHGs, JLGs, small borrowers** – often appears in Bank mains.  
- Shows how **rapid growth can lead to over‑indebtedness** if not checked, as seen in past MFI crises.  
- Helpful in interview answers on **risk management, responsible credit and SRO role**.

### Memory Trick – “Micro = Monitor”

> Just link: **“Microfinance = Monitor closely”** –  
> RBI wants growth, but **without over‑indebtedness**.

## TOPIC 2: ATMs Down, Digital & White‑Label ATMs Up, Branches Spreading Out

### What happened?

- Total **ATMs in India fell** from about **2.53 lakh to 2.51 lakh** by March 2025 – mainly due to closure of less‑used **off‑site ATMs** by banks.  
- **White‑Label ATMs (WLAs)** run by non‑banks rose from about **34,600 to 36,200+**, especially in semi‑urban/rural pockets.  
- At the same time, the number of **bank branches** increased by roughly **2.8% to 1.64 lakh**, with PSBs opening most new branches in **rural and semi‑urban areas**.  
- Digital payments continued to grow strongly, reducing the relative need for cash withdrawals.

### Why is it important for Bank exams?

- Explains why fewer ATMs does **not** automatically mean lower access – because of **WLAs + branches + digital**.  
- Good case study for mains: “How has **digital** changed the form, not the need, of physical infrastructure?”  
- Useful for interview questions on **banking outreach strategy**.

### Memory Trick – “ATMs thode kam, access zyada”

> Think: **ATMs slightly down, but branches + WLAs + UPI up** –  
> **form बदला है, पहुँच नहीं**.

## TOPIC 3: India Adds 5 Unicorns in 2025 – Role of Fintech & Digital Credit

### What happened?

- **Tracxn India Tech Annual Funding Report 2025** showed that **India added 5 new unicorns** in 2025 while **111 unicorns** were created worldwide.  
- New Indian unicorns include: **Navi** (financial services), **Juspay** (digital payments), **JSW One MSME** (B2B steel), **Jumbotail** (B2B grocery) and **Raise Financial Services** (brokerage/wealth).  
- On average, Indian start‑ups took about **5.8 years** from **Series A** round to attaining **unicorn status**, raising about **USD 149 million** before hitting USD 1 billion valuation.

### Why is it important for Bank exams?

- Shows India’s role as the **3rd largest unicorn ecosystem** after US and China.  
- Many new unicorns are **fintech/financial services**, directly linked to **payments, lending, wealth management**.  
- Good talking point for mains on **credit to start‑ups, venture capital, regulation of fintechs**.

### Memory Trick – “5 New U for 2025”

> 2025 me India ne **5 new Unicorns (5U)** add kiye –  
> isi se yaad rakho: **“5U in 2025”**.
