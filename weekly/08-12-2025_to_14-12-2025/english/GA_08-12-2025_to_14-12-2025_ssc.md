## TOPIC 2: First PPP‑Model Medical Colleges – Madhya Pradesh

### Kya hua?

- Government approved **India’s first Public–Private Partnership (PPP) model medical colleges** in **Madhya Pradesh**.  
- Foundation stone laid for colleges in **Dhar** and **Betul**, both **tribal‑dominated districts**.  
- Under PPP: state provides **land + district hospital**, private partner builds **college, hostel, labs**, etc.  
- District hospitals remain under **state control**, but are used as **teaching hospitals**.

### SSC exam points:

- First PPP‑model medical colleges: **Madhya Pradesh**, districts **Dhar** and **Betul**.  
- Objective: **reduce regional imbalance** in medical education in **tribal/backward areas**.  
- Questions can link to **health infrastructure, PPP concept, NMC norms**.

**Memory Trick – “Doctor from Dhar & Betul”**

> PPP medical colleges – imagine **“Dr. Dhar” and “Dr. Betul”** coming from tribal MP.

## TOPIC 3: Supreme Court on Passport Renewal & Personal Liberty

### Kya hua?

- In a key judgment, the **Supreme Court** held that **personal liberty is not a gift of the State but its first obligation**.  
- It ruled that **mere pendency of criminal cases does not automatically disqualify a person from holding/renewing a passport**.  
- Case: **Mahesh Agarwal vs Union of India** – petitioner sought passport renewal despite ongoing cases.  
- Court said: if **criminal court permits passport with conditions**, passport authority should **respect that order**.

### SSC exam points:

- Relates to **Article 21 – Right to life and personal liberty**.  
- Clarifies that **passport authorities** cannot override **judicial orders** and cannot impose extra conditions.  
- Very relevant for **Polity/Constitution** questions.

**Memory Trick – “Passport via Court, not by Mercy”**

> Think: **Court order > Passport office** – liberty is **constitutional right**, not state mercy.
