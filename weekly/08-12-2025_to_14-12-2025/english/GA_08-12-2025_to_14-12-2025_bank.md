## TOPIC 1: Q4 Treasury Bills – ₹3.84 Lakh Crore Short-Term Borrowing Plan

### What happened?

- The **Government of India** announced that in **Q4 of FY26** it plans to borrow about **₹3.84 lakh crore** through **short‑term Treasury Bills (T‑Bills)**.  
- Weekly T‑Bill auctions will be in the **₹29,000–35,000 crore** range, as per the Finance Ministry’s calendar.  
- This borrowing is **₹10,000 crore lower** than the ~₹3.94 lakh crore raised via T‑Bills in the same quarter of the previous year.  
- T‑Bills in India are issued for **91, 182 and 364 days**, are **zero‑coupon**, sold at a **discount**, and redeemed at **face value**.

### Why is it important for Bank exams?

- Shows how government manages **short‑term cash mismatches** – core part of **money market** questions.  
- Impacts **liquidity, short‑term yields (T‑Bill rates)** and therefore **pricing of bank CDs, CPs and savings products**.  
- Helps in mains answers on **fiscal–monetary coordination** and government borrowing mix (T‑Bills vs dated securities).

### Memory Trick – “3–8–4 for Q4”

> Q4 T‑Bill calendar ≈ **₹3.84 lakh crore** –  
> remember **“3‑8‑4 = short‑term for Q4”**.

## TOPIC 2: RBI’s Trend & Progress Report – Banks Strong and Profitable

### What happened?

- RBI released its annual **“Report on Trend and Progress of Banking in India 2024‑25”**.  
- The **consolidated balance sheet of Scheduled Commercial Banks (SCBs)** (ex‑RRBs) grew by about **11.2%** in FY25 (vs 15.5% in FY24).  
- **Gross NPA ratio** of SCBs fell to a **multi‑decadal low of 2.2%** in March 2025 and further near **2.1% by September 2025**.  
- **Capital adequacy (CRAR)** remained high at around **17.4% in March 2025**, with **RoA ~1.4%** and **RoE ~13.5%** – indicating robust profitability.  
- Public sector banks’ share in the overall SCB balance sheet decreased slightly to **about 54.9%**, as private banks and SFBs grew faster.

### Why is it important for Bank exams?

- Gives **headline numbers** for **NPA, capital, RoA/RoE** that can be asked as direct MCQs.  
- Shows that the system is in a **stronger position** than during the pre‑2018 NPA crisis – good for interview discussion.  
- Helps you frame answers on **credit growth, stability, PSB vs private bank share** and the impact of past reforms.

### Memory Trick – “2–2 NPAs, 17 CRAR, 1.4 RoA”

> Quick line: **“NPAs 2‑2, capital 17+, RoA 1.4”** –  
> three key indicators of a healthy system.

## TOPIC 3: Microfinance, ATMs and Jobs – Structural Shifts Highlighted by RBI

### What happened?

- RBI’s analysis showed **stress pockets in microfinance**: microfinance disbursements slowed in some southern states after tighter norms and self‑regulatory guardrails.  
- The **total number of ATMs** in India fell to **about 2.51 lakh** as on 31 March 2025 (from 2.53 lakh), with **off‑site ATMs closed** by many banks; but **white‑label ATMs rose** to 36k+.  
- At the same time, **bank branches grew by ~2.8% to 1.64 lakh**, with PSBs opening **two‑thirds of new branches in rural & semi‑urban areas**.  
- Bank employment data showed **Small Finance Banks (SFBs)** as major job creators, adding nearly **16,000 employees** and taking their staff strength to **1.77 lakh**.

### Why is it important for Bank exams?

- Good case study for mains: **cash vs digital**, why ATMs can shrink yet access grow via branches + digital + WLAs.  
- Microfinance section is key for **priority sector, financial inclusion and credit risk** questions.  
- Employment trends highlight the rising role of **SFBs in retail credit and inclusion** – useful for interview discussion.

### Memory Trick – “Less ATMs, More Access”

> Remember: **ATMs थोड़े कम, but branches + WLAs + digital ज्यादा** –  
> access badh raha hai, sirf रूप बदल रहा है.
