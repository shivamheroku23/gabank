## TOPIC 1: India–Fiji Extend Agriculture MoU for 5 Years

### What happened?

- In New Delhi, India’s Union Agriculture Minister **Shivraj Singh Chouhan** met Fiji’s Minister for Agriculture and Waterways **Tomasi Tunabuna** to review bilateral cooperation.  
- Both sides agreed to **extend their Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) on agriculture cooperation for another five years (2026–2031)**.  
- They also decided to establish a **Joint Working Group (JWG)** on agriculture and food security to monitor implementation and identify new areas of collaboration.  
- Key focus areas include **student and researcher exchanges, training and capacity building, small-scale farm machinery, digital agriculture tools, genetic resource exchange and reducing post-harvest losses**.

### SSC exam points:

- Sector of MoU extension → **Agriculture and food security**.  
- Period of extension → **5 years (2026–2031)**.  
- Mechanism created → **Joint Working Group (JWG)** on agriculture and food security.  
- Key themes → training, technology transfer, mechanisation and digital agriculture.

**Memory Trick – “5-year India–Fiji farm bridge”**

## TOPIC 3: National IED Data Management System (NIDMS)

### What happened?

- Union Home Minister **Amit Shah** virtually inaugurated the **National IED Data Management System (NIDMS)** at the **NSG garrison in Manesar, Gurugram**.  
- NIDMS is a **secure national-level digital platform** developed by the **National Security Guard (NSG)** for **systematic collection, storage and analysis of data on Improvised Explosive Devices (IEDs)**.  
- It standardises data on IED incidents – type of device, explosives, initiation systems, location, modus operandi and forensic findings – and uses **AI/ML tools** to identify patterns and trends.  
- The system is integrated with the **Integrated Criminal Justice System (ICJS-2)** and is accessible, with graded permissions, to agencies like the **National Investigation Agency (NIA), Anti-Terrorism Squads (ATS), state police and Central Armed Police Forces**.

### SSC exam points:

- NIDMS stands for → **National IED Data Management System**.  
- Developer/implementing agency → **NSG under the Ministry of Home Affairs**.  
- Purpose → **central repository for IED-related data + analytical tool for counter-terror and bomb-disposal operations**.  
- Linked platform → **ICJS-2** for “One data – One entry” integration.

**Memory Trick – “NIDMS = India’s bomb-data brain”**

> Think of **NIDMS as a single national ‘brain’ that stores and analyses all IED incident data.**

## TOPIC 4: Pravasi Bharatiya Divas (PBD) 2026 and 18th PBD Convention

### What happened?

- **Pravasi Bharatiya Divas (PBD)**, also known as **NRI Day**, is observed every year on **9 January** to mark the date in **1915** when **Mahatma Gandhi returned to India from South Africa**.  
- The large PBD convention is held **biennially**; the **18th PBD Convention** took place in **Bhubaneswar, Odisha, from 8–10 January 2025**.  
- The theme of the 18th PBD Convention was **“Diaspora’s Contribution to a Viksit Bharat”**, highlighting the role of overseas Indians in India’s development.  
- In 2026, while there is **no main convention** (the next one is expected in 2027), 9 January is still observed worldwide through **seminars, cultural programmes and outreach events**.

### SSC exam points:

- PBD / NRI Day date → **9 January** every year.  
- Significance → commemorates Gandhi’s **return to India (9 January 1915)**.  
- 18th PBD Convention → **Bhubaneswar, Odisha; 8–10 January 2025; theme “Diaspora’s Contribution to a Viksit Bharat.”**  
- PBD convention periodicity → **biennial**, but the day itself is an **annual observance**.

**Memory Trick – “9 January – Gandhi returns, diaspora day begins”**

> Link: **“Gandhi came back on 9 January 1915 → hence 9 January = Pravasi Bharatiya Divas.”**

## TOPIC 5: World Hindi Day 2026 – Different from Hindi Diwas

### What happened?

- **World Hindi Day (Vishwa Hindi Diwas)** is observed on **10 January** each year to mark the anniversary of the **first World Hindi Conference**, held in **Nagpur in 1975**.  
- It is distinct from **Hindi Diwas**, which India observes on **14 September**, commemorating the day in **1949** when Hindi was adopted as an official language by the Constituent Assembly.  
- On World Hindi Day 2026, Indian embassies, consulates and cultural centres organised programmes such as **poetry recitations, seminars, essay competitions and cultural evenings** to promote Hindi globally.  
- As of early 2026, there was **no widely confirmed single official global theme**; exams are therefore more likely to test **dates, origins and differences** between the two observances.

### SSC exam points:

- World Hindi Day → **10 January**; linked to **first World Hindi Conference, Nagpur (1975)**.  
- Hindi Diwas → **14 September**; marks Hindi’s adoption as an **official language** in 1949.  
- World Hindi Day is mainly celebrated by **Indian missions abroad and international institutions promoting Hindi**.

**Memory Trick – “10 January global, 14 September national”**

> Remember: **“10 Jan = World Hindi Day (global); 14 Sep = Hindi Diwas (within India).”**

## TOPIC 6: National Youth Day 2026 – “Ignite the Self, Impact the World”

### What happened?

- **National Youth Day (Yuva Diwas)** is observed every year on **12 January**, the birth anniversary of **Swami Vivekananda (born 1863)**.  
- The Government of India declared National Youth Day in **1984**, and it has been celebrated since **1985** to inspire youth with Vivekananda’s ideas of strength, character and service.  
- The theme for **National Youth Day 2026** is **“Ignite the Self, Impact the World,”** urging young people to focus on inner development and then use that strength for social transformation.  
- Across India, schools, colleges, NSS units and youth organisations marked the day with **runs, debates, cultural programmes, lectures and community service activities**.

### SSC exam points:

- National Youth Day date → **12 January**.  
- Associated personality → **Swami Vivekananda** (birth anniversary).  
- Declared by → Government of India in **1984**; first celebrated in **1985**.  
- Theme 2026 → **“Ignite the Self, Impact the World.”**

**Memory Trick – “Vivekananda: Ignite self, impact world”**

> Pair the date and theme: **“12 January – Vivekananda Jayanti – Ignite the Self, Impact the World.”**
