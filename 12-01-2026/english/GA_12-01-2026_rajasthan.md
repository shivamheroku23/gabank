## TOPIC 1: Cold Wave – School Holidays and Revised Timings Across Districts

### What happened?

- A sharp cold wave over Rajasthan in mid-January 2026 pushed several districts to announce **school holidays and delayed timings** for students.  
- In **Jaipur**, all government and private schools declared holidays for **classes 1 to 5 on 12–13 January**, effectively extending the winter break.  
- In **Sikar**, holidays for classes 1–5 were extended till **17 January 2026**, while timings for **classes 6–12** were shifted to roughly **11 a.m. to 4 p.m.**  
- Districts such as **Jhunjhunu, Jaisalmer, Nagaur, Bharatpur, Dausa and Jalore** also closed schools for junior classes (often up to class 5 or 8) and moved senior classes to late-morning slots, citing IMD cold-wave advisories.

### Rajasthan exams ke liye kyun important?

- Direct factual questions can ask **which districts** or **which classes** were given special winter breaks during the January 2026 cold wave.  
- It is a current example of **district-level disaster/seasonal risk management** and protection of children in extreme weather conditions.  
- In mains, you can quote this while writing on **climate variability, cold waves and administrative responses in Rajasthan**.

**Memory Trick – “Cold wave = late mornings and short school week”**

> Visualise empty classrooms and clocks moved forward: **severe cold = holidays for classes 1–5 and 10–11 a.m. starts for seniors.**

## TOPIC 2: Women’s Welfare – Higher Support Under PMMVY, Lado Protsahan and LPG Subsidy

### What happened?

- At a pre-budget interaction with girl students in Jaipur on **11 January 2026**, Chief Minister **Bhajanlal Sharma** highlighted a set of expanded **women-centric welfare measures** in Rajasthan.  
- Under **Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY)**, maternity assistance in the state has been increased to **₹6,500 per beneficiary**, with nearly **10 lakh women** covered.  
- Financial assistance under the **Lado Protsahan Yojana** has been raised to **₹1.5 lakh per girl**, linked to milestones in health and education; more than **10.5 lakh bicycles** and about **40,000 scooters** have also been distributed to girls.  
- Women from low-income households are being provided **LPG cylinders at ₹450**, while the state reports about **12 lakh “Lakhpati Didis”**, around **20 lakh women trained** under various programmes, and over **2,500 women** among newly recruited constables.

### Rajasthan exams ke liye kyun important?

- One-liners can target key amounts: **PMMVY assistance in Rajasthan = ₹6,500**, **Lado Protsahan benefit = ₹1.5 lakh**, LPG cylinder at **₹450**.  
- These schemes are powerful case-studies for mains answers on **women’s health, education, mobility and income empowerment** in Rajasthan.  
- They also connect to broader themes like **Lakhpati Didi, skill training and women’s participation in police and government jobs**.

**Memory Trick – “6.5k for mothers, 1.5 lakh for daughters, ₹450 for kitchens”**

> Link it as **“Mother 6.5k – Daughter 1.5 lakh – Gas 450”** to recall the three flagship numbers.

## TOPIC 3: Progress on 25 Flagship Schemes – Trees, Water, Livelihoods

### What happened?

- A recent state government briefing (late 2025) outlined progress under **25 flagship schemes**, covering women’s income, agriculture, water, housing, energy, skills and connectivity.  
- Women’s initiatives include the **Lado Incentive Scheme** and training-based models such as **Namo Drone Didi, Solar Didi, Lakhpati Didi, Bank Sakhi, Krishi Sakhi and Pashu Sakhi** to support livelihood generation.  
- Environmental and water programmes feature **Harialo Rajasthan** (target of **50 crore saplings in five years**), the **“Karmabhoomi se Matribhumi”** groundwater recharge campaign, and **Mukhyamantri Jal Swavlamban Abhiyan 2.0**, which involves new water-harvesting structures in more than **4,700 villages**.  
- Rural and urban development are supported by schemes like **Pandit Deendayal Upadhyay Garibi Mukt Gram Yojana, Atal Pragati Path, Jal Jeevan Mission, Atal Gyan Kendras**, and **Chief Minister Swanidhi** loans for unorganised workers.

### Rajasthan exams ke liye kyun important?

- Names like **Harialo Rajasthan (50 crore trees)**, **Jal Swavlamban Abhiyan 2.0 (4,700+ villages)** and **Garibi Mukt Gram Yojana** are high-probability MCQ entries.  
- In mains, you can use these schemes together to show a **convergence approach to water, livelihoods, women’s income and rural connectivity**.  
- They also demonstrate how **state schemes align with central missions** such as PM-KUSUM, PM Awas Yojana and Jal Jeevan Mission.

## TOPIC 4: Palash Barber – First Rajasthan Player in Indian Futsal Team

### What happened?

## TOPIC 6: Ind-Ra Projects India’s GDP Growth at 6.9% in FY27

### What happened?

- **India Ratings and Research (Ind-Ra)**, a Fitch Group rating agency, has projected India’s real GDP growth to be **6.9% in FY27**, compared to an estimated **7.4% in FY26**.  
- It highlights headwinds from a possible **El Niño pattern from mid-2026, weak global trade growth and investment-related uncertainties**, but still expects India to remain a **high-growth economy**.  
- Private Final Consumption Expenditure (PFCE) is projected to grow at about **7.6%** in FY27, with overall inflation expected to stay broadly manageable.

### Rajasthan exams ke liye kyun important?

- State exams often ask **“Which agency gave which growth figure?”** – here, **Ind-Ra → 6.9% (FY27)**.  
- The forecast is useful in mains answers on **India’s macro-economic outlook**, which directly affects **state finances, employment and welfare spending**.  
- It also helps differentiate projections by **NSO, RBI, IMF, World Bank and rating agencies**.

**Memory Trick – “Ind-Ra: 7.4 now, 6.9 next”**
