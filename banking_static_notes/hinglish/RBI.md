# Reserve Bank of India (RBI) – Full Static Notes

## 1. Basic Profile

- **Full Name:** Reserve Bank of India (RBI)  
- **Type:** Central Bank of India / Monetary Authority  
- **HQ:** Mumbai, Maharashtra  
- **Establishment:**  
  - RBI Act, 1934 ke under establish  
  - **Start of operations:** 1 April 1935, Kolkata  
  - **HQ shift to Mumbai:** 1937  
- **Ownership:**  
  - Initially: private shareholders ke saath  
  - **Nationalisation:** 1 January 1949 – fully owned by Government of India  
- **Official Website:** https://www.rbi.org.in  

> Exam line: *“RBI – India ka central bank + monetary authority, 1935 se kaam kar raha hai, aur 1949 se 100% Government owned hai.”*

---

## 2. History & Important Milestones (Timeline Style)

- **1926:** Hilton Young Commission ne ek central bank recommend kiya.  
- **1934:** Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 pass hua – RBI ke establishment ka main legal base.  
- **1 April 1935:**  
  - RBI ne operations start kiye (Kolkata se).  
- **1937:**  
  - RBI ka Head Office Kolkata se **Mumbai** shift hua.  
- **1 January 1949:**  
  - RBI ka **nationalisation** – ab 100% Government of India ki ownership.  
- **1949:**  
  - Banking Regulation Act, 1949 आया – commercial banks ko regulate karne ka main law.  
- **1950s–60s:**  
  - Priority sector lending, agriculture, development finance ke liye alag institutions ka birth (NABARD ka precursor, etc.).  
- **1969 & 1980:**  
  - Major public sector bank nationalisation waves – credit planning me RBI ka major role.  
- **1991 ke baad (Reforms Phase):**  
  - Liberalisation, private banks ka entry, prudential norms, CRR/SLR rationalisation, capital adequacy norms (Basel guidelines adoption), etc.  
- **2000s onwards:**  
  - Digital payments, RTGS, NEFT, later UPI, BBPS, Bharat QR, etc. ke liye RBI ne payment system regulator ka role aur strong kiya.  
- **Recent decades:**  
  - Inflation targeting framework (Monetary Policy Committee – MPC),  
  - Prompt Corrective Action (PCA) framework for weak banks,  
  - Financial Stability Report, etc.

> Timeline yaad rakhne ka trick: **“Act 34, Birth 35, Bombay 37, National 49”**

## 3. Objectives of RBI

RBI ke main objectives ko short me yaad rakh sakte ho – **“MPF – Monetary, Price, Financial”**:

1. **Monetary Stability:**  
   - Paisa (money supply, interest rates) ko aise manage karna ki growth bhi ho aur inflation bhi control me rahe.

2. **Price Stability with Growth:**  
   - Inflation bahut zyada bhi na ho, aur growth ko kill bhi na kare – balanced approach.

3. **Financial Stability:**  
   - Banking system, NBFCs, payment systems, forex market, govt debt market – sab properly function karein, systemic risk kam ho.

4. **Developmental Role:**  
   - Financial inclusion, rural credit, priority sector, new instruments (UPI, RuPay, etc.) ke through economy ka long‑term development support karna.

## 4. Main Functions of RBI (Exam Friendly Classification)

RBI ke functions ko yaad rakhne ke liye ek trick:

> **“I-B-G-F-P-D-E” – Issue, Banker, Government, Finance, Payment, Development, Exchange**

### 4.1 Issue of Currency (Note Issuing Authority)

- India ka **sole authority for bank notes** (except ₹1 note jo Ministry of Finance issue karti hai; lekin circulation/RBI ke through hi).  
- Currency ke design, security features, denominations, withdrawal/issue – sab RBI ki responsibility (RBI Act ke under).  
- “Issue Department” ke through notes issue hote hain, **backed by assets** (gold, foreign securities, Govt securities etc.).

### 4.2 Banker’s Bank & Lender of Last Resort

- Commercial banks ke liye **banker’s bank**:
  - Banks apna account RBI me rakhte hain.  
  - Clearing & settlement RBI ke through.  
- Jab koi bank liquidity crisis me ho, to RBI **lender of last resort** ka role play karta hai (certain conditions ke sath).

### 4.3 Banker, Agent & Advisor to Government

- **Central Government** aur kai cases me **State Governments** ke liye:
  - Banking transactions handle karta hai (receipts, payments, govt accounts).  
  - Govt securities issue aur manage karta hai (T‑Bills, dated securities).  
  - Borrowing programmes design aur manage karta hai.  
  - Economic & financial policy par **advisor** ki tarah suggestions deta hai.

### 4.4 Controller of Credit / Monetary Policy Authority

- **CRR (Cash Reserve Ratio)**  
- **SLR (Statutory Liquidity Ratio)**  
- **Repo / Reverse Repo rate**  
- **Bank Rate, MSF (Marginal Standing Facility)**  
- Open Market Operations (OMOs)  
In sab tools ke through RBI **credit aur liquidity** ko control karta hai.

- Ab **Monetary Policy Committee (MPC)** ke framework ke under:
  - Inflation target ke aas‑paas policy rate decide hota hai.  
  - MPC me 6 members (3 RBI + 3 Government appointees).

### 4.5 Regulator and Supervisor of Banks & NBFCs

- Scheduled Commercial Banks, RRBs, Cooperative Banks (multi‑state etc.), NBFCs ka regulation & supervision.  
- Licenses dena, branch expansion, capital norms, provisioning standards, asset classification norms – sab RBI decide karta hai (relevant Acts ke under).  
- On‑site inspection + off‑site surveillance ke through banks ka health monitor karta hai.

### 4.6 Custodian of Foreign Exchange Reserves & Forex Management

- Country ke **foreign exchange reserves** (FX reserves) manage karta hai.  
- External sector stability, rupee ke orderly movement me support, etc.  
- Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA) ke kuch provisions ke under forex market me regulation / monitoring role.

### 4.7 Regulator of Payment and Settlement Systems

- RTGS, NEFT, UPI, IMPS, BBPS, AEPS, card networks etc. ke liye regulatory framework.  
- Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007 ke under powers.  
- NPCI jaisi institutions ke through retail payment innovations ko oversee karta hai.

### 4.8 Developmental & Promotional Role

- Financial inclusion schemes, priority sector lending norms, small finance banks, payments banks, etc. ka framework.  
- Rural credit, MSME, agriculture, weaker sections ki financing ko promote karna.  
- Financial literacy campaigns, ombudsman schemes, etc.

## 5. Organisational Structure (High Level)

- **Central Board of Directors** – RBI ka apex decision making body.
  - **Official Directors:**
    - Governor (1)  
    - Deputy Governors (up to 4)  
  - **Non‑official Directors:**
    - Government ke nominees  
    - Different sectors/areas se nominated directors  
- Tenure, appointment Government of India decide karti hai (as per RBI Act).  
- RBI ke multiple **Regional Offices** (Mumbai, Delhi, Kolkata, Chennai, etc.) aur **sub‑offices** hain.

> Exam point: *“RBI ka HQ – Mumbai; Central Board of Directors – apex body; Governor = chief executive of RBI.”*

## 6. Important Legal Framework

### 6.1 Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934

- RBI ke establishment, structure, functions ka main law.  
- Currency issue, monetary policy powers, etc. yahi se aate hain.

### 6.2 Banking Regulation Act, 1949

- Commercial banks ka regulation & supervision.  
- Licensing, capital norms, management control, amalgamation, moratorium, etc. yahi se govern hote hain.

### 6.3 Other Important Laws

- **FEMA, 1999 (Foreign Exchange Management Act)** – forex market / external sector regulation.  
- **Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007** – payment systems regulation.  
- **Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation Act, 1961** – DICGC (RBI subsidiary) ke through deposit insurance.

---

## 7. Subsidiaries & Associated Institutions (Exam Angle)

- **DICGC – Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation**
  - RBI ki wholly owned subsidiary.  
  - Commercial banks, RRBs, cooperative banks ke deposits ko insurance provide karti hai (up to prescribed limit).

- **BRBNMPL – Bharatiya Reserve Bank Note Mudran Pvt Ltd**
  - Currency notes printing ke liye RBI ki subsidiary.

- **Other linked institutions (independent bodies but exam me RBI se relate karte hain):**
  - **NABARD** – agricultural & rural development finance (RBI + Govt promoted).  
  - **SIDBI** – MSME finance.  
  - **NHB** – housing finance (later ownership change – exam year ke hisaab se detail check karein).  
  - **EXIM Bank** – export‑import finance.

## 9. Important One‑Liners – RBI (Bank + SSC Exam)

1. RBI ka establishment – **1 April 1935**, **RBI Act 1934** ke under, pehle Kolkata; HQ shift – **Mumbai, 1937**.  
2. RBI ka **nationalisation – 1 January 1949**; ab 100% Government ownership.  
3. RBI – **India ka central bank + monetary authority + banking system ka regulator**.  
4. Bank notes issue authority – **RBI (issue department)**; ₹1 note – Ministry of Finance.  
5. CRR, SLR, Repo, Reverse Repo, Bank Rate, MSF, OMOs – sab RBI ke **monetary policy tools**.  
6. Government ke banker, agent, advisor – role RBI ka.  
7. RBI – **banks ka banker** + **lender of last resort**.  
8. RBI – **payment and settlement systems ka regulator** (RTGS, NEFT, UPI etc.).  
9. RBI – **forex reserves ka custodian** & external sector stability me key role.  
10. RBI ki important subsidiary – **DICGC** (deposit insurance) & **BRBNMPL** (note printing).
