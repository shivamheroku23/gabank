# SARFAESI – DRT – IBC Overview – Static Notes

Ye file **loan recovery / NPA resolution ke important laws** ko simple overview me explain karti hai:

- DRT (Debt Recovery Tribunals)  
- SARFAESI Act  
- Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC)  

---

## 1. Background – NPA Problem & Need for Special Laws

- Banks loans dete hain, kuch borrowers time par repay nahi karte → **NPAs (Non‑Performing Assets)** create hote hain.  
- Normal civil courts me recovery cases bohot slow hote the.  
- Isliye **special laws & institutions** banaye gaye:

1. Debt Recovery Tribunals (DRTs) – faster loan recovery ke liye.  
2. SARFAESI Act – secured assets ko court ke bina enforce karne ke liye powers.  
3. Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC) – time‑bound corporate insolvency resolution process (CIRP).

---

## 2. Debt Recovery Tribunals (DRTs)

### 2.1 Basic Idea

- DRTs ka objective:  
  - Banks & Financial Institutions ke **loan recovery cases ko fast‑track** karna.  
- DRTs special tribunals hain jo specified amount se upar ke debt recovery cases handle karte hain (threshold amount law/circulars me defined).

### 2.2 Legal Base (Concept Level)

- Debt recovery ke liye pehle ek Act pass hua (1990s me) jiske basis par:
  - **Debt Recovery Tribunals (DRTs)**  
  - aur **Debt Recovery Appellate Tribunals (DRATs)**  
  establish kiye gaye.  
- Baad me is Act ka naam badal kar broader **Recovery of Debts and Bankruptcy** coverage bhi include kiya gaya (amendments ke through).

(Exam year ke liye exact Act name, year & amendments ko alag note karna behtar hai.)

### 2.3 Features (High Level)

- Banks / FIs directly DRT me application file kar sakte hain.  
- DRT summary procedure follow karta hai –  
  - target – ordinary civil court se faster disposal.  
- DRT orders ke against appeal DRAT me.

> Exam angle: DRT = **special judiciary body** for bank debt recovery.

## 3. SARFAESI Act – Securitisation & Enforcement Law

### 3.1 Full Form & Purpose

- **SARFAESI:**  
  - Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act.  
- Objective:
  - Secured creditors (banks / FIs) ko **security interest enforce karne ki power** dena,  
  - taki court ke bina security enforce karke loan recovery fast ho sake.  
  - Asset Reconstruction Companies (ARCs) ke through stressed assets resolution ka framework create karna.

### 3.2 Key Concepts

1. **Security Interest:**  
   - Mortgage / charge / lien / hypothecation, etc. jisme bank ke paas asset par right hai.  

2. **Enforcement Without Court:**
   - Agar borrower default kare aur account NPA ban jaye,  
   - to SARFAESI ke under bank notice dekar **secured asset par possession / sale** kar sakta hai (conditions & procedure ke sath).

3. **Asset Reconstruction Company (ARC):**
   - Specialised company jo NPAs ko banks se purchase karke unka reconstruction / resolution karti hai.  
   - Banks apne stressed assets ko ARCs ko transfer karke balance sheet clean kar sakte hain (with regulatory norms).

### 3.3 Typical Process (Conceptual)

- Account NPA declare hone ke baad:
  1. Bank borrower ko **demand notice** send karta hai (certain days ka time diya jata hai).  
  2. Agar repayment nahi hota,  
     - bank **secured asset par symbolic / physical possession** le sakta hai,  
     - asset ko auction / sell kar sakta hai,  
     - ya management control change karne jaise measures le sakta hai.  
  3. Borrower ko limited scope hai DRT / court me challenge karne ka (as per Act).

(Exact days, sections aur process steps exam year ke hisaab se bare detail me alag se padho.)

### 3.4 Limitations & Coverage

- SARFAESI only:
  - secured loans ke liye apply hota hai,  
  - agricultural land etc. par restrictions ho sakti hain.  
- Small loans / small borrowers ke liye threshold conditions etc. RBI / law ke under defined hote hain.

> Exam line: **“SARFAESI = secured creditors ko court ke bina secured assets enforce karne ki legal power.”**

## 4. Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC)

### 4.1 Purpose

- Corporate borrowers ke insolvency cases jaise issues ke liye pehle multiple overlapping laws the –  
  - process slow, recovery low.  
- **Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC)** ka main objective:  
  - **single, time‑bound insolvency resolution framework** provide karna,  
  - creditor driven process,  
  - economic value preserve karna.

### 4.2 Key Features (High Level)

1. **Corporate Insolvency Resolution Process (CIRP):**
   - Jab corporate borrower (company) loan default karta hai,  
   - financial or operational creditor NCLT (National Company Law Tribunal) me insolvency application file kar sakte hain (threshold default amount ke basis par).  
   - NCLT admission ke baad:
     - moratorium period,  
     - Interim Resolution Professional (IRP) appointment,  
     - Committee of Creditors (CoC) formation,  
     - resolution plan invite, evaluate and vote.

2. **Time‑Bound Resolution:**
   - IBC attempt karta hai ki resolution **specified number of days** ke andar ho jaye (statutory time limit + extension provisions).  

3. **Committee of Creditors (CoC):**
   - Financial creditors milkar CoC banate hain.  
   - CoC hi resolution plan approve/reject karta hai (voting % threshold ke sath).

4. **Liquidation:**
   - Agar resolution possible nahi hua to company liquidation me ja sakti hai;  
   - assets sell hote hain; proceeds “waterfall” mechanism se distribute hote hain.

5. **Individuals & Partnerships ke liye bhi framework:**
   - IBC me corporate ke alawa individual / partnership insolvency provisions bhi hain (implementation stages exam year ke hisaab se check karo).

### 4.3 Banks & NPAs ke Liye IBC ka Importance

- IBC ne banks ko ek **powerful resolution tool** diya:  
  - Large NPAs ke cases IBC route se solve kiye gaye.  
- IBC ke through:
  - going concern sale (business as a whole sold to new promoter) possible,  
  - impairment ke sath lekin structured resolution.

> Exam angle: IBC = **time‑bound corporate insolvency & bankruptcy framework** with NCLT, IRP, CoC etc.

## 5. DRT vs SARFAESI vs IBC – Simple Comparison

| Aspect                     | DRT                               | SARFAESI                                  | IBC                                          |
|---------------------------|------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| Main Focus                | Debt recovery                     | Enforcement of security interest          | Insolvency resolution & bankruptcy           |
| Who Uses?                 | Banks / FIs                       | Secured creditors (banks / FIs)           | Financial / operational creditors, debtor    |
| Forum / Authority         | DRT / DRAT                        | Creditor action + DRT for appeals         | NCLT / NCLAT                                 |
| Security Needed?          | Secured/Unsecured (as per case)   | Mainly **secured loans**                  | Corporate debtor default (both secured/unsec)|
| Courtless Process?        | Tribunal based                    | Secured asset enforcement without court   | Tribunal based, but structured process       |
| Outcome                   | Recovery order                    | Asset possession / sale, recovery         | Resolution plan / liquidation                |

(Ye table conceptual difference dikhata hai; actual legal overlaps & procedures detail me Acts me given hain.)

## 6. Exam Special One‑Liners – SARFAESI, DRT, IBC

1. DRT – **special tribunal** banks / FIs ke loan recovery cases ke liye – normal civil court se faster disposal ke liye banaya gaya.  
2. SARFAESI Act – **Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest** –  
   secured creditors ko court ke bina secured assets enforce karne ki legal power deta hai.  
3. Asset Reconstruction Companies (ARCs) – SARFAESI ke under NPAs ko acquire karke unka reconstruction / resolution karte hain.  
4. IBC – **time‑bound insolvency & bankruptcy framework**; corporate insolvency ke liye NCLT forum.  
5. IBC me **Committee of Creditors (CoC)** resolution plan approve/reject karti hai.  
6. In teen frameworks ne milkar India me **NPA resolution & loan recovery** system ko strong banane ki try ki.

## 7. Memory Tricks – SARFAESI, DRT, IBC

**Trick 1 – “D–S–I = Decide, Seize, Insolvent”**

- **D – DRT**:  
  - Tribunal **decides** recovery cases.  
- **S – SARFAESI**:  
  - Creditor **seizes** / sells secured asset.  
- **I – IBC**:  
  - Company **insolvent** → resolution / liquidation.

**Trick 2 – “Court – Less – Code”**

- **Court (DRT)** – special court/tribunal.  
- **Less (SARFAESI)** – court **less** enforcement of security interest.  
- **Code (IBC)** – detailed **Code** for insolvency & bankruptcy.
