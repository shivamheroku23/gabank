## 2. Historical Background (Simplified Timeline)

SBI ka history thoda complex hai, isliye exam point of view se simple timeline yaad rakho:

1. **Presidency Banks Era:**
   - Bank of Calcutta (later Bank of Bengal) – early 19th century.  
   - Bank of Bombay.  
   - Bank of Madras.  
   - Ye teen milkar later **Imperial Bank of India** banaye gaye.

2. **Imperial Bank of India:**
   - British period ka major commercial bank.  

3. **Creation of SBI (1955):**
   - Government of India ne Imperial Bank of India ko acquire kiya.  
   - **State Bank of India Act, 1955** ke under SBI establish hua.  
   - SBI ko large‑scale branch network aur rural areas me banking spread karne ka mandate mila.

4. **Associate Banks Era & Merger:**
   - State Bank of Bikaner & Jaipur, State Bank of Mysore, State Bank of Patiala, etc. jaisi SBI associate banks.  
   - Time ke sath in associate banks ko SBI me merge kiya gaya (multiple phases).  
   - Result: SBI ek **very large, unified PSB** ban gaya jiska all‑India presence bahut strong hai.

> Exam line: SBI = “Imperial Bank ka successor + SBI Act, 1955 se born.”

## 3. Objectives & Role of SBI

- **Commercial Banking:**
  - Deposits, loans, remittances, retail and corporate banking – sab segments me active.  
- **Financial Inclusion & Government Schemes:**
  - PMJDY accounts, DBT, pensions, social security schemes ka large share.  
- **Priority Sector Lending:**
  - Agriculture, MSMEs, weaker sections, housing, education loans.  
- **International Presence:**
  - Overseas branches / offices ke through NRI services, trade finance, remittances.  
- **Government Business:**
  - Govt receipts & payments, tax collection, pensions, etc. handle karne me key player.

## 4. Main Functions & Business Areas

### 4.1 Retail Banking

- Savings & current accounts, FDs, RDs,  
- Personal loans, housing loans, vehicle loans, education loans, etc.  
- Debit cards, credit cards, internet & mobile banking.

### 4.2 Corporate & SME Banking

- Working capital finance, term loans, project finance.  
- Trade finance (LCs, bank guarantees, bill discounting).  
- SME loans, business loans, supply chain finance.

### 4.3 Rural & Agricultural Banking

- Kisan Credit Cards (KCC), crop loans, farm mechanisation loans.  
- Rural & semi‑urban branches ke through farmers, SHGs, rural enterprises ko finance.

### 4.4 International Banking

- Overseas branches / subsidiaries ke through:  
  - NRI services, remittances, trade finance, forex services.

### 4.5 Digital Banking & Technology

- Net banking, mobile banking app, UPI app, digital wallets etc.  
- Large ATM network, POS terminals.
