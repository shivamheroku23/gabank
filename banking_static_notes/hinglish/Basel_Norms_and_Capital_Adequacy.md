# Basel Norms and Capital Adequacy – Static Notes

Ye file banks ke **capital adequacy concept** aur **Basel norms (Basel I, II, III)** ko exam‑oriented tareeke se explain karti hai.

---

## 1. Capital Adequacy – Basic Idea

Bank lending karta hai, risk leta hai. Agar bade losses ho gaye to deposits ka kya hoga?  
Isliye bank ke paas **“owners ka paisa” (capital)** hona zaroori hai jo losses ko absorb kar sake.

- **Capital Adequacy** ka matlab:  
  - Bank ke paas **itna capital ho ki wo apne risk‑weighted assets ke comparison me safe level par ho**.  
- Isko measure karne ke liye ek main ratio use hota hai:

> **Capital to Risk‑Weighted Assets Ratio (CRAR) ya Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR)**  

Formula (conceptually):

- **CAR = (Regulatory Capital / Risk‑Weighted Assets) × 100**

Jitna zyada CAR, utna bank loss absorb karne me capable.

---

## 2. Regulatory Capital – Tier I & Tier II

### 2.1 Tier I Capital (Core Capital)

- **Highest quality capital** – jo losses ko turant absorb kar sakta hai.  
- Example (conceptually):
  - Paid‑up equity capital,  
  - Disclosed reserves,  
  - Retained earnings,  
  - Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) etc. (Basel III framework me).

### 2.2 Tier II Capital (Supplementary Capital)

- Medium quality capital – jo **going concern** se zyada **winding up** situation me losses absorb karta hai.  
- Example (conceptually):
  - Subordinated debt,  
  - General provisions,  
  - Hybrid instruments (subject to conditions).

Regulators minimum **overall CAR** aur **Tier I / CET1 ka bhi minimum %** specify karte hain (exact % exam year ke hisaab se RBI guidelines se verify karo).

---

## 3. Basel Committee on Banking Supervision (BCBS)

- 1974 ke Herstatt Bank collapse ke baad global forum ki need feel hui.  
- **Basel Committee on Banking Supervision (BCBS)**  
  - Bank for International Settlements (BIS), Basel (Switzerland) me located.  
  - Major developed countries ke central banks / supervisors ka group.  
  - Objective:  
    - banking supervision ke liye **global standards & guidelines** banana,  
    - cross‑border stability maintain karna.

Basel Committee ne hi **Basel I, Basel II, Basel III** capital framework issue kiya.

---

## 4. Basel I – First Capital Accord

- **Year:** 1988 (implementation late 80s / early 90s se countries me start).  
- Focus:
  - Credit risk ke liye minimum capital standard fix karna.  
- Key features (concept level):
  - Risk‑weighted assets (RWA) concept introduce hua.  
  - Different assets ko risk weight diya gaya (0%, 20%, 50%, 100% etc.).  
  - **Global minimum capital ratio (CAR)** decide hua (historically 8% of RWA).  

Basel I simple framework tha, jisme risk differentiation limited thi. Isko later Basel II ne enhance kiya.

---

## 5. Basel II – Three Pillar Framework

Basel II ka main focus:

- Risk measurement ko zyada risk‑sensitive banana,  
- aur supervision + market discipline ko strong karna.

### 5.1 Three Pillars of Basel II

1. **Pillar 1 – Minimum Capital Requirements**
   - Credit risk, market risk, operational risk ke liye minimum capital requirement.  
   - Risk measurement approaches (Standardised / IRB approaches).  

2. **Pillar 2 – Supervisory Review Process**
   - Regulators (jaise RBI) banks ke internal capital assessment, risk management systems ko review karte hain.  
   - Agar risk zyada hai to additional capital demand kar sakte hain.

3. **Pillar 3 – Market Discipline**
   - Disclosure requirements:  
     - banks ko apni capital, risk exposures, risk management practices etc. public ko disclose karna hota hai.  
   - Market participants (investors, depositors) informed decisions le paate hain.

Basel II ne risk management ka culture zyada structured banaya.

---

## 6. Basel III – Post‑Crisis Strengthening

2007–08 global financial crisis ke baad pata chala:

- Banks ke paas high quality capital kam hai,  
- leverage zyada hai,  
- liquidity mis‑match problem hai.

Isliye BCBS ne **Basel III** framework introduce kiya (2010 se onward).

### 6.1 Major Features of Basel III (Concept Level)

1. **Higher and Better Quality Capital**
   - **Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1)** ka concept –  
     - sabse high quality capital (pure equity, retained earnings).  
   - Minimum CET1, Tier I, Total capital ke liye higher thresholds.  

2. **Capital Buffers**
   - **Capital Conservation Buffer (CCB)** –  
     - banks ko normal times me extra capital buffer maintain karna hota hai.  
   - **Counter‑cyclical Capital Buffer (CCyB)** –  
     - credit boom ke time extra capital build‑up,  
     - bust ke time use for loss absorption (framework as per country decision).

3. **Leverage Ratio**
   - **Non‑risk based measure** –  
     - total exposure ke against minimum Tier I capital ratio.  
   - Objective: excessive leverage (bahut zyada assets / off‑balance sheet exposures) ko limit karna.

4. **Liquidity Standards**
   - **Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR)**:
     - short‑term liquidity stress (30 days) handle karne ke liye  
     - high quality liquid assets (HQLA) ka minimum stock maintain karna.  
   - **Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR)**:
     - long‑term stable funding ensure karne ke liye  
     - available stable funding vs required stable funding ratio.

5. **Systemically Important Banks (SIBs) Buffers**
   - Globally systemically important banks (G‑SIBs) aur domestically important banks (D‑SIBs) ke liye extra capital requirement.

(Exact % thresholds aur timelines har country ki implementation plan ke hisaab se hoti hain; India me RBI ne phased manner me Basel III implement kiya – exam year ke liye current ratios official circular se check karo.)

---

## 7. Basel Norms in India – High Level Adoption

- **Basel I:**  
  - India ne 1990s me Basel I norms adopt kiye.  

- **Basel II:**  
  - 2000s me phased manner me Indian commercial banks ke liye Basel II implement hua.  

- **Basel III:**  
  - 2010s se RBI ne Basel III framework phased manner me adopt kiya –  
    - CET1, CCB, leverage ratio, LCR, NSFR, etc. ke norms Indian banks par apply huye.  

- RBI generally **global minimum se thoda higher capital requirement** prescribe karta hai taaki Indian banking system zyada safe rahe.

---

## 8. Why Capital Adequacy and Basel Norms Matter (Exam Angle)

- Depositors ka confidence –  
  - strong capital ka matlab bank collapse ka risk kam.  
- Financial stability –  
  - systemic risk default kam hota hai.  
- International integration –  
  - cross‑border operations ke liye globally accepted standards follow karna zaroori.  
- Risk management –  
  - proper risk measurement & management culture build hota hai.

---

## 9. Exam Special One‑Liners – Basel & Capital

1. Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR/CRAR) = **Total Regulatory Capital / Risk‑Weighted Assets**.  
2. Tier I capital = **core capital** (equity, reserves); Tier II = **supplementary capital**.  
3. Basel Committee on Banking Supervision – **BIS, Basel (Switzerland)** me –  
   international banking supervision guidelines banata hai.  
4. **Basel I (1988)** – primarily **credit risk** par focus, simple risk weights + minimum capital.  
5. **Basel II** – **3 Pillars**:  
   - Pillar 1 – Minimum capital,  
   - Pillar 2 – Supervisory review,  
   - Pillar 3 – Market discipline.  
6. **Basel III** – crisis ke baad stronger framework:  
   - CET1, capital buffers, leverage ratio, LCR, NSFR.  
7. India me Basel norms RBI ke through **phased manner** me implement huye,  
   aur India generally global standard se **slightly higher capital requirement** rakhne ke favour me rehti hai.

## 10. Memory Tricks – Basel Norms

**Trick 1 – “1 Risk, 2 Pillars, 3 Buffers”**

- **Basel I** – ek main focus – credit **risk** ke liye capital.  
- **Basel II** – 3 **pillars** (capital, supervision, disclosure).  
- **Basel III** – multiple **buffers** (CET1 focus, conservation buffer, counter‑cyclical, liquidity buffers).

**Trick 2 – “CAR ka car – Capital Above Risk”**

CAR yaad rakhne ke liye:

- **Capital Above Risk** –  
  - *CAR = Capital / Risk‑Weighted Assets* –  
    risk ke upar jitna capital, utni safety.

Aap is file me exam year ke hisaab se:

- India ke liye exact minimum CET1, Tier I, total capital %,  
- CCB %, leverage ratio %, LCR / NSFR implementation dates,  
ko alag se note kar sakte ho, latest RBI Master Circular / Master Direction follow karke.
