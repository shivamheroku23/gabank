# Types of Banks in India – Static Notes

Ye file India me **banks ke main types / classifications** ko exam‑oriented tareeke se cover karti hai.  
Isse aapko “kaun sa bank kis category me aata hai” clear ho jayega.

---

## 1. Broad Classification – 3 Level Socho

India me banks ko broadly 3 angles se yaad rakho:

1. **Ownership ke hisaab se**
   - Public Sector Banks (PSBs)  
   - Private Sector Banks  
   - Foreign Banks  

2. **Function / Role ke hisaab se**
   - Central Bank – **RBI** (already separate file)  
   - Commercial Banks (Scheduled / Non‑Scheduled)  
   - Regional Rural Banks (RRBs)  
   - Cooperative Banks  
   - Development Banks / Financial Institutions (NABARD, SIDBI, EXIM, NHB, etc.)  

3. **Special Category ke hisaab se**
   - Payments Banks  
   - Small Finance Banks (SFBs)  
   - Local Area Banks (LABs – concept)  
   - Specialized institutions (Export‑Import bank, housing bank, etc.)

> Memory trick: **“OFS – Ownership, Function, Special”** – pehle yahi 3 angle yaad rakho.

## 3. Commercial Banks

Commercial banks ka main kaam:

- Public se deposits lena,  
- loans & advances dena,  
- payments / remittances handle karna,  
- normal banking services provide karna.

### 3.1 Scheduled vs Non‑Scheduled Banks

**Scheduled Banks:**

- Jo banks **RBI Act, 1934** ke **Second Schedule** me include hain.  
- Conditions:
  - Paid‑up capital + reserves minimum certain level (RBI decide karta hai).  
  - Business conduct prejudicial to depositor / public interest nahi hona chahiye.  
- Inko:
  - RBI se refinance facilities,  
  - clearing house membership,  
  - certain privileges milte hain.

**Non‑Scheduled Banks:**

- Jo Second Schedule me nahi hain.  
- Scale chhota hota hai, privileges kam.

### 3.2 Public Sector Banks (PSBs)

- Majority **Government of India / State Govt** ke paas shareholding (usually 51%+).  
- Example (data time ke hisaab se list change ho sakti hai; aap exam year ke hisaab se update karo).  
- PSBs ka main role:
  - Financial inclusion,  
  - priority sector lending,  
  - govt schemes implement karna,  
  - wide branch network.

### 3.3 Private Sector Banks

- Majority shareholding **private entities / individuals** ke paas.  
- Do type:
  - Old private sector banks  
  - New private sector banks (reforms ke baad wale)  
- Known for:
  - technology adoption,  
  - niche products,  
  - retail focused business model, etc.

### 3.4 Foreign Banks

- Jinke Head Office **India ke bahar** kisi foreign country me hota hai,  
- India me branch / subsidiary ke through operate karte hain.  
- RBI ke regulation + home country regulator dono ka compliance important.

## 4. Regional Rural Banks (RRBs)

- Establishment ka base: **RRB Act, 1976**.  
- Objective:
  - Rural areas me **small & marginal farmers, agricultural labourers, artisans, small entrepreneurs** ko banking service dena.  
- Ownership structure (typical pattern):
  - Central Govt + State Govt + Sponsor Bank (ek public sector bank) – shareholding ratio exam year ke hisaab se check karein.  
- RRBs ka focus:
  - Agricultural loans,  
  - rural self‑employment,  
  - govt rural schemes ka implementation.

Aap is topic ke liye separate detailed file bana sakte ho:  
`Regional_Rural_Banks_RRBs.md` – jisme list of RRBs, amalgamation, etc. daal sakte ho.

---

## 5. Cooperative Banks

- **Cooperative principle**: “One person, one vote” – profit se zyada **service** focus.  
- Mainly **rural + semi urban** areas me strong presence, lekin urban co‑operative banks bhi hote hain.  
- Structure (simplified):
  - Primary Agricultural Credit Societies (PACS)  
  - District Central Cooperative Banks (DCCBs)  
  - State Cooperative Banks (SCBs)  
- Regulation:
  - Dual control type structure – RBI + State Registrars, etc. (acts exam year ke hisaab se update karein).

Ispe detailed theory ke liye aap file bana sakte ho: `Cooperative_Banking_Structure.md`.

## 6. Development Financial Institutions (DFIs) / Specialized Banks

Ye institutions long‑term development ke liye banaye gaye:

- **NABARD** – agriculture & rural development finance.  
- **SIDBI** – MSME & small industries finance.  
- **EXIM Bank** – export‑import finance.  
- **NHB (National Housing Bank)** – housing finance sector ka development.  

In sab ke liye alag‑alag `.md` files banana best hai, jisme:

- establishment year,  
- parent Act,  
- ownership pattern,  
- main schemes,  
- exam one‑liners likh sakte ho.

## 7. Payments Banks

- Concept: **small deposits + payments / remittance** focused banks.  
- Inka main feature:
  - **Limited deposit amount** per customer (ceiling exam year ke hisaab se check karein),  
  - **loans nahi de sakte**,  
  - only **payment / remittance / small savings** type services.  
- Aim:
  - Unbanked / underbanked population ko simple banking service dena,  
  - migrants, small businesses, low income households etc. ke liye.

Aap iske liye `Payments_Banks.md` file bana kar list of licensed payments banks + features daal sakte ho.

---

## 8. Small Finance Banks (SFBs)

- Objective:
  - **Small borrowers** – small businesses, micro & small industries, small & marginal farmers, unorganised sector ke logon ko formal credit dena.  
- Fully functional banks, lekin:
  - focus unserved & underserved segments par,  
  - certain rules on priority sector lending, etc.

Iske liye recommended file: `Small_Finance_Banks.md` – jisme:

- licensing,  
- list of SFBs,  
- conversion from MFIs to SFBs,  
- exam ke important one‑liners likhe ja sakte hain.

## 10. Quick Revision One‑Liners – Types of Banks

1. **RBI** – India ka **central bank / monetary authority**; sirf ek hi hota hai.  
2. **Commercial Banks** – deposits lete hain + loans & advances dete hain + normal banking services.  
3. **Public Sector Banks** – majority Govt ownership; financial inclusion & govt schemes ka main carrier.  
4. **Private Sector Banks** – majority private ownership; technology & retail banking me aggressive.  
5. **Foreign Banks** – head office abroad; India me branches / subsidiaries.  
6. **RRBs** – rural credit focus; RRB Act 1976; target – small & marginal farmers etc.  
7. **Cooperative Banks** – “one person, one vote”; service motive; rural & urban co‑op banks.  
8. **DFIs** – long‑term development finance (NABARD, SIDBI, EXIM, NHB, etc.).  
9. **Payments Banks** – small deposits + payments / remittance; loans allowed nahi.  
10. **Small Finance Banks** – small borrowers + unserved segments ke liye specialized banks.
