# Indian Financial Markets and Instruments – Static Notes

Ye file **Indian financial markets** (money market, capital market) aur basic instruments (T‑Bills, bonds, shares, etc.) ka simple overview deti hai.  
Current affairs me jab “G‑Sec yield, CP, CD, IPO” jaise words aate hain, tab ye static base kaam aayega.

---

## 1. Financial Market – Basic Split

Financial markets ko broadly do main parts me socho:

1. **Money Market**
   - Short‑term funds ka market (generally **up to 1 year** maturity).  

2. **Capital Market**
   - Long‑term funds ka market (**more than 1 year** maturity).

Dono markets me different instruments & participants hote hain.

---

## 2. Money Market – Short‑Term Funds

Money market me highly liquid, short‑term instruments trade hote hain.  
Important instruments (concept level):

### 2.1 Treasury Bills (T‑Bills)

- **Issuer:** Government of India.  
- **Maturity:** Short‑term (e.g., 91‑day, 182‑day, 364‑day).  
- **Type:** Zero‑coupon – discount par issue, face value par redeem.  
- **Risk:** Sovereign (Govt) – default risk negligible.

### 2.2 Call Money / Notice Money

- **Call money:** Overnight (1 day) funds market between banks & FIs.  
- **Notice money:** 2–14 days funds.  
- **Call money rate:** Inter‑bank short term interest rate.

### 2.3 Commercial Paper (CP)

- **Issuer:** Highly rated corporates / financial institutions.  
- **Type:** Unsecured money market instrument, short‑term, issued at discount.  
- **Use:** Working capital needs ke liye funds uthana.

### 2.4 Certificate of Deposit (CD)

- **Issuer:** Banks (and certain FIs as per RBI rules).  
- **Type:** Negotiable money market instrument, usually large value, fixed maturity.  
- **Use:** Banks ke liye short term bulk deposits raise karne ka tool.

---

## 3. Capital Market – Long‑Term Funds

Capital market me **shares, debentures, bonds** etc. trade hote hain.

### 3.1 Primary Market

- Jahan **naye securities issue** hote hain:
  - IPO (Initial Public Offering),  
  - FPO (Follow‑on Public Offer),  
  - private placements, rights issues.  
- Companies yahan se **fresh capital raise** karti hain.

### 3.2 Secondary Market

- Jahan pehle se issue kiye gaye securities **investors ke beech trade** hote hain.  
- Stock exchanges (e.g., NSE, BSE) par listed shares / debentures ka trading.

---

## 4. Equity Shares & Debt Instruments – Basic Definitions

### 4.1 Equity Shares

- Company me **ownership stake**.  
- Equity shareholders ka:

  - voting rights (generally),  
  - dividend receive (if declared),  
  - residual claim on assets (after creditors).

- High risk, high return type investment.

### 4.2 Debentures / Bonds

- Company / government ke **debt instruments** –  
  - fixed interest (coupon) pay,  
  - maturity par principal repay.  
- Types (concept level):
  - Government bonds (G‑Secs),  
  - Corporate bonds / debentures,  
  - Tax‑free bonds, etc.

### 4.3 Government Securities (G‑Secs)

- Long‑term Govt of India bonds (maturity >1 year).  
- RBI auctions ke through issue.  
- G‑Sec yield / prices ka movement news me frequently aata hai.

---

## 5. Regulators of Indian Financial Markets

- **RBI (Reserve Bank of India)**  
  - Money market, government securities market, forex market, payment systems.  

- **SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India)**  
  - Capital market, mutual funds, stock exchanges, intermediaries, listed companies.  

- **IRDAI (Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India)**  
  - Insurance sector.  

- **PFRDA (Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority)**  
  - Pension sector (NPS, etc.).

Banks in markets:

- Banks money market me active, G‑Secs me invest karte hain,  
- capital market operations (merchant banking, MF, etc.) also have link.

---

## 6. Mutual Funds – Short Concept

- Mutual fund investors ka paisa pool karta hai,  
  - usko equity, debt, money market instruments me invest karta hai as per scheme objective.  
- **Regulator:** SEBI.  
- Types (basic):

  - Equity funds,  
  - Debt funds,  
  - Hybrid funds,  
  - Liquid funds, etc.

Banks mutual fund units distribute karte hain; some bank‑sponsored mutual funds bhi hote hain.

---

## 7. Derivatives – Very Basic View

- **Derivative:** Financial instrument jiska value kisi underlying (share, index, commodity, currency, interest rate) se derive hota hai.  
- Types:
  - Forwards, futures, options, swaps (concept only).  
- Use:
  - Hedging, speculation, arbitrage.

Banks derivatives me risk management & trading ke liye participate karte hain (regulatory norms ke under).

---

## 8. Forex Market – Concept Snapshot

- Foreign Exchange (Forex) market me different currencies trade hoti hain.  
- RBI external sector & forex market ko manage / monitor karta hai (FEMA ke under powers).  
- Banks ke **authorised dealers** forex transactions handle karte hain –  
  - trade, remittances, travel, investments etc. ke liye.

---

## 9. Exam Special One‑Liners – Financial Markets

1. Money market – **short‑term funds (≤ 1 year)** ka market; instruments – T‑Bills, call/notice money, CP, CD.  
2. Capital market – **long‑term funds (> 1 year)** ka market; instruments – equity, debentures, bonds.  
3. T‑Bills – short‑term Govt securities, zero‑coupon, discount par issue, face value par redemption.  
4. Commercial Paper – corporates ke short‑term unsecured debt instruments.  
5. Certificate of Deposit – banks ke negotiable short‑term deposits.  
6. Primary market – **new issues** (IPO, FPO etc.); secondary market – **stock exchange trading**.  
7. SEBI – capital market regulator; RBI – money & forex markets regulator.  
8. Mutual funds – pooled investment vehicle, regulator – SEBI.

---

## 10. Memory Trick – “M–C–R–S”

- **M** – Money market – short term, T‑Bills, CP, CD.  
- **C** – Capital market – long term, equity, bonds.  
- **R** – RBI – money, forex, G‑Secs regulator.  
- **S** – SEBI – securities (shares, MFs, capital market) regulator.

Is file ko current affairs ke sath jodo:

- G‑Sec yield movement,  
- RBI bond auctions,  
- SEBI circulars,  
- CP/CD issuance,  
- IPO news,  

taa ki static + dynamic dono strong ho jaye.
