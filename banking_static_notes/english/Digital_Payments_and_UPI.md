# Digital Payments and UPI – Static Notes (English)

This file gives an exam‑oriented overview of **India’s digital payments ecosystem** –  
UPI, BHIM, QR, BBPS, AEPS, RuPay, wallets, etc.

---

## 1. Digital Payments – Overview

Digital payments mean:

- Making payments via **electronic modes** instead of cash, such as:
  - Internet banking, mobile banking, cards, UPI, wallets, etc.

India’s digital payments revolution is driven by:

- **RBI** – regulator,  
- **NPCI** – retail payments system operator,  
- **Banks + Fintechs** – provide products and services.

---

## 2. NPCI – National Payments Corporation of India

- **Type:** Section 8 (not‑for‑profit) company.  
- **Role:** Umbrella organisation for **retail payment systems** in India.  
- **Promoted by:** Indian banks under the guidance of RBI & IBA.  
- **Major products / systems:**
  - IMPS, UPI, BHIM, RuPay, NACH, AEPS, BBPS, etc.

NPCI provides the “rails” for digital payments; regulation remains with RBI.

---

## 3. UPI – Unified Payments Interface

### 3.1 Basic Concept

- **UPI** is a real‑time payment system that:

  - Allows linking **multiple bank accounts** in a single mobile app,  
  - Enables payments using **VPA (Virtual Payment Address)**, mobile number, Aadhaar, account+IFSC, QR, etc.

- Operator: **NPCI**  
- Regulator: **RBI**

### 3.2 Key Features

- **24×7, 365‑day** instant payments.  
- Supports Person‑to‑Person (P2P) and Person‑to‑Merchant (P2M) transactions.  
- “Pay” and “Collect” functionality (can send payment requests).  
- Uses **IMPS infrastructure** in the background.  
- Allows users to pay via VPA without sharing bank account number and IFSC.

### 3.3 UPI Apps

- BHIM app (by NPCI),  
- Bank apps (SBI, HDFC, ICICI, etc.),  
- Third‑party apps (PhonePe, Google Pay, Paytm, etc. – as per regulatory approval).

> Exam line: UPI = **NPCI’s real‑time mobile‑based funds transfer system**.

---

## 4. BHIM – Bharat Interface for Money

- BHIM is a **UPI‑based mobile app** launched to promote digital payments.  
- Developed by **NPCI**, promoted by the Government of India.  
- Provides a simple interface for:
  - Send / receive money,  
  - Scan & pay using QR.

---

## 5. QR Codes, Bharat QR, UPI QR

- **QR (Quick Response) Code:**  
  - 2‑D matrix code storing merchant / payment information; scannable by mobile camera.  

- **UPI QR / Bharat QR:**
  - Customer scans the QR code, enters the amount, and authorises payment using UPI PIN.  

- For merchants:
  - Low‑cost acceptance (no expensive POS machine),  
  - Simple QR sticker or standee is enough to accept digital payments.

---

## 6. RuPay – Domestic Card Scheme

- **RuPay** is India’s domestic card payment network, operated by **NPCI**.  
- Types:
  - RuPay debit cards, credit cards, prepaid cards.  
- Objectives:
  - Low‑cost card payments,  
  - Financial inclusion (e.g. many PMJDY accounts are issued RuPay cards).

> Frequently asked: “Who operates the RuPay card network?” – **NPCI**.

---

## 7. AEPS – Aadhaar Enabled Payment System

- **AEPS (Aadhaar Enabled Payment System):** NPCI product.  
- Uses Aadhaar number + biometric (fingerprint/iris) for transactions such as:
  - cash withdrawal, cash deposit, balance enquiry, fund transfer.  
- Used mainly:
  - in rural areas through **Business Correspondents (BCs) / Customer Service Points (CSPs)** and micro‑ATMs.  
- Important for **financial inclusion**.

---

## 8. BBPS – Bharat Bill Payment System

- **BBPS (Bharat Bill Payment System):** integrated bill payment system by NPCI.  
- Features:
  - One platform to pay various bills – electricity, gas, DTH, water, insurance premiums, etc.  
  - Interoperable – available through both online channels and physical outlets.

> Exam line: BBPS = **interoperable bill payment system operated by NPCI**.

---

## 9. Prepaid Payment Instruments (PPIs) – Wallets, Prepaid Cards

- **PPIs** are instruments where value is **pre‑loaded** for future payments.

Main types (conceptual):

- Closed system wallets – usable only with the issuing entity (single merchant).  
- Semi‑closed wallets – usable with multiple merchants within a network.  
- Prepaid cards – gift cards, prepaid debit cards, etc.

Regulator: **RBI** (under the Payment and Settlement Systems Act).

---

## 10. Digital Banking Channels – Overview

- **Internet Banking** – browser‑based online banking.  
- **Mobile Banking** – app‑based banking on smartphones.  
- **USSD Banking (*99#)** – simple menu‑based banking for feature phones (UPI over USSD).  
- **Cards** – debit, credit, prepaid (used at PoS, ATMs, online, contactless, etc.).

Digital channels make banking **24×7 and location‑independent**.

---

## 11. Security & Customer Protection – Key Points

- Two‑factor authentication (2FA):  
  - Something you know – PIN/password,  
  - Something you have – device/SIM/card,  
  - Something you are – biometric.  
- Transaction alerts via SMS, email or app notifications.  
- Per‑transaction and daily transaction limits.  
- RBI guidelines on **customer liability** in unauthorised electronic transactions.

---

## 12. Exam One‑Liners – Digital & UPI

1. UPI – **NPCI’s real‑time mobile‑based payment system**, regulated by RBI.  
2. UPI uses **VPA (Virtual Payment Address)** to send/receive money without sharing account details.  
3. BHIM – UPI‑based app developed by **NPCI**, promoted by Government of India.  
4. RuPay – **domestic card payment network**, operated by **NPCI**.  
5. AEPS – **Aadhaar Enabled Payment System**, used at BC outlets via Aadhaar + biometric.  
6. BBPS – **Bharat Bill Payment System**, interoperable bill payment platform by NPCI.  
7. NPCI operates IMPS, UPI, RuPay, AEPS, BBPS, NACH and other retail payment systems.  
8. RBI is the **regulator** of payment systems under the Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007.
