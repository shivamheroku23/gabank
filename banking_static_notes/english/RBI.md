# Reserve Bank of India (RBI) – Full Static Notes (English)

## 1. Basic Profile

- **Full Name:** Reserve Bank of India (RBI)  
- **Type:** Central Bank of India / Monetary Authority  
- **Head Office:** Mumbai, Maharashtra  
- **Establishment:**  
  - Set up under the Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934  
  - **Commenced operations:** 1 April 1935 (from Kolkata)  
  - **Head Office shifted to Mumbai:** 1937  
- **Ownership:**  
  - Initially: privately owned with shareholder capital  
  - **Nationalisation:** 1 January 1949 – since then, fully owned by the Government of India  
- **Official Website:** https://www.rbi.org.in  

> Exam line: *“RBI is India’s central bank and monetary authority, working since 1935 and fully Government‑owned since 1949.”*

---

## 2. History & Key Milestones (Timeline Style)

- **1926:** Hilton Young Commission recommends setting up a central bank for India.  
- **1934:** Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 passed – main legal base for RBI’s establishment.  
- **1 April 1935:**  
  - RBI starts operations (from Kolkata).  
- **1937:**  
  - RBI’s Head Office shifted from Kolkata to **Mumbai**.  
- **1 January 1949:**  
  - **Nationalisation** of RBI – becomes 100% Government‑owned.  
- **1949:**  
  - Banking Regulation Act, 1949 comes into force – main law to regulate commercial banks.  
- **1950s–60s:**  
  - Focus on priority sector credit, agriculture, development finance; creation of specialised institutions (precursors to NABARD, etc.).  
- **1969 & 1980:**  
  - Two major rounds of bank nationalisation – RBI plays a key role in credit planning.  
- **Post‑1991 Reforms:**  
  - Liberalisation, entry of new private sector banks, prudential norms, rationalisation of CRR/SLR, capital adequacy norms (Basel guidelines), etc.  
- **2000s onwards:**  
  - Stronger role as payment system regulator – development of RTGS, NEFT and later UPI, BBPS, Bharat QR, etc.  
- **Recent decades:**  
  - Inflation targeting framework and Monetary Policy Committee (MPC),  
  - Prompt Corrective Action (PCA) framework for weak banks,  
  - Financial Stability Reports and macro‑prudential oversight.

> Timeline memory trick: **“Act 34, Birth 35, Bombay 37, National 49”**  
> (1934 – Act, 1935 – Operations start, 1937 – HQ Mumbai, 1949 – Nationalisation)

## 3. Objectives of RBI

You can summarise RBI’s objectives as **“MPF – Monetary, Price, Financial”**:

1. **Monetary Stability:**  
   - Manage money supply and interest rates so that the economy has adequate liquidity without excessive volatility.

2. **Price Stability with Growth:**  
   - Keep inflation under control while supporting sustainable economic growth – a balanced approach.

3. **Financial Stability:**  
   - Ensure the stability and smooth functioning of the banking system, NBFCs, payment systems, forex market, and government securities market.

4. **Developmental Role:**  
   - Support long‑term economic development through financial inclusion, rural credit, priority sector lending, and innovation in payments (UPI, RuPay, etc.).

## 4. Main Functions of RBI (Exam‑Friendly Classification)

A simple mnemonic for RBI’s functions:

> **“I‑B‑G‑F‑P‑D‑E” – Issue, Banker, Government, Finance, Payment, Development, Exchange**

### 4.1 Issue of Currency (Note‑Issuing Authority)

- RBI is the **sole authority for issuing banknotes** in India  
  (exception: ₹1 note is issued by the Ministry of Finance, but circulates through RBI).  
- Responsible for design, security features, denominations, withdrawal and introduction of notes.  
- Notes are issued through the **Issue Department** and backed by specified assets  
  (gold, foreign securities, Government securities, etc.).

### 4.2 Banker’s Bank & Lender of Last Resort

- For commercial banks, RBI is the **“banker’s bank”**:
  - Banks maintain accounts with RBI.  
  - Inter‑bank clearing and settlement happens through RBI.  
- In a liquidity crisis, RBI acts as **Lender of Last Resort** to banks (subject to conditions).

### 4.3 Banker, Agent & Advisor to Government

- For the **Central Government** and, in many cases, **State Governments**, RBI acts as:
  - **Banker:** maintains Government accounts; handles receipts and payments.  
  - **Agent:** manages issue and servicing of Government securities (T‑Bills, dated securities).  
  - **Debt Manager:** designs and manages Government borrowing programmes.  
  - **Advisor:** provides advice on economic and financial policies.

### 4.4 Controller of Credit / Monetary Policy Authority

RBI controls credit and liquidity using various tools:

- **CRR (Cash Reserve Ratio)**  
- **SLR (Statutory Liquidity Ratio)**  
- **Repo Rate & Reverse Repo Rate**  
- **Bank Rate, MSF (Marginal Standing Facility)**  
- **Open Market Operations (OMOs)** – purchase and sale of Government securities  

**Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) framework:**

- Policy repo rate is decided with reference to an inflation target.  
- MPC has 6 members – 3 from RBI (including the Governor) and 3 nominated by Government.

### 4.5 Regulator & Supervisor of Banks and NBFCs

- Regulates and supervises:
  - Scheduled Commercial Banks,  
  - Regional Rural Banks,  
  - certain categories of Cooperative Banks,  
  - NBFCs, etc.  
- Decides:
  - Licensing, branch expansion,  
  - capital norms, prudential norms,  
  - asset classification & provisioning,  
  - management and governance norms.  
- Uses on‑site inspection and off‑site surveillance to monitor financial health and risks.

### 4.6 Custodian of Foreign Exchange Reserves & Forex Management

- Manages the country’s **foreign exchange reserves**.  
- Supports external sector stability and orderly movement in the exchange rate.  
- Exercises powers under FEMA, 1999 for foreign exchange management and monitoring.

### 4.7 Regulator of Payment and Settlement Systems

- Regulates payment and settlement systems under the **Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007**.  
- Oversees systems like RTGS, NEFT, UPI, IMPS, AEPS, BBPS, card networks, etc.  
- Supervises and regulates institutions like NPCI in the payment space.

### 4.8 Developmental & Promotional Role

- Designs and supports:
  - Priority Sector Lending framework,  
  - Financial inclusion schemes,  
  - Licensing of Small Finance Banks, Payments Banks, etc.  
- Promotes:
  - Rural credit, MSME finance, weaker sections lending,  
  - Financial literacy, Ombudsman schemes, consumer protection.

## 5. Organisational Structure (High‑Level View)

- **Central Board of Directors** – apex decision‑making body of RBI:
  - **Official Directors:**
    - Governor (1)  
    - Deputy Governors (up to 4)  
  - **Non‑official Directors:**
    - Government nominees  
    - Directors from various sectors / fields  

- Appointment and tenure are governed by the RBI Act and Government decisions.  
- RBI has multiple **Regional Offices** (Mumbai, Delhi, Kolkata, Chennai, etc.) and **sub‑offices** across India.

> Exam line: *“RBI HQ – Mumbai; Central Board – apex body; Governor – chief executive of RBI.”*

## 6. Key Legal Framework

### 6.1 Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934

- Main law for RBI’s establishment, structure and functions.  
- Governs currency issue, monetary policy powers, and overall central banking functions.

### 6.2 Banking Regulation Act, 1949

- Main law for regulation and supervision of commercial banks.  
- Covers:
  - Licensing,  
  - capital and reserves,  
  - management and control,  
  - amalgamation and moratorium, etc.

### 6.3 Other Important Laws

- **FEMA, 1999 (Foreign Exchange Management Act):** foreign exchange management and external sector regulation.  
- **Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007:** regulation of payment and settlement systems.  
- **DICGC Act, 1961:** deposit insurance through DICGC (an RBI subsidiary).

---

## 7. Subsidiaries & Associated Institutions (Exam Angle)

- **DICGC – Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation**  
  - Wholly owned subsidiary of RBI.  
  - Provides deposit insurance (up to a prescribed limit) to depositors of commercial banks, RRBs and eligible cooperative banks.

- **BRBNMPL – Bharatiya Reserve Bank Note Mudran Pvt. Ltd.**  
  - RBI’s subsidiary for currency note printing.

- **Other linked institutions (independent but often asked with RBI):**
  - **NABARD** – apex body for agricultural & rural development finance.  
  - **SIDBI** – apex institution for MSME finance.  
  - **NHB** – apex housing finance institution (ownership structure has evolved – see current status).  
  - **EXIM Bank** – specialised institution for export‑import finance.

## 9. Key One‑Liners – RBI (Bank + SSC Exams)

1. RBI established on **1 April 1935** under the **RBI Act, 1934**; initial HQ at Kolkata; shifted to **Mumbai in 1937**.  
2. RBI nationalised on **1 January 1949**; since then 100% Government ownership.  
3. RBI is India’s **central bank, monetary authority and banking system regulator**.  
4. Authority for banknote issue – **RBI (Issue Department)**; ₹1 note – issued by **Ministry of Finance**.  
5. CRR, SLR, Repo, Reverse Repo, Bank Rate, MSF, OMOs – RBI’s key **monetary policy tools**.  
6. RBI acts as **banker, agent and advisor to Government**.  
7. RBI is the **bankers’ bank** and **Lender of Last Resort**.  
8. RBI is the **regulator of payment and settlement systems** (RTGS, NEFT, UPI, etc.).  
9. RBI is the **custodian of forex reserves** and plays a key role in external sector stability.  
10. Key RBI subsidiaries – **DICGC** (deposit insurance) and **BRBNMPL** (note printing).
