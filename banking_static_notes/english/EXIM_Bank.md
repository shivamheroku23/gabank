# EXIM Bank – Export‑Import Bank of India (English)

## 1. Basic Profile

- **Full Name:** Export‑Import Bank of India (EXIM Bank)  
- **Type:** Apex Export Finance Institution / Export Credit Agency  
- **Head Office:** Mumbai, Maharashtra  
- **Establishment:** 1 January 1982 (under the Export‑Import Bank of India Act, 1981)  
- **Main Focus:**  
  - Promote India’s exports and international trade through finance and related support.  
- **Ownership:**  
  - Specialised financial institution under Government of India (shareholding pattern to be checked as per latest data).  
- **Official Website:** https://www.eximbankindia.in  

---

## 2. Background & Establishment Timeline

- Rapid export growth required a **specialised export finance institution**.  
- The **Export‑Import Bank of India Act, 1981** was passed for this purpose.  
- EXIM Bank started operations on **1 January 1982**.  

EXIM Bank is designed as:

- The **apex institution** for export finance,  
- Providing medium / long‑term credit to exporters,  
- Arranging finance for overseas buyers and projects,  
- Supporting overseas investment and Lines of Credit to strengthen trade relations.

---

## 3. Objectives of EXIM Bank

- To **promote, finance, facilitate and coordinate** India’s exports.  
- To provide **short, medium and long‑term finance** to exporters.  
- To support overseas buyers, projects and institutions through lines of credit and buyer’s credit.  
- To support **overseas investments** (JVs, subsidiaries) of Indian companies.  
- To coordinate among banks, financial institutions and Government agencies in the export finance ecosystem.

---

## 4. Major Functions of EXIM Bank (Concept Level)

### 4.1 Financial Assistance to Exporters

- For exporters:
  - Pre‑shipment finance,  
  - Post‑shipment finance (particularly medium / long‑term),  
  - Supplier’s credit and buyer’s credit arrangements.  
- Special products for exports of capital goods, turnkey projects, consultancy services, etc.

### 4.2 Lines of Credit (LoCs)

- Provides **Lines of Credit** to foreign governments / institutions / development banks,  
  enabling them to import Indian goods and services.  

This helps:

- Expand India’s exports,  
- Strengthen trade and diplomatic relations.

### 4.3 Overseas Investment Finance

- Provides finance/support to Indian companies for:
  - Joint ventures,  
  - Wholly owned subsidiaries abroad,  

as per policy norms.

### 4.4 Guarantees & Other Support

- Plays a role (along with other institutions) in:
  - export credit guarantees,  
  - performance guarantees,  
  - bid bonds, etc.  
- Provides advisory services, sectoral and market studies, and export promotion support.

### 4.5 Research, Advisory & Promotional Role

- Conducts research studies on foreign markets, sectors and products.  
- Advises exporters on:
  - market entry,  
  - country risk,  
  - product positioning, etc.  
- Provides inputs to Government on export promotion policies.

---

## 5. EXIM Bank vs Normal Commercial Bank – Simple Difference

- **Commercial Bank:**
  - Provides general banking services across sectors – deposits, loans, remittances, domestic and foreign.

- **EXIM Bank:**
  - Specialised institution focused on **international trade, exports and overseas investments**.  
  - Its products, risk assessment and funding model are oriented towards export finance.
