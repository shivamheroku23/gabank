# Important Banking Terms and Ratios – Quick Glossary (English)

This file explains **important banking terms, ratios and acronyms** in 1–3 lines each.  
Use it as a **mini‑dictionary** during revision.

---

## 1. Monetary Policy & Liquidity Terms

### 1.1 CRR – Cash Reserve Ratio

- A certain % of a bank’s **NDTL (Net Demand and Time Liabilities)** that must be kept **as cash with RBI**.  
- RBI changes CRR to absorb or inject liquidity in the banking system.

### 1.2 SLR – Statutory Liquidity Ratio

- A certain % of NDTL that banks must hold in **approved securities + cash + gold**.  
- Ensures liquidity and solvency.

### 1.3 Repo Rate

- The interest rate at which banks **borrow short‑term funds from RBI** against Government securities (repurchase agreements).  
- RBI changes repo rate to influence overall interest rate levels in the economy.

### 1.4 Reverse Repo Rate

- The rate at which banks **park surplus funds with RBI** for short term.  
- Tool used by RBI to absorb liquidity.

### 1.5 Bank Rate

- A longer‑term policy rate historically used for certain types of refinance to banks.  
- Acts as an indicator rate in the monetary policy framework.

### 1.6 MSF – Marginal Standing Facility

- An overnight / emergency funding window under which banks can **borrow from RBI at a higher rate**,  
  typically when facing acute liquidity shortages (subject to conditions).

## 2. Asset Quality & NPAs

### 2.1 NPA – Non‑Performing Asset

- A loan where **interest or principal is overdue for more than 90 days** (general norm).  
- Classified into sub‑standard, doubtful and loss assets as per RBI norms.

### 2.2 Gross NPA

- Total amount of NPAs on the bank’s books – **before deducting provisions**.

### 2.3 Net NPA

- **Net NPA = Gross NPA − Provisions held on NPAs**  
- Indicates the residual problem after provisioning.

### 2.4 Provisioning

- Setting aside a portion of profits as **provisions** against NPAs / doubtful assets,  
  to absorb future losses without eroding capital.

## 3. Profitability & Efficiency Ratios

### 3.1 NIM – Net Interest Margin

- **NIM = Net Interest Income (NII) / Interest‑earning assets**  
- NII = Interest earned on loans & investments – interest paid on deposits & borrowings.  
- Higher NIM ⇒ better interest spread.

### 3.2 ROA – Return on Assets

- **ROA = Net profit / Total assets**  
- Shows profitability relative to total assets.

### 3.3 ROE – Return on Equity

- **ROE = Net profit / Shareholders’ equity**  
- Indicates return to shareholders.

### 3.4 Cost‑to‑Income Ratio

- **Cost‑to‑Income = Operating expenses / Operating income**  
- Lower cost‑to‑income ratio ⇒ higher efficiency.

---

## 4. Liquidity & Structural Ratios

### 4.1 CD Ratio – Credit‑Deposit Ratio

- **CD Ratio = Total advances / Total deposits**  
- Shows what portion of deposits is deployed as loans.  
- Too high ⇒ liquidity risk; too low ⇒ under‑utilisation of funds.

### 4.2 CASA Ratio

- **CASA Ratio = (Current Account + Savings Account deposits) / Total deposits**  
- Higher CASA ⇒ lower cost of funds (since CA/SA rates are low or zero).

### 4.3 Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR / CRAR)

- **CAR = Regulatory capital / Risk‑Weighted Assets**  
- Higher CAR ⇒ better loss‑absorbing capacity.  
- Minimum CAR is prescribed under Basel norms and RBI regulations.

---

## 5. Account & KYC‑Related Terms

### 5.1 KYC – Know Your Customer

- Mandatory process by which banks verify customer’s **identity, address and beneficial ownership**.  
- Requires documents like identity proof, address proof, photograph.

### 5.2 AML – Anti‑Money Laundering

- Laws and procedures to prevent **illicit funds** being introduced into the financial system and made to appear legitimate.  
- Banks file suspicious transaction reports (STRs), cash transaction reports (CTRs), etc.

### 5.3 CFT – Combating Financing of Terrorism

- Framework to detect and prevent financing of terrorism – often mentioned with AML as AML/CFT.

### 5.4 FATF – Financial Action Task Force

- International body that sets global **AML/CFT standards**.  
- India is a member of FATF.

## 6. Payment System Terms

### 6.1 RTGS – Real Time Gross Settlement

- Large‑value funds transfer system operated by RBI;  
  transactions are settled individually in real time.

### 6.2 NEFT – National Electronic Funds Transfer

- Retail funds transfer system using deferred net settlement;  
  account‑to‑account transfer between NEFT‑enabled bank branches.

### 6.3 IMPS – Immediate Payment Service

- 24×7 instant funds transfer service operated by NPCI.  
- Can be used via mobile banking, internet banking, ATMs, UPI front‑ends, etc.

### 6.4 UPI – Unified Payments Interface

- NPCI’s real‑time mobile‑based payment system using VPAs, mobile numbers, QR, etc.

### 6.5 NACH – National Automated Clearing House

- NPCI’s electronic clearing system for **bulk, repetitive transactions** – salaries, subsidies, utility bills, etc.

---

## 7. Basic Balance Sheet Terms

### 7.1 Assets (for a Bank)

- Loans and advances, investments, balances with RBI and other banks, fixed assets, etc.

### 7.2 Liabilities

- Deposits (savings, current, term), borrowings, other liabilities, equity and reserves.

### 7.3 Off‑Balance Sheet Items

- Guarantees, letters of credit (LCs), derivatives contracts, etc.  
- Not shown as direct assets/liabilities but their **credit‑equivalent exposure** is included in capital adequacy calculations.

---

## 8. Other Important Acronyms

- **SLR:** Statutory Liquidity Ratio – % of NDTL in liquid assets.  
- **CRR:** Cash Reserve Ratio – % of NDTL as cash with RBI.  
- **NDTL:** Net Demand and Time Liabilities – broadly, deposits plus certain borrowings (net of some items).  
- **ALM:** Asset–Liability Management – managing interest rate and maturity mismatches between assets and liabilities.  
- **PSL:** Priority Sector Lending – mandatory lending to specified sectors as per RBI.  
- **PCA:** Prompt Corrective Action – RBI’s framework for supervising and correcting weak banks.
