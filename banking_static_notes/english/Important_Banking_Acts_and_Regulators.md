# Important Banking Acts and Regulators – Static Notes (English)

This file summarises key **banking‑related Acts and regulators** in a short, exam‑oriented way.  
For detailed study (RBI, SARFAESI, IBC, etc.) refer to the respective static files; here we keep **1–2 line summaries**.

---

## 1. Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 (RBI Act, 1934)

- **Purpose:**  
  - To establish India’s **central bank (RBI)** and define its powers, structure and functions.  
- **Key Points:**
  - Legal base for RBI’s establishment (1935).  
  - Governs currency issue, monetary policy powers and central banking functions.

**Regulator:** Reserve Bank of India (RBI) – central bank / monetary authority.

---

## 2. Banking Regulation Act, 1949

- **Purpose:**  
  - To **regulate and supervise commercial banks** in India.  
- **Key Points:**
  - Licensing of banks, branching, capital and reserves, management, amalgamation / moratorium, etc.  
  - Defines “banking business” and lays down rules and restrictions.

**Regulator:** RBI – regulates banks under this Act.

---

## 3. Negotiable Instruments Act (NI Act)

- **Basic Idea:**  
  - Governs **cheques, bills of exchange, promissory notes** and other negotiable instruments.  
- **Exam Angle:**
  - Provisions relating to dishonour of cheque / cheque bounce.  
  - Definitions of “negotiable instrument”, endorsement, holder in due course, etc.

**Authority:**  
- Courts and enforcement agencies implement the law;  
- RBI and banks follow NI Act in day‑to‑day banking practice.

---

## 4. DICGC Act – Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation

- **Purpose:**  
  - To provide **deposit insurance** to bank depositors.  
- **Key Points:**
  - DICGC is a **wholly owned subsidiary of RBI**.  
  - Deposits in eligible banks (commercial banks, RRBs, many cooperative banks) are insured up to a **maximum limit per depositor per bank**.  
  - The current insurance limit must always be checked from the latest notification.

**Regulator / Entity:** DICGC (under RBI).

---

## 5. NABARD Act, 1981

- **Purpose:**  
  - To establish **National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD)**.  
- **Key Points:**
  - NABARD is the **apex development financial institution** for rural credit and rural development.  
  - Provides refinance and development support to cooperative banks, RRBs and others.

**Institution:** NABARD – rural credit apex DFI.

---

## 6. National Housing Bank Act, 1987 (NHB Act, 1987)

- **Purpose:**  
  - To establish **National Housing Bank (NHB)** as an apex housing finance institution.  
- **Key Points:**
  - Responsible for promoting, developing and (as per earlier framework) regulating housing finance institutions.  
  - Supports affordable housing and housing finance system development.

**Institution:** NHB – apex institution for housing finance (ownership and regulatory role have changed over time; check current status).

---

## 7. SARFAESI Act – Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest

- **Purpose:**  
  - To empower secured creditors (banks / FIs) to **enforce security interests**  
    without court intervention, and to provide a framework for Asset Reconstruction Companies (ARCs).  
- **Key Points:**
  - In case of NPAs, banks can take possession of and sell secured assets, following due procedure.  
  - Aims to speed up NPA resolution and loan recovery.

**Regulator / Authority:**  
- RBI – regulation of ARCs etc.  
- DRTs / Courts – for appeals and adjudication.

(See `SARFAESI_DRT_IBC_Overview.md` for more detail.)

---

## 8. Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007 (PSS Act, 2007)

- **Purpose:**  
  - To regulate, supervise and develop **payment and settlement systems** in India.  
- **Key Points:**
  - Provides legal framework for NEFT, RTGS, card networks, clearing systems, wallets, etc.  
  - Governs authorisation and operation of payment systems; gives RBI oversight powers.

**Regulator:** RBI – regulator and overseer of payment and settlement systems.

---

## 9. Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), 1999

- **Purpose:**  
  - To regulate and manage **foreign exchange transactions** in India,  
    including current and capital account transactions.  
- **Key Points:**
  - Replaced the earlier FERA (which had a more criminal/strict approach).  
  - FEMA is more **management‑oriented** – aims to facilitate legitimate forex transactions while monitoring and regulating them.

**Regulators:** RBI + Central Government  
(Enforcement Directorate handles certain violations).

---

## 10. Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC)

- **Purpose:**  
  - To provide a **single, time‑bound framework** for insolvency and bankruptcy of:
    - companies,  
    - limited liability partnerships,  
    - individuals and partnerships.  
- **Key Points:**
  - Corporate Insolvency Resolution Process (CIRP), liquidation, moratorium.  
  - NCLT / NCLAT as adjudicating authorities.  
  - Involves Insolvency Professionals, Committee of Creditors, resolution plans.  
  - Important tool for large NPAs resolution.

**Regulators / Bodies:**  
- Insolvency and Bankruptcy Board of India (IBBI),  
- NCLT / NCLAT,  
- RBI (from the banking perspective).

(Detailed overview in `SARFAESI_DRT_IBC_Overview.md`.)

---

## 11. SEBI Act, 1992 – Capital Market Regulator

- **Purpose:**  
  - To regulate the securities market – ensure investor protection, fair practices, and market development.  
- **Banking Link:**
  - Banks that raise capital in markets (equity / debt),  
  - run mutual funds, merchant banking, etc.,  
  are subject to SEBI regulations in those activities.

**Regulator:** SEBI – Securities and Exchange Board of India.

## 13. Memory Tricks – Acts & Regulators

**Trick 1 – “R–B–D–N–S–P–F–I–S”**

- **R** – RBI Act → **RBI** (central bank)  
- **B** – Banking Regulation Act → **Banks’ rulebook** (RBI regulates)  
- **D** – DICGC Act → **Deposits** insurance  
- **N** – NABARD/NHB Acts → **Niche** development institutions (rural/housing)  
- **S** – SARFAESI → **Secured assets** enforcement  
- **P** – PSS Act → **Payments** systems  
- **F** – FEMA → **Forex** management  
- **I** – IBC → **Insolvency** and bankruptcy  
- **S** – SEBI Act → **Securities** market

**Trick 2 – “RBI and its ‘Friends’ Acts”**

- RBI Act – central bank creation.  
- Banking Regulation Act – banks’ regulation.  
- DICGC Act – depositor protection.  
- NABARD / NHB Acts – development‑oriented institutions.  
- SARFAESI / IBC – recovery and resolution tools.  
- PSS / FEMA – payments and forex frameworks.  
- SEBI Act – capital market regulation.
