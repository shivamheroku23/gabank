# Important Banking and Financial Committees – Static Notes (English)

This file summarises important **banking and financial sector committees** in an exam‑oriented way –  
committee name, year/phase, chairman, subject and 2–3 key recommendations.

---

## 1. Narasimham Committee I (1991) – Financial Sector Reforms

- **Chairman:** M. Narasimham  
- **Year / Phase:** 1991 (post Balance of Payments crisis)  
- **Subject:** Financial sector and banking reforms.

**Key Recommendations (Concept Level):**

- Rationalise directed credit programmes.  
- Gradually reduce SLR and CRR.  
- Deregulate interest rates (make them more market‑linked).  
- Introduce prudential norms – income recognition, asset classification, provisioning, capital adequacy.  
- Strengthen banking supervision.

> Exam line: *“Narasimham Committee I (1991) – base blueprint for India’s banking reforms.”*

---

## 2. Narasimham Committee II (1998) – Banking Sector Reforms

- **Chairman:** M. Narasimham  
- **Year:** 1998  
- **Subject:** Second phase of banking sector reforms.

**Key Recommendations:**

- **Consolidation of banks** – create strong, competitive banks.  
- Strengthen capital adequacy standards.  
- Introduce Asset Reconstruction Companies (ARCs) for bad loans resolution.  
- Dilute Government stake in PSBs to a lower threshold.  
- Improve PSB autonomy and governance.

> Exam angle: *“Narasimham II – bank consolidation, capital adequacy, NPA resolution, PSB autonomy.”*

---

## 3. Rangarajan Committees – Computerisation & Financial Inclusion

### 3.1 Rangarajan Committee on Computerisation in Banks

- **Chairman:** Dr. C. Rangarajan  
- **Subject:** Computerisation / technology adoption in banking.  
- **Key Points:**
  - Roadmap for branch automation, core banking, and electronic payments.  
  - Laid the base for the later digital banking revolution.

### 3.2 Rangarajan Committee on Financial Inclusion

- **Subject:** Policy and strategy for financial inclusion.  
- **Key Recommendations:**
  - Steps to provide basic banking services to all,  
  - BC / BF model, low‑cost accounts, financial literacy.

(Exam questions are typically like: “Rangarajan Committee was related to what?”)

---

## 4. Nachiket Mor Committee (2013–14) – Financial Inclusion

- **Full Name:** Committee on Comprehensive Financial Services for Small Businesses and Low Income Households  
- **Chairman:** Dr. Nachiket Mor  
- **Subject:** Comprehensive financial services for small businesses and low‑income households.

**Key Recommendations:**

- Concept of “Universal Electronic Bank Account” for all citizens.  
- Idea of differentiated banking licences –  
  later became the base for **Payments Banks and Small Finance Banks**.  
- Emphasis on access, quality and reliability of financial services for all.

> Exam line: *“Nachiket Mor Committee – financial inclusion & differentiated banks (Payments Banks, SFBs).”*

---

## 5. Urjit Patel Committee – Monetary Policy Framework

- **Chairman:** Dr. Urjit Patel  
- **Subject:** Monetary policy framework and inflation targeting.

**Key Recommendations:**

- Adopt an inflation‑targeting framework (CPI‑based target).  
- Set up a **Monetary Policy Committee (MPC)** with RBI and Government‑nominated members to decide the policy rate.  
- Fix a clear inflation target and an accountability framework.

> Exam: *“Urjit Patel Committee – recommended inflation targeting and MPC framework.”*

---

## 6. Malegam Committee – Microfinance (NBFC‑MFIs)

- **Chairman:** Y. H. Malegam  
- **Subject:** Issues in the microfinance sector, especially NBFC‑MFIs.

**Key Points:**

- Examined MFI lending practices, interest rates and borrower over‑indebtedness.  
- Recommended a separate regulatory framework for NBFC‑MFIs:
  - caps on margins / interest rates,  
  - borrower‑level exposure norms,  
  - transparency and fair practices.

> Exam line: *“Malegam Committee – regulation of microfinance / NBFC‑MFIs and borrower protection.”*

---

## 7. Financial Inclusion‑Related Committees – Recap

Beyond Nachiket Mor:

- **Rangarajan Committee on Financial Inclusion** –  
  recommended specific targets and action plans for inclusion.  

- Various **RBI working groups** on BC model, small/payments banks, microfinance, etc. –  
  but in exams, the key names are usually **Nachiket Mor** and **Rangarajan**.
