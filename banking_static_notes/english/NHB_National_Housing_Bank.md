# National Housing Bank (NHB) – Static Notes (English)

## 1. Basic Profile

- **Full Name:** National Housing Bank (NHB)  
- **Type:** Apex Institution for Housing Finance  
- **Head Office:** New Delhi  
- **Establishment:** 9 July 1988 (under the NHB Act, 1987)  
- **Main Focus:**  
  - Development and regulation of the **housing finance system** in India.  
- **Ownership:**  
  - Initially promoted by RBI; later ownership pattern changed –  
    always check latest shareholding (Government / RBI / others) for the exam year.  
- **Official Website:** https://nhb.org.in  

---

## 2. Background & Establishment

- In the 1980s, there was a need for a dedicated apex institution for the housing sector.  
- Housing Finance Companies (HFCs) and housing projects required **organised, long‑term funding**.  
- The **National Housing Bank Act, 1987** was passed to create such an institution.  
- NHB started operations on **9 July 1988** as the **apex institution for housing finance**.  

Key roles:

- Provide refinance to HFCs/banks,  
- Support housing finance policies and schemes,  
- Develop new products/instruments for housing finance.

---

## 3. Objectives of NHB

- **Development and promotion of the housing finance system:**  
  - Encourage and support housing finance institutions (HFCs, banks).  

- **Regulation and supervision of housing finance institutions:**  
  - Ensure a sound and stable housing finance system.  

- **Promotion of affordable housing:**  
  - Especially for low and middle income groups.  

- **Resource mobilisation for housing:**  
  - Provide long‑term funds via refinance, bonds and other capital market instruments.

---

## 4. Major Functions of NHB (Concept Level)

### 4.1 Refinance to Housing Finance Institutions

- Provides **refinance** to banks and HFCs:  
  - so they can extend housing loans to individuals,  
  - and finance housing projects / bulk lending.

### 4.2 Regulation & Supervision of HFCs

- Plays a role in regulating and supervising Housing Finance Companies (HFCs)  
  (the exact allocation of powers between RBI and NHB has changed – refer to current framework).  

Objectives:

- Ensure financial soundness of HFCs,  
- Promote fair practices and customer protection.

### 4.3 Housing Sector Development & Policy Support

- Supports affordable housing, slum redevelopment, rural and semi‑urban housing through schemes and policies.  
- Provides inputs to Government on housing policy and interest subsidy schemes (e.g., under PMAY/CLSS).  
- Conducts research and publishes reports on housing markets, prices, affordability, demand–supply gaps.

### 4.4 Development of New Instruments

- Works on capital market instruments for housing finance,  
  - such as securitisation structures, mortgage‑backed securities.  
- Issues guidelines and promotes best practices for banks and HFCs.

---

## 5. NHB’s Role – Exam Point of View

(Note: Specific subsidy schemes, limits and eligibility criteria change from time to time – always update them from current sources.)

- Acts as a central institution in the housing finance ecosystem:  
  - connects banks, HFCs, developers, Government schemes and borrowers.  

- Supports **affordable housing** and **priority sector housing loans**.  

- Provides data, research and policy inputs on housing and housing finance.
