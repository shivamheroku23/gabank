## 2. Historical Background – Simplified Timeline

Since SBI’s history is complex, use this simplified timeline:

1. **Presidency Banks Era:**  
   - Bank of Calcutta (later Bank of Bengal), Bank of Bombay, Bank of Madras – early 19th century.  
   - These were key British‑era banks handling Government business and trade.

2. **Imperial Bank of India (1921):**  
   - Formed by merging the three Presidency Banks.  
   - Served as a major commercial bank and banker to Government.

3. **Creation of SBI (1955):**  
   - Government of India acquired the Imperial Bank of India.  
   - Under the **State Bank of India Act, 1955**, it was reconstituted as **State Bank of India**.  
   - SBI was given a mandate to expand banking in rural and semi‑urban areas.

4. **Associate Banks & Mergers:**  
   - SBI had several associates – State Bank of Bikaner & Jaipur, Mysore, Patiala, Travancore, Hyderabad, etc.  
   - Over time, these associate banks were **merged into SBI** in phased steps.  
   - Result: SBI emerged as a single, very large PSB with a strong all‑India presence.

> Exam line: **“SBI = successor of the Imperial Bank of India under SBI Act, 1955.”**

## 3. Objectives & Role of SBI

- **Commercial Banking:**  
  - Handles deposits, loans, remittances, and wide‑ranging retail and corporate banking.

- **Financial Inclusion & Government Schemes:**  
  - Major role in PMJDY, DBT, pensions, social security schemes, etc.

- **Priority Sector Lending:**  
  - Agriculture, MSME, weaker sections, housing, education, etc.

- **International Presence:**  
  - Overseas branches/subsidiaries providing NRI services, trade finance, remittances.

- **Government Business:**  
  - Manages Government receipts/payments, tax collections, pensions, etc.

## 4. Main Business Areas of SBI

### 4.1 Retail Banking

- Savings / current accounts, FDs, RDs.  
- Home loans, personal loans, vehicle loans, education loans.  
- Debit and credit cards, internet banking, mobile banking.

### 4.2 Corporate & SME Banking

- Working capital limits, term loans, project finance.  
- Trade finance – letters of credit, guarantees, bill discounting.  
- SME‑specific lending products.

### 4.3 Rural & Agricultural Banking

- Kisan Credit Cards (KCC), crop loans.  
- Loans for farm mechanisation, dairy, poultry, fisheries, etc.  
- Rural and semi‑urban branches serving farmers, SHGs, rural enterprises.

### 4.4 International Banking

- Overseas branches and offices.  
- NRI deposits and remittance services.  
- Trade finance and forex services.

### 4.5 Digital Banking & Technology

- Net banking, mobile apps, UPI services, YONO app.  
- Extensive ATM and POS network.
