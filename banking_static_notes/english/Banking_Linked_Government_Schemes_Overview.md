# Banking‑Linked Government Schemes – Overview (English)

This file gives a **static overview of important Government schemes** that have a **direct link with banking / financial inclusion**.  
(Exact amounts, interest rates, coverage and latest changes should always be updated from current affairs and official sources.)

---

## 1. PMJDY – Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana

- **Objective:**  
  - Provide at least one **basic bank account to every household**,  
  - promote financial inclusion (banking, remittance, credit, insurance, pension).  

- **Key Features (concept level):**
  - Basic Savings Bank Deposit Account (BSBDA) – zero / low minimum balance.  
  - RuPay debit card with accidental insurance cover (amount to be checked as per exam year).  
  - Platform for DBT (Direct Benefit Transfer) – Government subsidies and welfare payments.  
  - Overdraft facility subject to conditions.

> PMJDY = India’s **flagship financial inclusion programme** – “Every household to have a bank account”.

---

## 2. PMJJBY – Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana

- **Type:** Life insurance scheme.  
- **Target Group:** Savings bank account holders aged 18–50 years.  
- **Coverage:** Fixed sum assured on death (natural / accidental) – exact amount as per current norms.  
- **Premium:** Low annual premium auto‑debited from the bank account.

> PMJJBY = “Low‑cost life insurance through bank accounts”.

---

## 3. PMSBY – Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana

- **Type:** Accidental insurance scheme.  
- **Target Group:** Savings bank account holders aged 18–70 years.  
- **Coverage:** Fixed insurance cover for accidental death / disability.  
- **Premium:** Very low annual premium, auto‑debited from bank account.

> PMSBY = “Low‑cost accident insurance via bank account”.

---

## 4. APY – Atal Pension Yojana

- **Type:** Pension scheme for unorganised sector workers.  
- **Objective:**  
  - Ensure a **guaranteed minimum pension** in old age (pre‑defined pension slabs).  

- **Target Group:** Individuals aged 18–40 years having a bank account.  

- **Key Features:**
  - Monthly contributions based on entry age and chosen pension slab.  
  - Government co‑contribution for eligible categories (as per policy, time‑bound).

> APY = “Pension for unorganised sector, linked to bank accounts via auto‑debit”.

---

## 5. PMMY – Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (MUDRA Loans)

- **MUDRA:** Micro Units Development and Refinance Agency Ltd.  
- **Objective:**  
  - Provide loans to non‑corporate, non‑farm small / micro enterprises.  

- **Loan Categories (concept level):**
  - **Shishu** – smallest ticket loans (up to lower slab).  
  - **Kishore** – mid‑range loans.  
  - **Tarun** – higher‑range loans for micro / small enterprises.  

(Exact upper limits for each category must be checked from latest official data.)

- **Bank Link:**
  - Loans are sanctioned through banks, RRBs, MFIs, NBFCs etc.;  
  - refinancing / guarantee support via MUDRA and other institutions (e.g. NABARD) as per scheme design.

---

## 6. Stand‑Up India Scheme

- **Objective:**  
  - Provide finance for **greenfield enterprises** set up by SC / ST and women entrepreneurs.  

- **Loan Range (concept):**  
  - Term loan + working capital within a specified range (exact limits as per current scheme guidelines).  

- **Bank Link:**
  - Each Scheduled Commercial Bank branch is expected to support at least:
    - 1 SC/ST entrepreneur, and  
    - 1 woman entrepreneur  
    with loans for greenfield enterprises.

> Focus – **new enterprise creation among SC/ST and women** through bank loans.

---

## 7. PM SVANidhi – PM Street Vendor’s AtmaNirbhar Nidhi

- **Objective:**  
  - Provide working capital loans to **urban street vendors** to restart or expand their businesses.  

- **Key Features (concept):**
  - Small initial working capital loan (with scope for enhanced limits on good repayment).  
  - Interest subsidy / incentives for timely repayment.  
  - Additional incentives for digital transactions.

- **Implementation:**  
  - Loans extended by banks, RRBs, cooperative banks, NBFCs, etc.

---

## 8. Other Important Banking‑Linked Schemes (Concept Level)

### 8.1 Kisan Credit Card (KCC)

- Provides farmers with a **flexible credit limit** for crop loans and allied activities.  
- KCC loans count towards **Priority Sector – Agriculture** targets.  
- Issued by commercial banks, RRBs, cooperative banks.

### 8.2 Interest Subvention Schemes

- Interest subvention on short‑term crop loans.  
- Additional subvention for timely repayment – reducing the **effective interest rate** for farmers.

### 8.3 Credit‑Linked Subsidy Schemes for Housing

- Under affordable housing initiatives (e.g. PMAY), home loans are supported with **interest subsidy**:
  - Credit Linked Subsidy Scheme (CLSS) type structures.  

> Note: Scheme names, exact interest rates, limits and eligibility conditions change over time –  
> for exams, always refer to the latest official information (PIB, ministry websites).

## 9. Exam‑Oriented One‑Liners – Banking‑Linked Schemes

1. **PMJDY** – financial inclusion via **basic bank account + RuPay card + DBT platform**.  
2. **PMJJBY** – low‑cost **life insurance scheme** for bank account holders.  
3. **PMSBY** – low‑cost **accident insurance scheme** for bank account holders.  
4. **APY** – pension scheme for unorganised sector workers; contributions auto‑debited from bank accounts.  
5. **PMMY / MUDRA** – micro / small business loans; categories – **Shishu, Kishore, Tarun**.  
6. **Stand‑Up India** – greenfield enterprise loans for **SC/ST and women entrepreneurs**.  
7. **PM SVANidhi** – working capital loans and digital incentives for **urban street vendors**.  
8. **KCC** – flexible credit limit for farmers; core tool for PSL‑Agriculture.
