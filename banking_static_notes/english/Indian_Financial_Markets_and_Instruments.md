# Indian Financial Markets and Instruments – Static Notes (English)

This file gives a simple overview of **Indian financial markets** (money market and capital market)  
and key instruments (T‑Bills, bonds, shares, etc.).  
Use this as the static base whenever you see terms like “G‑Sec yield, CP, CD, IPO” in current affairs.

---

## 1. Financial Markets – Basic Split

Broadly, financial markets are divided into two main parts:

1. **Money Market**  
   - Market for short‑term funds (generally **up to 1 year** maturity).  

2. **Capital Market**  
   - Market for long‑term funds (**more than 1 year** maturity).

Each segment has different instruments and participants.

---

## 2. Money Market – Short‑Term Funds

The money market deals in highly liquid, short‑term instruments.

### 2.1 Treasury Bills (T‑Bills)

- **Issuer:** Government of India.  
- **Maturity:** Short‑term (e.g., 91‑day, 182‑day, 364‑day).  
- **Type:** Zero‑coupon instruments – issued at a discount, redeemed at face value.  
- **Risk:** Sovereign (Government‑backed) – negligible default risk.

### 2.2 Call Money / Notice Money

- **Call Money:**  
  - Overnight (1 day) funds between banks and financial institutions.  
  - **Call money rate** = short‑term inter‑bank interest rate.

- **Notice Money:**  
  - Funds for 2–14 days.

### 2.3 Commercial Paper (CP)

- **Issuer:** Highly rated corporates / financial institutions.  
- **Type:** Unsecured money market instrument, issued at a discount for short term.  
- **Use:** To raise short‑term funds for working capital needs.

### 2.4 Certificate of Deposit (CD)

- **Issuer:** Banks (and some FIs as per RBI rules).  
- **Type:** Negotiable money market instrument, usually large‑value, fixed maturity.  
- **Use:** Tool for banks to raise short‑term bulk deposits.

---

## 3. Capital Market – Long‑Term Funds

The capital market deals in **shares, debentures, bonds, etc.**

### 3.1 Primary Market

- Where **new securities are issued**:
  - IPO (Initial Public Offer),  
  - FPO (Follow‑on Public Offer),  
  - Rights issues, private placements, etc.  
- Companies raise **fresh capital** here.

### 3.2 Secondary Market

- Where existing securities are **traded among investors**.  
- Stock exchanges (e.g. **NSE, BSE**) are key secondary markets for shares and listed debt.

---

## 4. Equity Shares & Debt Instruments – Basic Definitions

### 4.1 Equity Shares

- Represent an **ownership stake** in a company.  
- Equity shareholders:
  - typically have voting rights,  
  - receive dividends (if declared),  
  - have residual claim on assets after all creditors.  
- High risk, potentially high return.

### 4.2 Debentures / Bonds

- Debt instruments issued by companies or Government:  
  - pay regular interest (coupon),  
  - repay principal at maturity.  

Types include:

- Government bonds (G‑Secs),  
- Corporate bonds / debentures,  
- Tax‑free bonds, etc.

### 4.3 Government Securities (G‑Secs)

- Long‑term Government of India bonds (maturity > 1 year).  
- Issued via auctions conducted by RBI.  
- Movements in G‑Sec yields / prices frequently appear in financial news.

---

## 5. Regulators of Indian Financial Markets

- **RBI (Reserve Bank of India)**  
  - Regulator of the money market, Government securities market, foreign exchange market and payment systems.  

- **SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India)**  
  - Regulator of capital markets – stock exchanges, listed companies, mutual funds, intermediaries.  

- **IRDAI (Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India)**  
  - Regulator of the insurance sector.  

- **PFRDA (Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority)**  
  - Regulator of the pension sector (e.g., NPS).

Banks:

- Are active participants in the money market and G‑Secs market,  
- Also involved in capital markets (merchant banking, mutual funds, etc.).

---

## 6. Mutual Funds – Short Concept

- A mutual fund pools money from investors and invests it in:
  - equity, debt, money market instruments, etc., depending on the scheme objective.  
- **Regulator:** SEBI.  
- Main types (basic):
  - Equity funds,  
  - Debt funds,  
  - Hybrid funds,  
  - Liquid / money market funds, etc.

Banks:

- Act as distributors of mutual fund schemes,  
- Some mutual funds are sponsored by banking groups.

---

## 7. Derivatives – Very Basic View

- **Derivative:** A financial instrument whose value is derived from an underlying asset (share, index, commodity, currency, interest rate).  
- Types (concept level):
  - Forwards, futures, options, swaps.  
- Uses:
  - Hedging, speculation, arbitrage.

Banks participate in derivatives for risk management and trading (as per regulatory norms).

---

## 8. Forex Market – Concept Snapshot

- The foreign exchange (forex) market is where different currencies are traded.  
- RBI manages and monitors forex markets and external sector stability under FEMA.  
- Banks act as **authorised dealers** for forex transactions:
  - trade (import/export),  
  - remittances,  
  - travel,  
  - investments, etc.

---

## 9. Exam One‑Liners – Financial Markets

1. Money market – market for **short‑term funds (≤ 1 year)**; main instruments include T‑Bills, call/notice money, CP, CD.  
2. Capital market – market for **long‑term funds (> 1 year)**; instruments include equity, debentures, bonds.  
3. T‑Bills – short‑term Government securities, zero‑coupon, issued at discount and redeemed at face value.  
4. Commercial Paper – short‑term unsecured debt issued by corporates/FIs.  
5. Certificate of Deposit – negotiable short‑term deposit instrument issued by banks.  
6. Primary market – **new issues** (IPO, FPO, rights, etc.); secondary market – trading of existing securities (stock exchanges).  
7. SEBI – capital market regulator; RBI – money market and forex market regulator.  
8. Mutual funds – pooled investment vehicles; regulator – **SEBI**.

---

## 10. Memory Trick – “M–C–R–S”

- **M – Money Market** – short term, T‑Bills, CP, CD.  
- **C – Capital Market** – long term, equity and bonds.  
- **R – RBI** – regulator for money, forex, G‑Secs.  
- **S – SEBI** – regulator for securities (shares, mutual funds, etc.).
