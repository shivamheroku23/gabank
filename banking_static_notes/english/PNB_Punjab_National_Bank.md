- **Full Name:** Punjab National Bank (PNB)

- **Special Identity:**

## 2. Historical Background – PNB

- **1894:**  
  - Established in Lahore (now in Pakistan) by Indian promoters as a **Swadeshi bank**.  
  - Aim – to provide an Indian‑owned bank for Indian businesses and the public.  

- **Pre‑Independence:**  
  - Served trade, business and individuals in North India.  

- **Post‑Independence:**  
  - Operations shifted to India after partition;  
  - Expanded across various Indian states.

- **Bank Nationalisation – 1969:**  
  - PNB was among the **14 major commercial banks nationalised** in 1969.  
  - Became a Public Sector Bank (PSB).

- **Recent Consolidation (High Level):**  
  - In the recent PSB consolidation phase, PNB merged with other PSBs (exam year‑specific details – names and dates – should be taken from current affairs).

> Exam line: **“PNB – Swadeshi bank founded in Lahore in 1894; nationalised in 1969.”**

## 3. Role & Business Areas of PNB

- **Retail Banking:**  
  - Savings and current accounts, FDs, RDs,  
  - Home loans, personal loans, vehicle loans, education loans.

- **Corporate & MSME Banking:**  
  - Working capital finance, term loans, trade finance (LCs, guarantees).  
  - MSME and small business lending.

- **Agriculture & Rural Banking:**  
  - Kisan Credit Cards (KCC), crop loans, allied activities (dairy, poultry, fisheries) finance.  

- **Government Business:**  
  - Implementation of Government schemes, pension payments, DBT, subsidy disbursement.  

- **Digital Banking:**  
  - Internet banking, mobile banking, debit/credit cards, digital products.
