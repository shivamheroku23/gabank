# SIDBI – Small Industries Development Bank of India (English)

## 1. Basic Profile

- **Full Name:** Small Industries Development Bank of India (SIDBI)  
- **Type:** Apex Development Financial Institution for MSMEs / Small Industries  
- **Head Office:** Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh  
- **Establishment:** 2 April 1990 (under the SIDBI Act, 1989)  
- **Main Focus:**  
  - Finance and development support for MSMEs (Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises), small industries, service sector units and start‑ups.  
- **Ownership:**  
  - Development financial institution under Government of India ecosystem  
  - (exact shareholding to be checked as per latest data).  
- **Official Website:** https://www.sidbi.in  

---

## 2. Background & Establishment

- Earlier, small industries finance was largely handled by **IDBI** and other institutions.  
- A need was felt for a dedicated apex institution for the MSME / small industries sector.  
- The **SIDBI Act, 1989** was enacted.  
- SIDBI started operations on **2 April 1990**.  

Over time, SIDBI has grown in roles including:

- refinance,  
- direct lending,  
- venture capital / risk capital,  
- credit guarantee support,  
- and MSME ecosystem development.

> Exam line: **“SIDBI = apex development financial institution for the MSME sector.”**

## 3. Objectives of SIDBI

- Ensure **adequate and timely credit** to MSMEs.  
- Design MSME‑friendly financial products (term loans, working capital, etc.).  
- Support competitiveness, technology upgradation and modernisation in MSMEs.  
- Promote employment generation, entrepreneurship and exports.  
- Improve the overall MSME ecosystem – infrastructure, clusters, innovation, start‑ups.

---

## 4. Major Functions of SIDBI

### 4.1 Refinance to Banks / FIs

- Provides **refinance** to:
  - commercial banks,  
  - small finance banks,  
  - NBFCs,  
  - microfinance institutions (MFIs),  

so that they can lend to MSMEs.

### 4.2 Direct Finance to MSMEs

- Gives direct term loans, project finance and working capital assistance to MSME units (as per policy).  
- Supports green / energy‑efficient projects, technology upgradation, cluster infrastructure, etc.

### 4.3 Promotional & Development Activities

- Conducts cluster development programmes (for MSME / industrial clusters).  
- Runs entrepreneurship development and skill development programmes.  
- Supports the start‑up ecosystem, including equity / quasi‑equity support via funds.

### 4.4 Risk Capital / Venture Support

- Provides risk capital / venture capital support to start‑ups and innovation‑driven MSMEs through various funds and schemes.

### 4.5 Policy Advocacy & Coordination

- Identifies issues faced by MSMEs and recommends policy measures to Government and regulators.  
- Works as a coordinating body among banks, industry associations and Government departments.
