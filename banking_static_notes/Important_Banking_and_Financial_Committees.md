# Important Banking and Financial Committees – Static Notes

Is file me banking & finance se related **important committees** ko exam‑oriented tareeke se summarise kiya gaya hai:  
committee ka naam, year/phase, chairman, main subject, 2–3 important recommendations.

---

## 1. Narasimham Committee I (1991) – Financial Sector Reforms

- **Chairman:** M. Narasimham  
- **Year / Phase:** 1991 (post‑BoP crisis)  
- **Subject:** Financial sector & banking reforms.

**Key Recommendations (concept level):**

- Directed credit programmes ko rationalise karna.  
- SLR, CRR ko gradually reduce karna.  
- Interest rate deregulation (market linked).  
- Prudential norms introduce karna – income recognition, asset classification, provisioning, capital adequacy etc.  
- Stronger banking supervision framework.

> Exam line: *“Narasimham Committee I – 1991 – India ke banking reforms ka base blueprint.”*

---

## 2. Narasimham Committee II (1998) – Banking Sector Reforms

- **Chairman:** M. Narasimham  
- **Year:** 1998  
- **Subject:** Banking sector reforms second phase.

**Key Recommendations:**

- Consolidation of banks – strong, competitive banks create karna.  
- Capital adequacy standards ko strengthen karna.  
- Asset Reconstruction Companies (ARCs) ka concept; bad loans resolution.  
- Dilution of Govt stake in PSBs (to certain %).  
- Autonomy and governance reforms in PSBs.

> Exam angle: “Narasimham II – bank consolidation, capital adequacy, NPAs resolution, PSB autonomy.”

---

## 3. Rangarajan Committees – Computerisation & Financial Inclusion

### 3.1 Rangarajan Committee on Computerisation in Banks

- **Chairman:** Dr. C. Rangarajan  
- **Subject:** Banking sector me computerisation / technology adoption.  
- **Key Points:**
  - Branch automation, core banking, electronic payments system ke liye roadmap.  
  - Later digital banking revolution ka base.

### 3.2 Rangarajan Committee on Financial Inclusion

- **Subject:** Financial inclusion policy & strategy.  
- **Key Recommendations:**
  - Basic banking services sabko provide karne ke steps.  
  - BC / BF model, low‑cost accounts, financial literacy.

(Exam me mostly “Rangarajan committee kis se related?” type conceptual question aata hai.)

---

## 4. Nachiket Mor Committee (2013–14) – Financial Inclusion

- **Full Name:** Committee on Comprehensive Financial Services for Small Businesses and Low Income Households.  
- **Chairman:** Dr. Nachiket Mor  
- **Subject:** Financial inclusion & small customers ke liye comprehensive financial services.

**Key Recommendations:**

- “Universal Electronic Bank Account” concept.  
- Differentiated banking licences ka idea –  
  - later **Payments Banks & Small Finance Banks** ke creation ka base.  
- Focus on access, ubiquity, and reliability of financial services.

> Exam line: “Nachiket Mor Committee – financial inclusion & differentiated banks (Payments Banks, SFBs).”

---

## 5. Urjit Patel Committee – Monetary Policy Framework

- **Chairman:** Dr. Urjit Patel  
- **Subject:** Monetary policy framework, inflation targeting.

**Key Recommendations:**

- Inflation targeting framework adopt karna (CPI based target).  
- Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) banane ka proposal –  
  - jahan RBI + Govt nominees milkar policy rate decide karein.  
- Clear inflation target + accountability structure.

> Exam: “Urjit Patel Committee – inflation targeting & MPC ke liye recommend.”

---

## 6. Malegam Committee – Microfinance (NBFC‑MFIs)

- **Chairman:** Y. H. Malegam  
- **Subject:** Microfinance sector me issues (particularly NBFC‑MFIs).

**Key Points:**

- MFI lending practices, interest rates, client over‑indebtedness par focus.  
- NBFC‑MFIs ke liye separate regulatory framework suggestions:  
  - caps on margins / interest rates,  
  - borrower level exposure norms,  
  - transparency & fair practices.  

> Exam line: “Malegam Committee – microfinance / NBFC‑MFIs ke regulation se related.”
