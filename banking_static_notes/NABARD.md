# NABARD – National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development

## 1. Basic Profile

- **Full Name:** National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD)  
- **Type:** Apex Development Financial Institution for Agriculture & Rural Development  
- **HQ:** Mumbai, Maharashtra  
- **Establishment:** 12 July 1982 (NABARD Act, 1981)  
- **Main Focus:**  
  - Agriculture, rural development, cottage & village industries, rural infrastructure  
- **Ownership:**  
  - Development financial institution under Government of India (shareholding pattern exam year ke hisaab se check karein).  
- **Official Website:** https://www.nabard.org  

---

## 2. Background & Establishment Timeline

- Pre‑NABARD era me agriculture & rural credit ka kaam mainly:  
  - RBI ka Agriculture Credit Department,  
  - Agricultural Refinance and Development Corporation (ARDC) karte the.  
- 1970s me rural credit ko strengthen karne ke liye alag apex institution ki need feel hui.  
- **B. Sivaraman Committee** (Committee to Review Arrangements for Institutional Credit for Agriculture and Rural Development – CRAICARD) ne recommend kiya ki ek integrated apex body banai jaye.  
- Is recommendation ke base par:  
  - **NABARD Act 1981** pass hua,  
  - **12 July 1982** ko NABARD formally establish hua.  
- ARDC aur RBI ke agriculture credit related functions ko NABARD me transfer kar diya gaya.

> Exam yaad rakhne ka point: NABARD = 1982, Act = 1981, HQ = Mumbai.

## 3. Objectives of NABARD

NABARD ka main objective:

- Agriculture aur rural sector ke liye **integrated credit support** dena.  
- Rural areas me production, productivity, income aur employment ko badhana.  
- Rural infrastructure (roads, irrigation, markets, godowns, etc.) ka development.  
- Rural financial institutions (RRBs, cooperative banks, etc.) ko strengthen karna.  
- Financial inclusion aur sustainable rural livelihoods promote karna.

Exam point style:

- “NABARD – apex institution for policy, planning and operations in credit for agriculture and other economic activities in rural areas.”

## 4. Major Functions of NABARD

### 4.1 Refinancing Function

- NABARD directly farmers ko loan nahi deta (normally);  
  - ye **RRBs, Cooperative Banks, Commercial Banks, State Land Development Banks** ko refinance deta hai.  
- Ye institutions farmers, SHGs, JLGs, rural entrepreneurs ko loans dete hain;  
  - NABARD unko medium/long term refinance provide karta hai.

### 4.2 Credit Planning & Policy

- Rural credit ka **overall policy framework** banane me help karta hai.  
- State Credit Plans, Potential Linked Credit Plans (PLPs) banwane me support.  
- Priority areas jahan rural credit focus hona chahiye, unka identification.

### 4.3 Supervision & Development of Rural Financial Institutions

- RRBs and cooperative banks (rural credit institutions) ki:  
  - inspection,  
  - supervision,  
  - organisational development,  
  - capacity building,  
me important role play karta hai (as per powers given).

### 4.4 Rural Infrastructure Development

- Various specialised funds ke through:  
  - irrigation projects,  
  - rural roads, bridges,  
  - markets, warehouses, cold storages,  
  - rural drinking water, sanitation, etc. ko finance karta hai.  

### 4.5 Promotional & Developmental Role

- Self Help Groups (SHGs), Joint Liability Groups (JLGs) ka promotion.  
- Farmer Producer Organisations (FPOs) ka support.  
- Micro‑finance, financial inclusion, rural entrepreneurship promotion.  
- Skill development, livelihood promotion projects (off‑farm and non‑farm activities).

### 4.6 Research & Training

- Rural development, agriculture finance, micro‑finance par research studies.  
- Training programmes for rural financial institutions ke staff.  
- Rural innovation & best practices ka documentation.

## 5. Important Funds / Initiatives (High‑Level Overview)

(Exact schemes/funds exam year ke hisaab se update hoti rehti hain, isliye yaha concept level pe yaad rakho.)

- **Rural Infrastructure Development Fund (RIDF):**  
  - States ko rural infrastructure projects ke liye long‑term finance.  
  - Fund ka source – usually scheduled commercial banks ke priority sector shortfall ka contribution.  

- **Micro‑finance & SHG–Bank Linkage Programme:**  
  - SHGs ko formal banking system ke sath link karna,  
  - women self‑help groups ka promotion.  

- **Watershed Development, Tribal Development, Farmer clubs, etc.:**  
  - Area development projects, sustainable livelihoods, natural resource management.

## 7. Memory Tricks – NABARD

**Trick 1 – “NA-BA-RD = National Bank for Rural Development”**

- NA = National  
- BA = Bank  
- RD = Rural Development  
Isse pure naam ka sense ban jaata hai – agriculture + rural focus.

**Trick 2 – “82 ka NABARD, 81 ka Act, Mumbai me HQ”**

- 1981 – Act pass  
- 1982 – NABARD start  
- Mumbai – HQ (RBI jaisa hi city, yaad rakhna easy)
