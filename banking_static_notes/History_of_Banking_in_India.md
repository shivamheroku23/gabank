## 1. Ancient & Pre‑Modern Period – Indigenous Banking

### 1.1 Pre‑historic / Ancient Times

- Trade & commerce ke saath‑saath **money lending** bhi develop hua.  
- Goldsmiths, sahukars, seths, mahajans – logon ko udhaar dete the.  
- Promissory notes, hundis jaisi cheezein use hoti thi (informal instruments).

### 1.2 Indigenous Bankers & Hundis

- **Indigenous bankers** – baniya, chettiars, marwari financiers etc.  
- **Hundi** – ek type ka traditional bill of exchange / remittance instrument:
  - Merchant ek city se dusre city me paisa bhejne ke liye hundi use karte the.  
- Formal “bank” word nahi tha, lekin **lending, deposits, remittances** ka kaam hota tha.

> Exam ke liye: Ancient / medieval India me banking ka base – **indigenous bankers + hundis**.

## 2. Modern Banking ka Shuruaat – Presidency Banks

### 2.1 First Joint Stock Bank – Bank of Hindustan

- **Bank of Hindustan (1770)** ko first modern style bank in India maana jata hai.  
- Later ye bank close ho gaya, lekin exam me naam aa sakta hai.

### 2.2 Presidency Banks

British period me 3 major presidency banks banaye gaye:

1. **Bank of Bengal** – 1806  
2. **Bank of Bombay** – 1840  
3. **Bank of Madras** – 1843  

- Ye banks East India Company ke influence me kaam karte the.  
- Currency, govt business, large trade finance ke liye important the.

### 2.3 Imperial Bank of India

- 3 presidency banks ko merge karke **Imperial Bank of India** banaya gaya (1921).  
- Ye ek major commercial bank + banker to Govt / banking system ka kaam karta tha.  
- Later isi Imperial Bank ko takeover karke **State Bank of India (SBI)** banaya gaya.

> Memory line: “Bengal 1806, Bombay 1840, Madras 1843 – teeno milkar Imperial 1921.”

---

## 3. Early Indian Commercial Banks (Swadeshi Phase)

- Late 19th – early 20th century me “Swadeshi” movement ke chalte **Indian owned banks** bane:  
  - Punjab National Bank (PNB – 1894, Lahore),  
  - Bank of Baroda (1908),  
  - Canara Bank, Indian Bank, Central Bank of India, etc.  
- Ye banks Indian entrepreneurs & public ke liye bane, jahan British ownership kam ya nahi thi.

> Exam ke liye: PNB – swadeshi bank, established 1894 in Lahore.

---

## 4. Reserve Bank of India (RBI) – 1935

- Central banking ke liye **Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934** pass hua.  
- **1 April 1935** – RBI ne operations start kiye (Kolkata se).  
- 1937 – HQ Mumbai shift.  
- 1949 – RBI nationalised, 100% Government ownership.  
- RBI ne:
  - currency issue,  
  - monetary policy,  
  - banking regulation,  
  - govt banker,  
  ka role lia.

## 5. Nationalisation Phase – Social Control & PSBs

### 5.1 Social Control (1960s)

- 1960s me realise hua ki private banks ka credit **industry / business houses** tak limited hai;  
  - agriculture, small farmers, rural areas ko proper credit nahi mil raha.  
- 1967–68 me **social control** policy introduce hui –  
  - banks ke top management me govt nominees,  
  - credit planning me govt influence badha.

### 5.2 First Bank Nationalisation – 1969

- **19 July 1969** – 14 major commercial banks (with large deposits) nationalise kiye gaye.  
- Objective:
  - **“class banking se mass banking”** ki taraf shift,  
  - rural & priority sector me credit expansion.

### 5.3 Second Nationalisation – 1980

- 1980 me 6 aur commercial banks nationalise kiye gaye.  
- Nationalisation ke baad:
  - Public Sector Banks (PSBs) banking system par dominant ho gaye,  
  - branch expansion, rural branch licensing norms ke through rural areas me outreach.

> Exam one‑liner: 1969 – 14 banks, 1980 – 6 banks nationalised.

## 6. Regional Rural Banks (RRBs) & Cooperative Strengthening – 1970s

- **RRB Act, 1976** ke under **Regional Rural Banks** establish hue:  
  - objective – small & marginal farmers, rural artisans, landless labourers ko institutional credit.  
- Cooperative banks & land development banks bhi rural credit me important.  
- **NABARD (1982)**: rural credit & development ka apex institution bana (detail alag file me).

---

## 7. Reforms & Liberalisation – 1990s Onwards

### 7.1 Narsimham Committees

- 1991 ke balance of payments crisis ke baad banking sector reforms ke liye **Narsimham Committee** banayi gayi.  
- Recommendations:
  - prudential norms (income recognition, asset classification, provisioning),  
  - capital adequacy norms (Basel),  
  - interest rate deregulation,  
  - entry of new private sector banks,  
  - reduction in SLR / CRR, etc.  
- **Narsimham Committee II** later further reforms ke liye.

### 7.2 New Private Sector Banks

- RBI ne 1990s me new private sector banks ko licenses diye.  
- Modern tech, strong capital, professional management ke saath new players aaye.  
- Competition, efficiency, product innovation me boost.

### 7.3 Other Reforms

- Branch licensing liberalisation,  
- Capital market access (public issues, follow‑on offers),  
- NPAs ke liye legal reforms (SARFAESI Act, DRTs, IBC etc. later timeline me).

---

## 8. Recent Phase – Consolidation, Financial Inclusion & Digital Banking

### 8.1 PSB Consolidation (Mergers)

- Recent saalon me Govt ne **Public Sector Banks ka consolidation** start kiya:  
  - multiple PSBs merge hoke large banks bane.  
  - Objective – strong balance sheet, better capital utilisation, duplication kam.  
- Exam ke liye: kaun sa bank kisme merge hua – ye cheez CA + static me alag se maintain karo.

### 8.2 Financial Inclusion Push

- **No‑frills accounts → Basic Savings Bank Deposit Accounts → PMJDY (Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana)**.  
- DBT (Direct Benefit Transfer) ke through subsidy / welfare payments.  
- Business Correspondents (BCs), micro‑ATMs, banking mitras ke through last mile delivery.

### 8.3 Digital & Payments Revolution

- **NEFT**, **RTGS**, **IMPS** ke baad  
- **UPI (Unified Payments Interface)**, **BHIM**, **QR payments**, **BBPS**, **AePS** etc.  
- Fintechs, payment banks, small finance banks – new entities add hue.  
- RBI as **payment system regulator** – digital payments ko safe & robust banane ka role.

## 9. Important Acts / Committees – Banking History Angle

- **RBI Act, 1934** – central bank creation.  
- **Banking Regulation Act, 1949** – banking regulation framework.  
- **Bank Nationalisation Acts, 1969 & 1980** – PSBs creation.  
- **RRB Act, 1976** – Regional Rural Banks.  
- **NABARD Act, 1981** – rural credit apex body.  
- **NHB Act, 1987** – housing finance apex institution.  
- **Narsimham Committees I & II** – 1990s reforms blueprint.  
- **Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007** – payment system regulation.

## 10. Super Short Timeline – Banking in India

1. **Ancient / Medieval:** Indigenous bankers, hundis.  
2. **18th–19th century:** Bank of Hindustan, Presidency Banks (Bengal, Bombay, Madras).  
3. **1921:** Imperial Bank of India.  
4. **1935:** RBI established (central bank).  
5. **1955:** SBI created from Imperial Bank.  
6. **1969:** 14 banks nationalised.  
7. **1976:** RRBs Act – Regional Rural Banks.  
8. **1980:** 6 banks nationalised.  
9. **1982:** NABARD.  
10. **1988:** NHB.  
11. **1990s:** Narsimham reforms, new private sector banks.  
12. **2000s onward:** Payment systems development, digital banking.  
13. **Recent years:** PSB consolidation, UPI, financial inclusion push.
