## TOPIC 1: World AIDS Day 2025 – “Overcoming disruption, transforming the AIDS response”

### What happened?

- On **1 December 2025**, the world observed **World AIDS Day**.  
- The **global theme for 2025 (WHO/UNAIDS)** was: **“Overcoming disruption, transforming the AIDS response”**.  
- In India, the Union Health Ministry held the national event at **Vigyan Bhawan, New Delhi**, focusing on prevention, testing and treatment.  
- As per **UNAIDS estimates**, India has around **2.4 million (24 lakh) people living with HIV (PLHIV)**, with adult (15–49 years) HIV prevalence of roughly **0.2%**.  
- Due to Anti‑Retroviral Therapy (ART) coverage, AIDS‑related deaths have fallen sharply over the last decade, but risk remains high in key populations and some regions.  

### Why important for bank exams?

- Health outcomes affect **human capital, productivity and long‑term growth**, which are key for macroeconomics questions.  
- Government spending on health, insurance schemes, NHM/NACO etc. often appears in **budget and social sector** questions.  
- Likely one‑liners:  
  - World AIDS Day – **1 December**  
  - 2025 theme – **“Overcoming disruption, transforming the AIDS response”**  
  - India PLHIV (approx) – **2.4 million**

### Memory Trick – “Overcome & Transform”

> Think: **“In 2025, AIDS message = Overcome disruption, Transform the response.”**

Code: **O–T = Overcome – Transform** → AIDS response O.T. (operation theatre).

## TOPIC 2: World Inequality Report 2026 – Global & India picture

### What happened?

- The **World Inequality Lab** released the **World Inequality Report 2026**, analysing global **income and wealth inequality**.  
- Globally:  
  - The **top 10% of people own about 75% of total private wealth**,  
  - The **bottom 50% own only about 2% of wealth**.  
- For India, key numbers:  
  - The **top 1% hold about 40% of national wealth**,  
  - The **top 10% hold about 65% of wealth**,  
  - The **top 10% receive about 58% of national income**, while the bottom 50% receive around 15%.  
- The report stresses that today’s extreme inequality is the result of **policy and institutional choices**, not an automatic outcome of development.

### Why important for bank exams?

- Banking and economic questions on **inclusive growth, financial inclusion, micro‑credit, priority sector lending** are directly linked to inequality.  
- In mains answers you can quote data:  
  - “In India, the top 1% hold about 40% of national wealth.”  
- MCQ‑style facts:  
  - Global top 10% wealth share ≈ **75%**  
  - India top 1% wealth share ≈ **40%**

### Memory Trick – “10–75, 1–40, 10–65”

> Line: **“Globally 10% people hold 75% wealth; in India 1% hold 40% and 10% hold 65% wealth.”**

Number code:  
- World: **10 → 75**  
- India wealth: **1 → 40**, **10 → 65**.

## TOPIC 3: Women, Peace & Security (WPS) Index 2025/26 – India’s position

### What happened?

- The **Women, Peace & Security (WPS) Index 2025/26** was released by the **Georgetown Institute for Women, Peace and Security (GIWPS)** and **PRIO**.  
- The index measures women’s status on three pillars – **Inclusion, Justice, and Security**.  
- Results:  
  - **Denmark** ranks **1st** (score ~0.939),  
  - **India** ranks **131st out of 181 countries** (score ~0.607).  
- This indicates significant gaps in women’s participation, justice and safety in India.

### Why important for bank exams?

- Many schemes – **SHG lending, Jan Dhan, Stand‑Up India, Mudra, women‑centric financial inclusion** – aim to improve women’s economic status.  
- In mains answers on gender, you can quote: **“India ranks 131st in the WPS Index 2025/26.”**  
- MCQs:  
  - WPS Index publisher – **GIWPS + PRIO**  
  - Top country – **Denmark**

### Memory Trick – “D‑1, I‑131”

> Line: **“In WPS, Denmark is No.1, India is 131.”**

Code: **D‑1, I‑131**.

## TOPIC 4: Global AI Vibrancy Index 2024 – India 3rd

### What happened?

- **Stanford HAI (Human‑Centered Artificial Intelligence)** released the **Global AI Vibrancy Tool/Index (2024 data, in the 2025 AI Index)**.  
- Based on AI ecosystem strength (research, talent, economy, policy & governance, infrastructure, responsible AI, public opinion):  
  - **USA – Rank 1**,  
  - **China – Rank 2**,  
  - **India – Rank 3**, with a score of about **21.59**.  
- India moved from **7th to 3rd** in one year, driven by growth in AI research output, startups, talent, and digital infrastructure.

### Why important for bank exams?

- Banking increasingly uses AI for **fraud detection, credit scoring, chatbots, risk modelling**.  
- MCQs:

  - Index developed by – **Stanford University (HAI)**

## TOPIC 5: Ponduru Khadi – GI tag for fine cotton from Andhra Pradesh

### What happened?

- **Ponduru Khadi** is a traditional ultra‑fine **hand‑spun, hand‑woven cotton khadi** from **Srikakulam district, Andhra Pradesh**.  
- It received a **Geographical Indication (GI) tag** in 2025 (registration completed in November 2025, widely reported in December).  
- Key features:  
  - Uses local desi cotton varieties to produce **very fine, durable yarn**.  
  - Follows traditional **charkha spinning and handloom weaving** techniques.  
- The GI registration was granted by the **Geographical Indications Registry, Chennai**, under the Ministry of Commerce & Industry.

### Why important for bank exams?

- GI‑tagged products link to **rural livelihoods, exports, MSME/cottage‑industry finance**.  
- RRBs and PSBs have opportunities to support such clusters with **working capital, credit, and marketing finance**.  
- MCQs:  
  - Ponduru Khadi belongs to which state? → **Andhra Pradesh**  
  - What type of fabric is it? → **Hand‑spun, hand‑woven cotton khadi**

### Memory Trick – “Ponduru = AP’s premium khadi”

> Line: **“Ponduru cloth is Andhra’s ultra‑fine khadi brand.”**

Code: **Ponduru–AP–Khadi** (remember all three together).
