## TOPIC 1: Rajasthan AI-ML Policy 2026 and Green Credit Voucher Initiative

### What happened?

- On 30 December 2025, the Rajasthan Cabinet approved the **Rajasthan Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning (AI-ML) Policy 2026** along with the **Green Credit Voucher Initiative 2025**.  
- The AI-ML Policy aims to make Rajasthan a hub for technology-driven investment, innovation and digital governance, and to make public services faster, more transparent and more citizen-centric.  
- It stresses **ethical AI**, with principles such as transparency, accountability, fairness, privacy protection, bias reduction and secure handling of personal data, plus explainable and auditable AI decisions.  
- The Green Credit Voucher Initiative 2025 supports a **green circular economy** by encouraging environment-friendly actions that can earn structured green credits or vouchers.  

### Why important for Rajasthan exams?

- Names and years are classic one-liners: **AI-ML Policy 2026** and **Green Credit Voucher Initiative 2025**, both cleared by the Rajasthan Cabinet.  
- Policy questions can link these with topics like **Digital Rajasthan, e-governance, climate action and green finance**.  
- Useful in mains answers to show how Rajasthan is combining **AI reforms and environmental incentives** in its development strategy.  

**Memory Trick – “AI green double combo”**

> Think: **“30 December – AI for smart governance, Green Credits for green growth”** – two flagship decisions cleared in one Cabinet sitting.

## TOPIC 3: “Viksit Rajasthan/2047” and Vishwakarma Yuva Udyami Protsahan Yojana

### What happened?

- In August 2025, the Rajasthan Cabinet approved the state’s long-term vision document **“Viksit Rajasthan/2047”** to guide development up to the centenary year of India’s independence.  
- The roadmap targets making Rajasthan a **350-billion-dollar economy by 2030** and roughly a **4.3-trillion-dollar economy by 2047**, with focus on literacy, skills, women and youth empowerment, health, water, housing, urbanisation and green energy.  
- In the same meeting, the Cabinet cleared the **“Vishwakarma Yuva Udyami Protsahan Yojana”**, under which youth aged **18–45 years** can receive loans of up to **₹2 crore** with interest subvention of up to **8%** to start or expand enterprises.  

### Why important for Rajasthan exams?

- Vision documents and flagship youth schemes are favourites in **RAS prelims and mains**.  
- Key points to remember: name **“Viksit Rajasthan/2047”**, targets for **2030 and 2047**, and the youth scheme’s **age band, loan ceiling and interest subsidy**.  
- Useful for essays on **demographic dividend, entrepreneurship, employment and regional growth strategies**.  

**Memory Trick – “Two Vs for future Rajasthan”**

> Link it as: **“Viksit Rajasthan for 2047, Vishwakarma Yuva for vyapar (business)”** – vision plus youth enterprise in one package.

## TOPIC 4: “Heal in Rajasthan Policy 2025” and RUHS Critical Care Block

### What happened?

- On 15 December 2025, Chief Minister **Bhajan Lal Sharma** launched the **“Heal in Rajasthan Policy 2025”** at the RUHS Hospital Complex in Jaipur to mark two years of the state government.  
- The policy aims to promote **medical tourism** and position Rajasthan as a reliable, accessible and affordable destination for medical value travel while strengthening public healthcare across the state.  
- At the same event, a new **Critical Care Block** was inaugurated at RUHS, built at a cost of around **₹20 crore**, with ICU beds, emergency care units, advanced ventilators, modular operation theatres and dialysis facilities.  
- The launch was accompanied by state-wide health camps and blood donation drives to expand access to screening and treatment.  

### Why important for Rajasthan exams?

- Adds a new **sector-specific policy** to Rajasthan’s list – this time focusing on **healthcare and medical tourism**.  
- Facts to note: policy name **“Heal in Rajasthan Policy 2025”**, focus on **medical tourism plus public hospital infrastructure**, and the **Critical Care Block at RUHS, Jaipur**.  
- Useful in mains answers on **health infrastructure, tourism diversification and service-sector growth in Rajasthan**.  

**Memory Trick – “Heal in Rajasthan = Health + Tourism”**

> Remember the phrase: **“Heal in Rajasthan – health system strong, medical tourism along.”**

## TOPIC 5: Recruitment, diagnostics and health campaigns in Rajasthan

### What happened?

- Over the last two years, Rajasthan has filled around **35,000 posts** in the Medical and Health Department and begun recruitment for about **15,000 additional posts**, strengthening doctors, nurses and technical staff.  
- Under the National Health Mission, the state is rolling out a **Hub-and-Spoke diagnostic model** with **42 mother labs, 137 hub labs and 1,333 spokes** to provide more than 100 specialised tests free of cost in government hospitals.  
- Around **132 new treatment packages** have been added under the **Chief Minister’s Ayushman Arogya Yojana**, helping over **35 lakh people** receive cashless medical services.  
- Campaigns such as **FCM injections for pregnant women to tackle anaemia**, state-wide health camps, and **eye-examination programmes with free spectacles and screening for thalassemia and sickle-cell disease** are being implemented.  

### Why important for Rajasthan exams?

- Provides multiple **number-based facts** for one-liners: **35,000 + 15,000 posts**, **42–137–1,333 lab network**, **132 packages** and **35 lakh beneficiaries**.  
- Shows how schemes like **Ayushman Arogya Yojana** and new diagnostics are improving **public health outcomes in Rajasthan**.  
- Good material for mains answers on **human resources in health, preventive healthcare and universal health coverage at state level**.  

**Memory Trick – “35–15 and 42–137–1333”**

> Use the code: **“35–15 staff, 42–137–1333 labs”** – recruitment in tens of thousands and a four-tier diagnostic network for a healthier Rajasthan.

## TOPIC 6: RUHS upgrade to RIMS and Rajasthan Mandapam project

### What happened?

- The Rajasthan Cabinet has approved the upgradation of **Rajasthan University of Health Sciences (RUHS)** in Jaipur into the **Rajasthan Institute of Medical Sciences (RIMS)**, modelled on AIIMS Delhi.  
- RIMS is planned to be the state’s **only postgraduate medical institute**, boosting specialist education, research and advanced clinical services in Rajasthan.  
- The Cabinet also cleared a large infrastructure project to develop **Rajasthan Mandapam convention centre, a Global Capability Centre and an IT Tower** on RIICO land near Jaipur’s B-2 bypass.  
- The convention centre is estimated to cost about **₹3,700 crore** and will have a seating capacity of roughly **7,000–7,500 people**, enabling Rajasthan to host major national and international events.  

### Why important for Rajasthan exams?

- One-liners: **RUHS → RIMS**, **only PG medical institute**, and **Rajasthan Mandapam near Jaipur B-2 bypass**.  
- The Mandapam and IT Tower project is relevant for questions on **investment promotion, MICE tourism (Meetings, Incentives, Conferences, Exhibitions) and the services economy**.

## TOPIC 7: Adani–RRVUNL coal transport dispute and court order

### What happened?

- In July 2025, Jaipur Commercial Court Judge **Dinesh Kumar Gupta** delivered a judgment in a long-running coal transport dispute between **Parsa Kente Collieries Ltd** (an Adani-led joint venture) and **Rajasthan Rajya Vidyut Utpadan Nigam Limited (RRVUNL)**.  
- The court held that the joint venture had wrongfully recovered **over ₹1,400 crore** in road transportation charges for moving coal from a Chhattisgarh mine to Rajasthan, even though the original agreement required rail connectivity, and imposed a fine of **₹50 lakh** on the company.  
- On the same day, the judge was shifted from the commercial court to a district court in Beawar, and later the Rajasthan High Court stayed the operation of his order on a plea by the company.  
- Hearings in the case are continuing, with the matter still under judicial consideration in early 2026.  

### Why important for Rajasthan exams?

- Combines themes of **state public sector undertakings, mining contracts, judicial oversight and accountability** – all key areas for RAS mains.  
- Facts to remember: parties involved (**RRVUNL and an Adani-led JV**), amount of alleged wrongful gain (**over ₹1,400 crore**), and **₹50 lakh** penalty ordered by the commercial court.  
- Useful as a case-study example in answers on **public resources, contract enforcement and transparency in large infrastructure projects**.  

**Memory Trick – “1,400 crore coal case”**

> Recall it as: **“Adani–RRVUNL coal road charges ≈ ₹1,400 crore; court fine ₹50 lakh; order now under High Court stay.”**

## TOPIC 8: NSO’s First Advance Estimates – India’s GDP growth at 7.4% in FY 2025–26

### What happened?

- On 7 January 2026, the **National Statistical Office (NSO)** released the **First Advance Estimates (FAE)** of Gross Domestic Product for the financial year **2025–26 (FY26)**.  
- India’s **real GDP growth** for FY26 is projected at **7.4%**, with real GDP at about **₹201.90 lakh crore**, up from ₹187.97 lakh crore in FY25.  
- **Nominal GDP growth** at current prices is estimated at **8.0%**, taking nominal GDP to around **₹357.14 lakh crore**.  
- Real **Gross Value Added (GVA)** is projected to grow by about **7.3%**, with the **tertiary sector** (services) leading at around **9.1%**, while private consumption, government spending and investment all show solid growth.  

### Why important for Rajasthan exams?

- Rajasthan exams ask national economy questions, especially **growth rates, key numbers and institutions**.  
- One-liners: **NSO**, real GDP growth **7.4%**, nominal GDP growth **8%**, and real GVA growth **7.3%** in FY26.  
- Can be linked with state-level discussions on **fiscal policy, employment and investment climate in Rajasthan**.  

**Memory Trick – “7.4 real, 8 nominal”**

> Remember: **“Real 7.4, nominal 8”** – India’s FY26 growth picture as per NSO’s First Advance Estimates.

## TOPIC 9: Sanjay Malhotra – 26th Governor of the Reserve Bank of India

### What happened?

- The Government of India has appointed **Sanjay Malhotra** as the **26th Governor of the Reserve Bank of India (RBI)**, succeeding **Shaktikanta Das** whose extended term ended in December 2024.  
- Sanjay Malhotra is a **1990-batch IAS officer of the Rajasthan cadre** with over three decades of experience in finance, taxation, power and infrastructure.  
- Before this appointment, he served as **Revenue Secretary** in the Ministry of Finance and earlier as **Secretary, Department of Financial Services**, overseeing banking and insurance reforms.  
- As RBI Governor, he will head India’s **monetary policy, inflation management, financial stability framework and regulation of banks and key financial institutions**.  

### Why important for Rajasthan exams?

- RBI Governor is a **high-frequency current affairs topic** across all competitive exams, including Rajasthan state exams.  
- Key facts: **26th RBI Governor**, name **Sanjay Malhotra**, and his **Rajasthan cadre background** plus earlier role as **Revenue Secretary**.  
- Useful in economy answers connecting **monetary policy, interest rates and financial sector reforms** with exam questions.
