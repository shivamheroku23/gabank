### Topic 1: Rajasthan AI‑ML Policy 2026 & Regional AI Impact Conference – Jaipur

### What Happened?

- On **6 January 2026**, the Government of Rajasthan hosted the **Rajasthan Regional AI Impact Conference 2026** in **Jaipur**.  
- The conference formed part of **Rajasthan DigiFest × TiE Global Summit 2026**, and is one of the **Regional AI Impact Conferences** organised under the **IndiaAI Mission of the Ministry of Electronics & IT (MeitY)**, feeding inputs into the national‑level **India AI Impact Summit 2026 (Bharat Mandapam, New Delhi – 19–20 February 2026)**.  
- At this event, Chief Minister **Bhajan Lal Sharma** formally launched the **“Rajasthan Artificial Intelligence–Machine Learning (AI‑ML) Policy 2026”**.  

- Key features of the policy:  
  - Position Rajasthan as a **technology‑driven hub for investment and innovation**,  
  - Promote **ethical, transparent, and accountable AI** – with focus on **data protection, cyber security, reduction of bias, explainability and accountability**,  
  - Create an **AI Centre of Excellence**, AI cloud infrastructure and secure data‑storage systems.  

- Several state‑level digital platforms were launched alongside:  
  - **National AI Literacy Programme – “AI for All”** – a roughly 4‑hour foundational AI course with a Government of India certificate,  
  - **iStart Learning Management System (LMS)** – for startups and learners,  
  - **Rajasthan AVGC‑XR Portal** – to boost the animation, VFX, gaming, comics and XR ecosystem,  
  - **Rajasthan AI Portal** – a central hub for the state’s AI projects, datasets and initiatives.  

- The conference also saw MoUs with partners including **Google, IIT Delhi, National Law University Jodhpur and the Wadhwani Foundation**, aimed at strengthening AI research, skilling and legal/ethical frameworks.  
- Union IT Minister **Ashwini Vaishnaw** announced that **Rajasthan’s first data centre** would be established soon, further positioning the state as an AI and digital‑infrastructure hub.  
- (Official: Government of Rajasthan – Rajasthan AI‑ML Policy 2026 PDF – https://rising.rajasthan.gov.in/storage/app/public/files/pdf/rajasthan-ai-ml-policy-2026.pdf)

### Why Important for Rajasthan Exams?

  - Which state launched the **AI‑ML Policy 2026**? → **Rajasthan**.  
  - In which city/event was it launched? → **Rajasthan Regional AI Impact Conference 2026, Jaipur**.  
  - IndiaAI Mission is under which ministry? → **MeitY**.  
- Analytical angle:

### Topic 4: Jalore Panchayat’s Smartphone Ban – and the U‑Turn

### What Happened?

- In **Jalore district (Rajasthan)**, a **Chaudhary community panchayat** across several villages passed a decision in December 2025 stating that:  
  - **“Daughters‑in‑law and young women”** should not use **smartphones (camera phones)** and may only use **basic keypad phones**.  
  - Women should not carry mobiles to **weddings, social functions or neighbours’ houses**.  
  - School‑going girls could use phones **only at home for studies**.  

- The order was announced on **21 December 2025** at a community event near **Sundha Mata**, with a statement that it would be treated as effective from **26 January 2026**.  
- The panchayat’s stated reason:  
  - rising **mobile‑phone addiction among children**,  
  - children watching videos all day, harming studies and health (especially eyesight).  

- However, after media coverage and criticism that the decision was **regressive and discriminatory towards women**,  
  - In a follow‑up meeting in the **first week of January 2026**, the panchayat **withdrew the smartphone‑ban decision**.  
  - Members later claimed the order was “misunderstood” and that the intention was to address children’s phone habits, not to control women.  

- (According to multiple reputed sources; wording and some details may vary – reports appeared in outlets like Times of India and NDTV.)  

### Why Important for Rajasthan Exams?

- Important example for **Society & Governance**:  
  - For RAS mains, this is a ready case study on **women’s rights, panchayati raj, social customs vs constitutional rights**.  
- You can use it in answers such as:  
  - “Discuss the role and limits of community panchayats. How can their informal decisions conflict with fundamental rights of citizens?”  
- For Police/SI exams, it can appear as a conceptual question on **social control, informal justice systems or gender discrimination**.  

### Memory Trick – “Jalore: quick order, quick rollback”

> One line: **“In Jalore, a Chaudhary panchayat first ‘banned’ smartphones for women, then quickly rolled the order back in January 2026.”**

## 6. Science & Technology

### Topic 9: PARAM Shakti Supercomputer – IIT Madras, Chennai

### What Happened?

- In **January 2026**, the **Ministry of Electronics & IT (MeitY)** inaugurated the **“PARAM Shakti”** supercomputing facility at **IIT Madras, Chennai**.  
- It is based on the **PARAM RUDRA supercomputing system**, with a **peak performance of about 3.1 petaflops** (3.1 × 10^15 calculations per second).  
- The hardware and software stack have been developed in India by **C‑DAC (Centre for Development of Advanced Computing)**.  

- PARAM Shakti will accelerate research in:  
  - aerospace engineering, climate and weather modelling,  
  - materials science, drug discovery, nuclear research,  
  - advanced computational fluid dynamics and more.  

- The facility includes 24×7 power backup, advanced cooling and an integrated data‑centre setup.  

### Why Important for Rajasthan Exams?

- Science & Tech questions often cover **Indian supercomputers, petaflops capacity, and C‑DAC**.  
- Likely MCQs:  
  - “PARAM Shakti is installed at which institute?” → **IIT Madras, Chennai**.  
  - “Its approximate peak performance?” → **around 3.1 petaflops**.  

### Memory Trick – “Shakti at Madras = 3.1 PF”

> Line: **“PARAM Shakti gives IIT Madras a computing power of about 3.1 petaflops.”**

### Topic 10: Justice Manoj Kumar Gupta – Chief Justice, Uttarakhand High Court

### What Happened?

- In January 2026, the **Department of Justice** (Ministry of Law & Justice) notified that:  
  - **Justice Manoj Kumar Gupta**, a senior judge of the **Allahabad High Court**,  
  - has been appointed as the **Chief Justice (CJ) of the Uttarakhand High Court**.  

- The appointment was made by the President under **Article 217(1)** of the Constitution of India.  
- He will assume office after the retirement of the incumbent CJ, **Justice G. Narendra**, on **9 January 2026**.  

- Brief profile:  
  - Enrolled as an advocate on **6 December 1987**,  
  - Elevated as an Additional Judge of the Allahabad High Court on **12 April 2013**,  
  - Became a Permanent Judge in 2015,  
  - Also served for some time as **Acting Chief Justice of Allahabad High Court** in 2023.  

### Why Important for Rajasthan Exams?
