## TOPIC 1: Cold Wave, Alerts and School Holidays Across Rajasthan

### What happened?

- On **13 January 2026**, a strong **cold wave** continued to grip Rajasthan, with **red alert** for **Sikar, Jhunjhunu and Churu** districts and **yellow alert** over several others including parts of Jaipur, Alwar, Bharatpur, Hanumangarh, Sri Ganganagar, Bikaner, Nagaur and Jaisalmer.  
- Many areas reported **dense fog, ground frost and sub‑zero night temperatures**; in some towns like Fatehpur and Palsana, minimum temperatures dipped close to **‑2°C**.

- IMD guidance suggested that cold‑day to severe cold‑day conditions could persist till **15 January**, with gradual improvement thereafter.

## TOPIC 4: Orobanche – Mustard Fields of Rajasthan Under Threat

### What happened?

- The parasitic weed **Orobanche aegyptiaca**, locally known as **Margoja**, has become a major problem in **mustard-growing regions of Rajasthan**, especially in north‑western districts.  
- It is a **root-parasitic flowering plant** that attaches to mustard roots underground and sucks water and nutrients, causing plants to turn weak, yellow and stunted.  
- Seeds are microscopic and can survive in the soil for **many years**, spreading through irrigation water, tools, wind and movement of contaminated soil.  
- The weed often emerges after the **first irrigation**, so farmers realise the problem only when crop stands are already heavily infested.

### Rajasthan exams ke liye kyun important?

- Direct MCQ material: **name (Orobanche aegyptiaca / Margoja)**, **crop (mustard)**, **state (Rajasthan)** and **parasitic nature**.  
- Links to topics on **Rajasthan agriculture, oilseeds, food security and climate‑resilient farming**.  
- Can be used as a case study for **plant-protection and extension challenges** in RAS mains.

**Memory Trick – “Margoja = mustard ka underground chor”**

> Visualise a **small ghost attached to mustard roots underground** – woh hi Margoja jo plant ka saar चुरा रहा है.

## TOPIC 5: Kathputli Puppetry of Jaipur – Preserving a Living Folk Tradition

### What happened?

- **Kathputli ka khel** – Rajasthan’s famous **string puppetry** – remains alive in localities such as **Kathputli Nagar in Jaipur**, where nearly **200–250 puppeteer families** live and perform.  
- These communities belong mainly to traditional **Bhat/Nat performer groups** and travel to fairs, tourist spots and cultural events across the state and country.  
- Performances use wooden string puppets with bright Rajasthani costumes to narrate **stories of kings, folk heroes, social messages and moral tales**.  
- Recent media coverage has again highlighted the need for **better housing, training support and regular shows** so that the art is not lost to migration and other jobs.

### Rajasthan exams ke liye kyun important?

- Kathputli puppetry is a **classical folk art of Rajasthan**, often asked in culture‑based MCQs (state, style, material, community).  
- City link → **Jaipur (Kathputli Nagar)** as an important cluster for traditional puppeteers.  
- Useful in mains answers on **intangible cultural heritage, tourism and livelihoods**.

## TOPIC 6: Crypto KYC Tightened – Why It Matters for Rajasthan Exams

### What happened?

- The Government of India, through **FIU‑India**, has introduced **stricter KYC/AML rules for users of crypto exchanges** operating in India.  
- New norms demand **live selfie verification, geo‑tagging of user location and small bank‑account test transfers**, ensuring each crypto account is tied to a real individual and bank account.  
- The aim is to increase **traceability of virtual digital‑asset transactions** and reduce risks of **money laundering and terror financing**.

### Rajasthan exams ke liye kyun important?

- National‑level schemes and regulations – including those on **crypto, digital payments and KYC** – are regularly asked in Rajasthan exams.  
- Candidates should remember that **FIU‑India (Finance Ministry)**, not RBI/SEBI, drives this specific KYC framework.  
- Links well with questions on **PMLA, FATF and financial‑sector reforms**.
