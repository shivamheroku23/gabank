## TOPIC 1: RBI – Delhi Government MoU for Banking &amp; Public Debt Management

### Kya hua?

- **Reserve Bank of India (RBI)** aur **Government of NCT of Delhi** ne **6 January 2026** ko ek **MoU** sign kiya.  
- MoU ke hisaab se **9 January 2026** se RBI:  
  - Delhi sarkar ka **official banker, debt manager aur financial agent** ban gaya.  
  - Delhi ke **general banking operations** (receipts, payments, treasury) handle karega.  
  - Delhi ke **rupee public debt / State Development Loans (SDLs)** manage karega.  
- Isse pehle Delhi ko infrastructure ke liye zyadatar **12–13% ke high-cost loans** lene padte the;  
  ab RBI ke through **market-based SDLs ~7% ke aas‑paas** pe raise kiye ja sakenge.  
- RBI Delhi ke **surplus cash balances ka automatic daily investment** karega  
  aur **Ways and Means Advances (WMA) + Special Drawing Facility (SDF)** ke through short‑term cash mismatch bhi manage honge.  

*(Official background: RBI–Delhi MoU coverage, RBI Act 1934 ke Section 21A framework ke under.)*

### Bank exam ke liye kyun important?

- **Federal finance + RBI functions** ka classic example:  
  - RBI only **central government + state governments** ka banker hota hai,  
    ab **Delhi (UT with legislature)** bhi RBI framework me aa gaya.  
- **SDL market**:  
  - Delhi ke bonds ab **RBI auction** ke through issue honge → banks, insurance, PF funds ke liye investible paper.  
  - Lower cost borrowing se **fiscal health improve** hogi, default risk kam hoga.  
- Mains / interview:  
  - “Explain the significance of RBI taking over Delhi’s banking and debt management operations for India’s sub‑national fiscal framework.”

### Memory Trick – “Dilli ka Paisa, Ab RBI ke Paas”

> Yaad rakho line: **“9 January se Dilli ka khata RBI ke saath.”**  
> Matlab Delhi ka banking + debt management ab RBI karega, high‑cost loans se hatkar SDL route.

## TOPIC 2: RBI Draft Norms – Dividend Payout Cap 75% of PAT

### Kya hua?

- RBI ne **7 January 2026** ko draft **“Commercial Banks – Prudential Norms on Declaration of Dividend and Remittance of Profits, 2026”** issue ki.  
- Proposal:  
  - Banks ka **maximum dividend payout ratio** (pichhli policy me ~40%)  
    ko ab **75% of Profit After Tax (PAT)** tak allow kiya ja sakta hai,  
    **capital position &amp; risk** ke hisaab se.  
  - **Regional Rural Banks (RRBs) &amp; Local Area Banks (LABs)** ke liye cap **80% of PAT** tak.  
- Framework:  
  - **10 capital buckets** – jisme **Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) ratio** jitna strong,  
    utna zyada payout allowed.  
  - **CET1 &lt; 8%** wale banks **dividend declare nahi kar sakte**.  
  - Board ko dividend recommend karte waqt  
    **asset quality, provisioning gap, future capital needs, growth plan** sab dekhna hoga.  
- RBI ne clearly bola:  
  - Dividend **exceptional / one‑off income**, unrealised fair‑value gains ya sirf provisioning reversal se **pay nahi kiya ja sakta**.

### Bank exam ke liye kyun important?

- **MCQ points:**  
  - New cap: **75% PAT** (commercial banks), **80% PAT** (RRBs/LABs).  
  - CET1 threshold: **8% se kam** par dividend ban.  
- Concept:  
  - Strong capital wale banks shareholders ko **higher payout** de sakte hain,  
    lekin weak capital wale banks ko **retain earnings** karke capital build karna hoga.  
- Mains angle:  
  - “How do prudential dividend norms protect financial stability while balancing investor expectations?”

### Memory Trick – “75–80 Rule”

> Line: **“Normal banks 75, rural banks 80, par CET1 8 se neeche – dividend zero.”**  
> Is ek line se cap + CET1 condition yaad.

## TOPIC 3: Supervisory Data Quality Index – Banks ka Score 90.7

### Kya hua?

- RBI ne **Supervisory Data Quality Index (sDQI)** banaya hai  
  taaki banks ke regulatory data (returns, statements) ki **quality** measure ho sake.  
- Parameters: **Accuracy, Timeliness, Completeness, Consistency**.  
- Latest data (September 2025 quarter):  
  - **Scheduled Commercial Banks** ka aggregate sDQI **90.7** (June 2025 me 89.9).  
  - **Timeliness** component **93.5** tak improve hua – matlab regulatory returns jaldi aur discipline ke saath file ho rahe hain.  
  - Category wise:  
    - **Small Finance Banks ~91.5** (top performers),  
    - **Public Sector Banks ~91.1**,  
    - **Private Banks ~90.6**,  
    - **Foreign Banks ~90.4**.  

### Bank exam ke liye kyun important?

- **Regulatory reporting quality** → direct impact on  
  - RBI ki **risk assessment**, stress testing, supervisory action;  
  - market confidence – “data credible hai ya nahi”.  
- Interview angle:  
  - “Why is data governance and accuracy critical for banks in the age of digital supervision and early‑warning systems?”

### Memory Trick – “DQ = 90+”

> Yaad rakho: **“Data Quality (DQ) 90 ke upar”** –  
> September 2025 me sDQI ~**90.7**, aur timeliness 93.5.

## TOPIC 4: HDFC Bank – Digital Rupee (e₹) SmartGateway Integration

### Kya hua?

- **HDFC Bank** ne **6 January 2026** ko announce kiya ki usne RBI ke **Central Bank Digital Currency – Digital Rupee (e₹)** ko  
  apne **SmartGateway online merchant payment platform** ke saath integrate kar diya hai.  
- Ab SmartGateway use karne wale merchants:  
  - Customers se **e₹ me payments accept** kar sakte hain.  
  - Transactions **zero merchant discount rate (MDR) / zero transaction fees** par ho sakte hain (bank ke announcement ke mutabik).  
- Customers ke liye:  
  - Sovereign‑backed digital rupee ka use,  
  - real‑time settlement, high security, UPI‑type convenience.  

### Bank exam ke liye kyun important?

- **CBDC (Central Bank Digital Currency)** ka ek practical use‑case:  
  - Retail merchants, e‑commerce sites pe real adoption.  
- Banks ke liye:  
  - Payment ecosystem me **intermediary‑free settlement**,  
  - transaction cost kam, settlement risk kam.  
- Mains / interview:  
  - “Differentiate between UPI‑based payments and CBDC‑based payments from the perspective of banks and merchants.”

### Memory Trick – “SmartGateway = Smart e₹”

> Line: **“HDFC SmartGateway, ab smart e₹ gateway.”**  
> Yaani SmartGateway = place jahan se merchants Digital Rupee accept kar sakte hain.

## TOPIC 5: Indian Banking Sector – Fitch &amp; S&amp;P ka Outlook

### Kya hua?

- **Fitch** ki recent analysis (Jan 2026 coverage):  
  - Indian banks **stronger footing** par:  
    - **Gross NPA ratio ~2.2% (H1 FY26)** – 2018 ke **11.2% peak** se bahut niche.  
    - **CET1 ratio ~14.8% (FY26)** – 2014 me ~9.3% tha.  
    - **Return on Assets (RoA) ~1.3%**, Asia‑Pacific peers ke barabar.  
    - **Credit‑to‑GDP ratio ~59% (2025)** – global peers ka average ~101%.  
      → Matlab **headroom for credit growth** jab tak underwriting sensible rahe.  
- **S&amp;P Global** (IBEF ke through) ne highlight kiya:  
  - Q3 2023 me Indian banks ka **market capitalization** badha  
    kyunki festive demand, better business sentiment, tax cuts,  
    aur credit growth strong raha.  

### Bank exam ke liye kyun important?

- **Data points** jo interview &amp; mains me kaam aayenge:  
  - GNPA 11.2% → 2.2%; CET1 9.3% → 14.8%; credit‑to‑GDP ~59%.  
- Narrative:  
  - “India ne ek decade me systemic NPA problem ko resolve karke  
    banking system ko **growth‑ready** bana diya hai।”  

### Memory Trick – “11 se 2, 9 se 15, 59 ka Room”

> Ek line: **“NPA 11 se 2, capital 9 se 15, credit‑GDP 59 – growth ka room।”**  
> Isse teen main numbers yaad ho jate hain।
