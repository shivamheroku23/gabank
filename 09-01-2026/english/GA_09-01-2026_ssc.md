## TOPIC 1: RBI–Delhi Government MoU – RBI as Banker to the Capital

### What happened?

- The **Reserve Bank of India (RBI)** and the **Government of NCT of Delhi** signed a **Memorandum of Understanding (MoU)** on **6 January 2026**.  
- Under this MoU, from **9 January 2026**:  
  - RBI becomes Delhi’s **official banker, debt manager and financial agent**.  
  - All **receipts, payments and treasury operations** of the Delhi government will be routed through RBI.  
  - RBI will manage Delhi’s **State Development Loans (SDLs)**, allowing it to borrow directly from the market.  
- Earlier, Delhi relied heavily on **high‑cost loans at around 12–13%**; now SDLs can be raised at **around 7%**.  
- Delhi will also gain access to **Ways and Means Advances (WMA) and Special Drawing Facility (SDF)** to manage temporary cash‑flow gaps.  

### SSC exam points:

- RBI acts as **banker and debt manager to governments**; Delhi has now joined this framework.  
- Borrowing instrument: **State Development Loans (SDLs)**.  
- Impact: **lower borrowing cost** + improved **fiscal discipline and transparency** for the capital.

**Memory Trick – “Delhi’s account, RBI’s count”**

> Think: **“Delhi’s government account is now counted and settled by RBI.”**

## TOPIC 2: India’s First Hydrogen-Powered Train – Jind–Sonipat Route

### What happened?

- Indian Railways has planned the trial of **India’s first hydrogen-powered train** on the **Jind–Sonipat route in Haryana** (around **90 km**).  
- Reports indicate that trial runs are scheduled for **January 2026**, with **26 January 2026** widely mentioned (subject to final clearances).  
- The train:  
  - Is being developed by **Integral Coach Factory (ICF), Chennai**,  
  - Uses **hydrogen fuel-cell technology**, with **water vapour** as the main exhaust – effectively zero local CO₂ emissions.  
- A dedicated **hydrogen production and refuelling plant at Jind** will produce **green hydrogen** using electrolysis.  

### SSC exam points:

- Route: **Jind–Sonipat (Haryana)** – first hydrogen train pilot section.  
- Technology: **hydrogen fuel-cell**, developed at **ICF, Chennai**, working with **RDSO**.  
- Objective: **clean, low-emission transport** and advancement in **indigenous rail technology**.

**Memory Trick – “Steam without smoke in Haryana”**

> Visualise a train where **only steam, no smoke** comes out –  
> that’s the hydrogen train on **Jind–Sonipat**.

## TOPIC 3: Prime Minister Research Chair (PMRC) – 120 Global Indian Scientists for IITs

### What happened?

- The **IIT Council** has discussed a new scheme called the **Prime Minister Research Chair (PMRC)**.  
- Plan: over **five years**, create about **120 high-end research positions** in IITs for **top Indian-origin scientists and researchers** working abroad.  
- The scheme will have three main categories:  
  - **Young Research Fellows**,  
  - **Senior Research Fellows**,  
  - **Research Chairs**.  
- PMRC will focus on around **14 national priority sectors**, such as **semiconductors, artificial intelligence, clean energy, advanced materials** etc.  

### SSC exam points:

- Scheme: **Prime Minister Research Chair (PMRC)**.  
- Target: **~120 Indian-origin scientists** in 5 years.  
- Implementing institutions: **IITs**.  
- Purpose: **reverse brain drain**, strengthen **research &amp; innovation** in key technology sectors.

**Memory Trick – “120 chairs for brain-gain”**

> Line: **“PMRC = 120 research chairs to turn brain drain into brain gain.”**

## TOPIC 4: Science &amp; Nuclear Safety – A.K. Balasubrahmanian and DRDO Foundation Day

### What happened?

- **Atomic Energy Regulatory Board (AERB):**  
  - **A. K. Balasubrahmanian** assumed charge as **Chairman, AERB** on **1 January 2026**.  
  - Tenure: **three years**, approved by the **Appointments Committee of the Cabinet**.  
  - AERB regulates **nuclear and radiation safety** in nuclear power plants and related facilities.  
- **Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO):**  
  - Celebrated its **68th Foundation Day** on **1 January 2026** at its HQ in **New Delhi**.  
  - DRDO was established on **1 January 1958**.  
  - Recent highlights: **22 Acceptance of Necessity (AoN) approvals (~₹1.3 lakh crore)**, **11 defence contracts (~₹26,000 crore)**, and focus on **cyber, space and AI** domains.  

### SSC exam points:

- AERB: **Atomic Energy Regulatory Board** – new Chairman: **A. K. Balasubrahmanian**.  
- DRDO: **Defence Research and Development Organisation** – established **1958**, HQ **New Delhi**, Foundation Day **1 January**.

## TOPIC 5: e-B-4 Electronic Business Visa – For Chinese Nationals

### What happened?

- India has launched a new **e-B-4 Production Investment Business Visa** from **1 January 2026**.  
- Target group: **Chinese nationals** visiting India for  
  **manufacturing, equipment installation, commissioning, quality checks, maintenance, IT/ERP implementation, training and supply-chain work**.  
- Key features:  
  - Maximum stay: **up to 6 months** in India.  
  - **Fully online application** via the Indian e-visa portal and the **National Single Window System (NSWS)**, where Indian companies can sponsor.  
  - Processing time: around **45–50 days**.  
  - Biometric capture on arrival and **electronic FRRO registration** within 14 days.  

### SSC exam points:

- Visa type: **e-B-4 Production Investment Business Visa**.  
- Focus country: **China**.  
- Maximum stay: **6 months**.  
- Aim: facilitate **technical/business experts** for projects in electronics, renewable energy, manufacturing, etc.

**Memory Trick – “B-4 = Business For China”**

> Think: **“e-B-4 means Business For Chinese experts in India.”**
