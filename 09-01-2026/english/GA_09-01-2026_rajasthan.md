### TOPIC 1: Rajasthan AI-ML Policy 2026 – Responsible AI in governance

### What Happened?

- The Government of Rajasthan has launched the **Rajasthan Artificial Intelligence–Machine Learning (AI-ML) Policy 2026**, notified by the **Department of Information Technology &amp; Communication (DoIT&amp;C)** on **7 January 2026**.  
- Chief Minister **Bhajanlal Sharma** released the policy along with the **National AI Literacy Programme**, aimed at spreading basic and advanced AI awareness among students, government staff and youth across the state.  
- The policy focuses on **ethical, transparent and citizen-centric use of AI and ML** in government functioning.  
- Priority sectors include **public service delivery, urban governance, health, education, agriculture, policing and grievance redressal**.  
- It proposes the creation of an **AI Centre of Excellence**, shared computing and data infrastructure and frameworks for **data protection and cyber security**.  
- Departments are expected to identify **AI use-cases**, appoint **AI nodal officers** and run pilot projects before scaling up.  
- (Official source: DoIT&amp;C Rajasthan – “RAJASTHAN AI/ML POLICY 2026”, order dated 07/01/2026 – https://doitc.rajasthan.gov.in/writereaddata/PoliciesGuidelinesOrders/202601081149516478189AIMLpolicy2026.pdf)  

### Why is this important for Rajasthan exams?

- State policies like the **Rajasthan AI-ML Policy 2026** are favourite one-liner questions in **RAS prelims** and descriptive topics in mains.  
- It shows how Rajasthan is aligning with **Digital India and IndiaAI** while building its own **innovation and employment ecosystem**.  
- Names and years to remember: **Bhajanlal Sharma** (Chief Minister), **DoIT&amp;C** as nodal department, policy year **2026**.  
- Can be quoted in essays on **e-governance, start-ups, skilling, cyber security and new-age jobs in Rajasthan**.  
- For police/administrative exams, it links technology with **better service delivery and predictive governance**.  

### Memory Trick – “AI for Smart Rajasthan 2026”

> Remember: **“AI-ML Policy 2026 = AI for Smart Rajasthan 2026”** – AI for faster services, safer data and more skilled youth.

### TOPIC 2: Divisional-level pre-budget consultations in Rajasthan

### What Happened?

- The **Finance Department, Government of Rajasthan** has posted a notice titled **“Prior budget stakeholders consultations at Divisional level”** dated **7 January 2026**.  
- The circular asks **Divisional Commissioners** to organise meetings with key stakeholders **before the presentation of the State Budget**, so that field-level issues are reflected in budget proposals.  
- Stakeholder groups typically include representatives from **industry and trade bodies, agriculture and farmers’ organisations, small businesses, social-sector organisations and experts**.  
- Suggestions are to be compiled and sent to the Finance Department for consideration while finalising **tax proposals, schemes and departmental allocations**.  
- This continues Rajasthan’s recent practice of making the **budget-making process more participatory and evidence-based**.  
- (Official source: Finance Department, Govt of Rajasthan – “Prior budget stakeholders consultations at Divisional level”, What’s New entry dated 07/01/2026 – https://finance.rajasthan.gov.in)  

### Why is this important for Rajasthan exams?

- **Budget process and institutions** are core topics in **RAS, Junior Accountant, Accounts Assistant and Patwari** exams.  
- One-liner questions can ask: consultations are being held at which administrative level – answer: **Divisional level**.  
- It shows practical use of **decentralisation** – inputs from divisional headquarters are aggregated into the State Budget.  
- Good example for mains answers on **participatory budgeting, cooperative federalism and transparency in public finance**.  

### Memory Trick – “Budget ideas from every division”

> Link the phrase: **“Before the Budget, Rajasthan listens at the Division.”** – divisional-level meetings feed into the State Budget.

### TOPIC 4: Pravasi Bharatiya Diwas 2026 – diaspora as “Rashtradoots”

### What Happened?

- **Pravasi Bharatiya Diwas (PBD)** is observed every year on **9 January** to honour the contributions of Indians abroad.  
- The date marks **9 January 1915**, when **Mahatma Gandhi returned to India from South Africa**, a turning point in the freedom struggle.  
- On **9 January 2026**, Prime Minister **Narendra Modi** greeted the Indian diaspora, calling them a **“powerful bridge between India and the world”** and referring to them as **“Rashtradoots” (ambassadors of the nation)**.  
- He highlighted their role in popularising **Indian culture, yoga, cuisine and democratic values** across the world.  
- The PBD convention, held every two years, serves as a platform for **diaspora engagement, investment and knowledge-sharing**.  
- (Official source: PIB – PM’s message on Pravasi Bharatiya Diwas 2026 – https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleseDetail.aspx?PRID=2212750&amp;reg=6&amp;lang=1)  

### Why is this important for Rajasthan exams?

- Days and their **dates plus background** are frequent one-liner questions in **RAS, REET, Police and RSMSSB exams**.  
- Rajasthan has a large diaspora in **Gulf countries, Africa and Europe**; PBD is a natural link topic for essays on **migration, remittances and soft power**.  
- Knowing the difference between **Pravasi Bharatiya Diwas (9 January)** and **Hindi Diwas (14 September)** helps avoid trick options in MCQs.  

### Memory Trick – “9 January: Gandhi returns, diaspora honoured”

> Line to remember: **“9 January – Gandhi came home, so we honour Indians abroad.”**

### TOPIC 5: Third International Conference on Indian Languages – New Delhi

### What Happened?

- On **9 January 2026**, Vice-President **C. P. Radhakrishnan** inaugurated the **Third International Conference on Indian Languages** in **New Delhi**.  
- The event took place at the **Indira Gandhi National Centre for the Arts (IGNCA)** and involved organisations such as **Vaishvik Hindi Parivaar, Antarashtriya Sahyog Parishad and the Department of Bharatiya Bhasha**.  
- In his address, the Vice-President said that **India’s linguistic diversity strengthens national unity and civilisational ethos**, rather than dividing the country.  
- He emphasised the need to use **digital archives, AI-based translation tools and multilingual platforms** to preserve and promote Indian languages.  
- The conference discussed the role of Indian languages in **education, knowledge systems, culture and technology**.  
- (Official sources: PIB – press release on the Third International Bharatiya Languages Conference 2026 – https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleseDetail.aspx?PRID=2213089&amp;reg=3&amp;lang=1; Vice-President Secretariat.)

- The event links culture with **technology (AI tools, digital archives)** – useful for mains answers on **language policy, cultural heritage and Digital India**.
