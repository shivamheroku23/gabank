## TOPIC 1: December 2025 WPI – 0.83% Wholesale Inflation

### What happened?

- The Government announced that **India’s Wholesale Price Index (WPI) inflation for December 2025** stood at **0.83% year‑on‑year**.  
- In October and November 2025, WPI was in negative territory (deflation), so the shift back to **positive territory in December** is significant.  
- The rise was driven mainly by higher prices of **manufactured products, food products and textiles**.  
- The WPI Food Index saw an annual change of around **0%**, while **CPI retail inflation** for December 2025 was around **1.3%**.  

(Official source: DPIIT – WPI Press Release; Photo: https://www.insightsonindia.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/wpi_index.png)

### SSC exam points:

- December 2025 WPI inflation = **0.83% (positive)**.  
- WPI → measures **wholesale/producer level prices**; CPI → **retail consumer prices**.  
- Data released by → **Ministry of Commerce &amp; Industry (DPIIT)**.  
- Inflation is a key topic under **Indian Economy** and often appears in SSC GA questions.

**Memory Trick – “0.83 – WPI is free from minus”**

> Think: **“0 point 8‑3 – WPI is free from minus”** → it moved from deflation to positive.

## TOPIC 3: RBI Discussion Paper – New Licences for Urban Co‑operative Banks

### What happened?

- On **13 January 2026**, the **Reserve Bank of India (RBI)** issued a **Discussion Paper** proposing to restart **licensing of Urban Co‑operative Banks (UCBs)**.  
- The proposal focuses on granting licences mainly to **large multi‑state co‑operative credit societies** already operating at scale.  
- Indicative key eligibility norms:  
  - Minimum **paid‑up capital of about ₹300 crore**.  
  - At least **10 years of operational track record**.  
  - **CRAR around 12%** and **Net NPA below around 3%**.  
- RBI has invited comments from stakeholders up to **mid‑February 2026**, after which final guidelines may follow.  

(Official source: RBI – Discussion Paper on Licensing of UCBs; Photo: https://www.rbi.org.in/scripts/BS_PressRelease_UCB2026.jpg)

### SSC exam points:

- Discussion Paper topic → **Licensing framework for Urban Co‑operative Banks**.  
- Regulator → **RBI**.  
- One key figure → minimum **paid‑up capital ≈ ₹300 crore**.  
- Links to SSC syllabus under **Indian Economy, Banking System, and Financial Inclusion**.

**Memory Trick – “Rule of 3 – ₹300 capital, 3% NPA”**

> Remember: **“Urban co‑op bank = 300 crore + NPA below 3%”** – enough to crack an MCQ.

## TOPIC 4: Tripura Gramin Bank – India’s First Solar‑Powered ATM Van

### What happened?

- **Tripura Gramin Bank (TGB)** has launched **India’s first fully solar‑powered ATM van**, named **“TGB on Wheels”**.  
- The van travels across rural and remote areas, providing **cash withdrawals, balance enquiries and mini‑statements** to people.  
- It runs entirely on **solar panels and battery systems** mounted on the roof, reducing dependence on diesel generators or grid power.  
- The innovation has received support from **NABARD** and was recognised with a **SKOCH Silver Award**.  

(Official source: Tripura Gramin Bank / NABARD release; Photo: https://currentaffairs.adda247.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/tripura_gramin_bank_solar_atm_van.jpg)

### SSC exam points:

- Bank → **Tripura Gramin Bank (Regional Rural Bank)**.  
- Claim → **India’s first fully solar‑powered ATM van**.  
- Support agency → **NABARD**.  
- Relevant for SSC under **banking, environment (green energy) and rural development**.

**Memory Trick – “Tripura’s sun‑run ATM”**

> Imagine: **a small van with solar panels and ‘TGB on Wheels’ written on it** – a moving ATM powered by the sun.

## TOPIC 5: National Youth Day 2026 – Swami Vivekananda’s Birth Anniversary

### What happened?

- Every year, **12 January** is celebrated as **National Youth Day** in India – it marks the birth anniversary of **Swami Vivekananda**.  
- In 2026, schools and colleges across India organised speeches, quizzes, youth runs and volunteer programmes.  
- The message for the year drew inspiration from Swami Vivekananda’s famous call – **“Arise, Awake, and Stop Not”**, highlighting youth empowerment and perseverance.  
- The day aims to inspire youth towards **character building, discipline, nation‑building and community service**.
